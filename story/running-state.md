## Chapter Summaries
- **Ch. 1 "Threshold":** Ada arrives at an empty trailhead at dusk, late autumn. She deliberately leaves her phone in the car, eats dried Psilocybe cyanescens on a nurse log, and encounters a large Western toad that watches her with ancient stillness before vanishing. As psilocybin onset begins, moss breathes, colors saturate past normal, and she discovers bioluminescent Omphalotus fungi. A compulsion to walk pulls her deeper into the forest. The clinical voice in her head narrates with decreasing authority. When she turns back, the forest behind her has rearranged -- different trees, different undergrowth, the trail gone. Her hands close into fists involuntarily. The chapter closes with Ada standing in a rearranged forest, the first crack in her armor, the voice's last explanation dissolving "like wet paper."
- **Ch. 2 "Swarm":** Ada tries to grid-walk back to the clearing, counting paces, calculating moon azimuth -- every analytical strategy fails. She finds her paper bag impossibly relocated to a Sitka spruce far from the nurse log. The undergrowth thickens as she transitions into Zone 2 (The Tangle), where Mycena chlorophos replaces Omphalotus. A brief moment of beauty: Mycena caps pulsing in a cascade along a branch, silencing her mind. Then the swarm arrives -- moths, mosquitoes, beetles assembling into a unified presence whose drone develops almost-word fragments in Ada's own anxious cadence. She runs, crashes through devil's club (spines embed in left forearm and right hand), falls twice, and the swarm tightens with every effort. When exhaustion empties her completely, her hands open involuntarily, and the swarm thins and disperses. The clinical voice tries to reassemble but sounds like "a hand waving." Ada kneels in mud, holding the body-knowledge that fighting feeds it without analyzing it. She stands, picks a direction, and walks into the Tangle.
- **Ch. 3 "Teeth":** Ada pushes through the Tangle on stubbornness alone, the clinical voice effectively silent. Rain begins -- cold drizzle through the canopy. She enters a small clearing where the forest goes completely silent. Her childhood scanning instinct activates: a hallway, bare feet, listening for footsteps. The wolf materializes from shadow -- enormous, flickering between solid and shadow, amber eyes fixed on her. It circles with tightening arcs. Ada deploys every survival tactic she knows (eye contact, breaking eye contact, making herself large, standing ground, edging sideways, noise); each is absorbed by the wolf's patience. The wolf speaks: "You've been running your whole life." "What are you bracing for?" The circling closes to four feet. Ada's legs give from depletion. She kneels in mud. The wolf approaches, breathes on the back of her neck. Says: "I'm tired too." Lowers its head in acknowledgment. Turns and walks into the Tangle, absorbed by darkness. In the aftermath, Ada's hypervigilance hum drops a fraction for the first time. The frog appears on a stone, croaks once, leads Ada through a navigable gap in the Tangle. Says "This way." Guides her to the ravine's edge -- first open sky, moonlight on a fast stream below, the faintest vibration of something beneath the water's sound. The frog vanishes. Ada stands at the threshold between Zone 2 and Zone 3, alone, the crossing ahead.
- **Ch. 4 "Crossing":** Valley chapter. Ada descends the ravine wall (fifteen to twenty feet, steep, mossy) and crosses the stream -- eight to ten feet wide, fast current, snowmelt-cold. The cold cuts through the psilocybin haze and adrenaline fog, returning her to her body. She falls, goes fully under, surfaces gasping. Crawls onto the far bank and collapses face-down in the moss -- a sustained moment of pure physical presence ("she was only a body"). A distant frog call from across the ravine eases something fractionally. She processes: drinks water for the first time since Ch. 1 (bottle goes to one-third), retrieves and puts on the rain jacket, examines her devil's club scratches (worsened by water). The clinical voice stirs but cannot complete its sentence. Ada enters Zone 3: undergrowth thins, clearings open, shelf fungi glow with heartbeat rhythm, ghost pipe signals dense mycelium, Amanita muscaria marks boundaries. She feels faint warmth and vibration beside the ghost pipes -- the mycelium hum, growing. Her composure reassembles automatically: spine straightens, chin lifts, "You can handle this." She does not recognize it as armor. She recognizes it as feeling more like herself. The chapter closes with Ada walking into the Hollow's arenas, armored and unknowing: "the patience had antlers."
- **Ch. 5 "Antlers":** The clinical voice tentatively re-engages as Ada walks through Zone 3, cataloging observations. She enters a moonlit clearing -- canopy gap directly above, waxing gibbous flooding it with silver light, fly agarics in a broken ring, theatrical stage quality. A Roosevelt elk stands on a ridge at the far end, utterly still, antlers fractalizing into a branching lattice under psilocybin. The stag's composure is effortless; Ada tries to match it, breathing its rhythm, holding her posture, and fails because hers costs everything. The stag speaks three lines: "Is this who you are? Or who you've practiced being?" "How long have you been holding that pose?" "You can put it down. You won't. But you can." A kitchen-table memory surfaces involuntarily (someone asking "Are you okay?" and Ada's automatic "Of course" smile). Ada says "I'm fine" aloud; the stag's silence is devastating. Her body fails before her mind decides to stop: legs shake, she stumbles, makes an involuntary compressed sound. The stag turns, walks toward the tree line, shedding antler tines piece by piece -- dry, structural sounds -- until the beams are bare stumps. It lowers its unburdened head and walks into the forest. Ada picks up an antler fragment (cold, hard, hollow), holds it, sets it down. She walks on. The composure reassembles but fits differently: looser, joints no longer flush, a hairline fracture. "And through the crack, the dark water beneath."
