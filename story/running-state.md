# Running State

## Last Chapter Completed: Chapter 26 — "Five Answers"

## Characters Active — Five Reactions to the Ultimatum

### Vale (fury → grim recognition)
Confronted Noll in the Speaking Chambers. Called it imprisonment. Noll: "You may be correct. It is also the reason we have survived." Vale asked about refusing both options. Noll: "There are only two options." Vale saw the dictator he always suspected. He also cannot entirely argue with the logic. Hart stood in the doorway as silent witness.

### Marsh (quiet grief)
Sat with Oona at the nursery pools. Cried. Oona was distressed by human tears ("You are leaking"). Oona's grandmother joke: "Good. I was running out of things to ferment." Marsh laughed. It helped more than it should. A young caretaker stared openly with flickering chromatophores. An older caretaker did not look up.

### Reed (guilty vertigo)
Found Krrik in the lab. Horror tangled with guilty scientific thrill ("I could spend the rest of my life here"). Krrik mentioned the Tupi trader (22 years, content near the end). Junior engineer: "The archive records say the human sang in the fermentation cellars. Badly. Everyone liked it." Reed went to work at the array because the mechanism was easier to hold than the thing it carried.

### Hart (pragmatic recognition)
Sat with Talla at the settlement's edge. Calm that surprised her. Talla: "Departure is not a category the Threnn understand." Hart realized she stopped checking exits weeks ago. She does not know when. Talla: "Now, so do you."

### Quill (spiritual ambiguity)
Found Vesh in his quarters. Did NOT ask about the ultimatum. Asked about full merging: "What would it actually feel like?" Vesh: "I do not know. Nobody knows. That is why we keep trying." The sleeping elderly frog stirred and murmured. Quill does not know if he has been condemned or invited. He suspects the distinction has never been clear.

### Shared silence
All five in their quarters. Nobody spoke. The settlement hummed. The civilization continued.
