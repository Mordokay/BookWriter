# Project Goals

## Scope

- Format: novel (shorter)
- Target word count: ~85,000-90,000 words
- Target chapter count: 27 (22 plot + 4 character-development/world-exploration + 1 religion/mourning ritual)
- Chapter style: short, fast, dialogue-driven

## Creative Objectives

1. **Build a fully realized frog civilization** that feels genuinely alien — not humans in frog costumes. Their biology shapes their philosophy, their short lifespans shape their institutions, their technology coexists with primitive habits.

2. **Create an ensemble-driven dialogue novel** where five human biologists and six core frogs interrogate frog civilization from every angle: politics, daily life, science, philosophy, ecology, history.

3. **Sustain absurd comedy with philosophical depth.** The humor emerges from earnestness and inherent absurdity, never from forced whimsy. The ideas are deep but arrive through friction, not lecture.

4. **Deliver detective-novel pacing.** Each chapter opens a new door. The reader should feel pulled forward greedily. Minimal description, maximal dialogue and interaction.

5. **Land an ending that is ambiguous, hauntingly peaceful, and morally unresolved.** The frogs absorb the biologists into their civilization with calm, civilized coercion. Each biologist reacts differently. The reader finishes the book unsure whether they witnessed imprisonment or transcendence.

6. **Maintain the central unresolved question throughout:** Are the frogs wise, or have they simply become very sophisticated at surviving their own absurdity?

## What Success Looks Like

The reader finishes the book feeling entertained, intellectually stimulated, emotionally unsettled, and still thinking about the frogs days later — not just as a funny concept, but as a real society with a real soul.
