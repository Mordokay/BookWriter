# Project Goals

## Primary goal

Create a coherent and compelling book-length narrative through a multi-agent workflow.

## Proof of concept goal

Produce a readable and satisfying book of approximately 80 pages.

## Quality goals

- emotionally engaging
- internally consistent
- distinct character voices
- structured escalation
- satisfying ending

## Practical goals

- keep canon in markdown
- keep the user in the loop
- make iteration easy
- minimize chaos

## Default target

- 12 to 14 chapters
- approximately 80 pages equivalent
- one contained story arc
- single protagonist (Ada), no secondary human characters
- the forest and its animals as the only "cast"

## Reading Experience

The reader should feel like they have swallowed something and it is changing them from the inside. The first pages disorient. The middle pages frighten and then fascinate. The final pages open into quiet, earned peace. The book should feel like a fever dream that turns out to be medicine.

Specifically:
- The reader should feel physically present in the forest: damp, dark, close, alive.
- The reader should feel Ada's fear as visceral and specific, not generic dread.
- Each animal encounter should feel like a mirror the reader did not ask to look into.
- The ending should produce a bodily sensation: an exhale, a loosening, a feeling of being held by something vast.
- After finishing, the reader should sit quietly for a moment before returning to their life.

## Emotional Promises

1. **We will not waste your discomfort.** Every frightening or painful moment in the story pays off. Nothing is gratuitous.
2. **We will not lecture you.** The spiritual and psychological dimensions are experienced, not explained.
3. **We will earn the peace.** The ending is not premature or sentimental. It passes through genuine darkness first.
4. **We will trust you.** The symbolism is present but not decoded. The reader is treated as an intelligent participant.
5. **We will make you feel something real.** The story prioritizes emotional honesty over spectacle, cleverness, or genre convention.

## What Makes This Project Interesting

- **The antagonist is the protagonist.** Every threat Ada faces is a part of herself she has refused to integrate. This is not a metaphor laid on top of an adventure plot; it is the actual structure of the story.
- **The setting is also a character and also a mirror.** The forest is simultaneously a real place, a psychedelic hallucination, a spirit realm, and a map of Ada's psyche. The story never resolves which of these is "true."
- **The genre sits in a gap.** This is not literary fiction, not standard fantasy, not horror, not spiritual self-help. It occupies the space where psychological realism meets mythic symbolism, and it stays there without apologizing.
- **The resolution is integration, not victory.** Ada does not defeat anything. She accepts. This is structurally unusual and emotionally distinctive.
- **The story is sensory and embodied.** It lives in mud, spores, moonlight, breath, and heartbeat. It is a book you feel in your body, not just your mind.
- **The frog.** A wise spirit animal that teaches through presence, gesture, and demonstration. The frog is funny, strange, patient, and sacred all at once — a counselor and guide who leads Ada toward her own revelations without words.
