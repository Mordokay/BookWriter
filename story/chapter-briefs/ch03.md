# Chapter 3 Brief -- "Teeth"

## Objective

Dramatize the wolf encounter -- Ada's chronic fear and hypervigilance confronted by their mirror image. Demonstrate that yielding (not fighting) releases the wolf. Introduce the frog as active guide (2nd appearance) who leads Ada from the Tangle to the ravine threshold. End with Ada at the edge of Zone 3.

## POV

Close third person, locked to Ada. Past tense. During the wolf encounter, prose rhythm shifts to short, clipped, hard-consonant sentences -- fear rhythm per style-guide.md. After the wolf departs and the frog appears, the rhythm opens slightly -- not calm, but the tight wiring of fear loosening into exhaustion.

## Setting

- **Zone:** Zone 2 (The Tangle)
- **Specific locations:** Dense Tangle undergrowth, a small clearing where the wolf encounter occurs, then the navigable path the frog reveals, ending at the ravine's edge.
- **Time of night:** Approximately 7:00-8:00 PM. Full dark.
- **Light conditions:** Near-total darkness. Canopy nearly closed. Moonlight in rare, narrow shafts. Bioluminescence (Mycena chlorophos, foxfire) is the primary light. The wolf's eyes reflect this light -- pale amber glow.
- **Weather:** Light intermittent rain begins during this chapter. Cold drizzle through canopy gaps. Ada should feel it on her face and hands.
- **Temperature:** Upper 30s F, dropping. The rain makes the cold sharper.
- **Key environmental features:** Dense undergrowth -- red huckleberry (Vaccinium parvifolium), salal, devil's club. Deadfall barriers. A small clearing where the Tangle briefly opens -- this is where the wolf waits. Then: the frog reveals a navigable gap in the undergrowth leading to the ravine.

## Characters Present

- **Ada:** Scratched from Ch. 2 (devil's club spines in forearms), shaken, running on survival instinct. The analytical voice is badly cracked. She is in fight-or-flight.
- **The Wolf:** Gray wolf (Canis lupus). Far larger than any wolf Ada has seen in photographs. Under psilocybin, it flickers between solid flesh and shadow. Legs too long, shoulders too high. Eyes pale yellow, fixed with personal intelligence. Its growl vibrates in Ada's ribcage (subsonic). It circles with absolute economy -- no wasted motion. It does not attack. It mirrors. It tests.
- **The Frog (2nd appearance -- The Guide):** After the wolf departs. Sitting on a stone several yards ahead, visible in bioluminescent light. It hops forward. Pauses. Hops. Ada follows without deciding to. The frog leads her through a navigable path in the Tangle to the ravine. At the ravine's edge, it vanishes. First active guidance -- the teaching: guidance exists if you follow it, but you must cross your own thresholds.
- **Forest mood:** Sudden silence before the wolf -- every insect and bird goes quiet. The forest compresses. During the encounter, the trees lean inward (fear response from world.md). After the wolf departs, the forest loosens fractionally.

## Scene Sequence

1. **Opening -- Pushing through the Tangle.** Ada is moving. Not with purpose -- she picked a direction at the end of Ch. 2 and is committing to it through sheer stubbornness. The undergrowth is hostile: salal thickets, deadfall, devil's club. The rain begins -- cold drizzle on her face. She is running on adrenaline residue and the refusal to stop. Her body is in survival mode. The clinical interior voice is reduced to fragments: "Keep moving. Watch for the trail. Keep --"

2. **The silence falls.** Everything goes quiet. No insects. No dripping. No wind. The sudden absence of sound is worse than any sound. Ada stops. Her body knows what silence means before her mind does. She scans the undergrowth -- old habit, the survival scanning she has done since childhood.

3. **The wolf appears.** It materializes from shadow at the edge of a small clearing. Under bioluminescent light, its eyes glow amber. It is enormous. Its body flickers at the edges -- solid flesh becoming shadow becoming flesh. Its growl is subsonic: Ada feels it in her sternum before she hears it. The wolf does not charge. It circles. Slowly. Testing. Ada backs against a tree.

4. **Ada's survival responses fail.** She tries everything her fear-trained body knows: makes herself large, backs away, holds eye contact, breaks eye contact, stands still, edges sideways. The wolf adjusts to each response. Nothing works. It closes the circle, step by step. The encounter stretches -- what might be minutes feels like an eternity. Ada's adrenaline is flooding, but there is nowhere for it to go. She cannot fight a wolf. She cannot outrun a wolf. The knowledge is physical: she is prey, and the predator is patient.

5. **The collapse.** Ada cannot sustain the terror. Her legs give. She sinks to her knees in the mud. Not a choice -- depletion. Her hands are in the wet earth. She is looking at the ground, at the ferns, at the moss. The wolf approaches. She can hear its breath. She can feel the heat of it. It is so close she could touch it. For one terrible, endless moment, it stands over her. She feels its breath on the back of her neck. Then: the wolf lowers its head. Not submission -- something else. Acknowledgment. It turns and walks into the undergrowth. It does not look back.

6. **Aftermath -- The body's lesson.** Ada stays on her knees. She is shaking. Mud on her hands, her knees. The rain is cold on her neck. But something has shifted -- not in her mind (her mind is blank) but in her body. The chronic hum of hypervigilance has dropped a notch. The fear that held the wolf there -- she felt it. She does not understand it yet. She just feels the exhaustion of having been afraid her whole life, concentrated into one encounter. Backstory fragment: a flash of the childhood hallway, listening for footsteps, always scanning. The wolf was the thing that made that scanning necessary. And it left when she stopped.

7. **The frog appears.** A single clear croak cuts through the aftermath. Ada looks up. The frog is sitting on a stone several yards ahead, at the edge of a gap in the undergrowth. Visible in bioluminescent light -- mottled olive-brown and gold, warty, heavy. It hops forward. Ada, without deciding, follows. She is on her feet, moving, following a toad through the forest. The frog leads her through a navigable path -- not a trail, but a gap, a seam in the Tangle that Ada would never have found on her own.

8. **Closing -- The ravine's edge.** The frog leads Ada to the edge of a ravine. Below: the sound of running water. The frog stops. Ada looks down -- the ravine is steep, the walls mossy and slick, and at the bottom a stream flashes in rare moonlight. When she looks back, the frog is gone. She is at the threshold between Zone 2 and Zone 3. She must cross alone.

## Emotional Progression

- **Opening (pushing through the Tangle):** Grim determination layered over residual fear from the swarm. The specific stubbornness of someone who does not know what else to do but keep moving. The body is in charge now -- the mind's strategies have failed.
- **The silence:** Primal dread. The specific fear of a prey animal sensing a predator. The body knows before the mind. Ada's childhood survival instinct -- the scanning, the reading of rooms -- is fully activated.
- **The wolf encounter:** Terror that is focused, not diffuse (contrast with the swarm's chaos). The wolf's circling creates a slow compression. Ada feels seen -- not by the wolf's intelligence but by her own fear looking back at her. The wolf IS her fear, and seeing it from the outside is both horrible and clarifying.
- **The collapse:** Not surrender but depletion. The body giving out. And in the giving out, a faint recognition: the moment she stopped fighting, the encounter changed. The wolf lowered its head. Something in this matters, even though she cannot name it.
- **The frog appearing:** Relief that does not feel like relief yet. Something simpler -- the presence of another living thing that is not trying to kill her. Ada follows the frog on instinct, the way a child follows a parent through a crowd. Not trust exactly. Need.
- **The ravine's edge:** A wary alertness replaces the terror. She is at a threshold. The frog has brought her here and then left. She must cross alone. The feeling is: something is on the other side, and she does not know what it is, but going back is not an option.

- **Time/light:** Full dark. Rain began. Moon mostly hidden by dense canopy and clouds.
- **Frog arc:** 2nd appearance. Active guidance. Leads Ada through the Tangle to the ravine. Vanishes at the threshold. Teaching: guidance exists if you follow it, but you must cross your own thresholds.
- **Wolf lesson:** Stop running, stop fighting = the wolf releases. Ada's body learns this. Her mind has not caught up.
- **Mycelium hum:** Barely perceptible -- the faintest vibration underfoot as Ada nears the ravine (approaching Zone 3 territory). She does not notice it consciously.
- **Sound continuity:** The forest went silent before the wolf. After the wolf departs, sounds return -- but subtly different. The insect chorus is thinner. The varied thrush does NOT call (it calls at dusk and dawn, not midnight).
- **Light rain begins in this chapter and continues through Ch. 4.**

## Chapter Opening

Do NOT open the same way as Ch. 2 (which opened with Ada's interior voice / disorientation). Ch. 3 should open with physical action or environmental detail -- Ada moving through the Tangle, body-first. The reader should feel the undergrowth pressing, the rain arriving, the body working before the mind can comment.

Possible approach: open with the sensation of pushing through dense vegetation. The physical texture of the Tangle -- branches catching her jacket, fern fronds wet against her jeans, the sound of her own passage too loud in the near-silence.

## Desired Chapter Ending Energy

Threshold energy. The chapter should end with Ada at the ravine's edge, looking down at running water, the frog gone. The energy is not resolution -- it is a breath between terrors. The wolf is behind her. Something else is ahead. The ravine is a physical and symbolic boundary, and the final image should hold the weight of that: water running in darkness, the sound loud enough to drown thought, and Ada standing on the edge of a descent she has not chosen but cannot avoid.

## Estimated Word Count

4,000-4,500 words. The wolf encounter is a major set piece and needs enough space to build the circling, sustain the tension, and render the collapse. But the aftermath should not linger -- the frog's guidance carries momentum into the chapter's close.

## Risks and Pitfalls

- **Pacing trap -- the wolf circling too long.** The circling creates tension through repetition, but too much repetition becomes monotonous. The encounter needs internal variation: Ada trying different responses, the wolf adjusting, the distance closing incrementally. Each circuit should be different.
- **Tone risk -- gratuitous horror.** Per constraints.md: the story must never become horror. The wolf encounter is terrifying but its purpose is psychological revelation, not shock. The wolf is not malicious. Its gaze is intelligent, almost weary. It is tired of circling too.
- **Character risk -- Ada too brave or too passive.** She should try things. She is resourceful. But her resourcefulness fails here -- that is the point. She should not freeze immediately (she has too much survival training for that) and she should not calmly stand her ground (she is not that brave yet). She fights, and the fighting does not work, and the exhaustion of fighting brings the collapse.
- **Frog risk -- appearing too conveniently.** The frog's arrival after the wolf should feel like grace, not mechanics. Let a beat of silence and emptiness pass between the wolf's departure and the frog's croak. Ada needs to sit in the aftermath before guidance arrives. If the frog appears too quickly, it undermines the cost of the wolf encounter.
- **Repetition risk -- another "Ada is terrified" chapter.** Ch. 2 was also fear-driven. The fear must feel different here: the swarm's fear was diffuse and mental; the wolf's fear is focused and physical. The swarm overwhelmed; the wolf pins. Different mechanisms of terror. The prose must mark this distinction.
- **Backstory risk -- too much flashback.** The wolf encounter should activate brief, fragmentary sensory memories of Ada's childhood hypervigilance. These must be fragments -- a hallway, footsteps, the quality of a closing door -- not a narrative flashback. Per constraints.md: no explicit backstory chapters, no flashbacks that are thinly disguised exposition dumps.
