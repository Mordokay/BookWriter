# World

## Premise

A civilization of hyper-intelligent frogs has existed on Earth for roughly 200 million years, hidden from humanity entirely. They possess interstellar travel, quantum displacement of matter, organic computation, and a self-renewing energy substrate that humans have walked past for centuries without noticing. They also have no sewers, crude wet-root furniture, gossip-driven local politics, and ongoing arguments about fermented fly preparation. The Amazon rainforest is their capital. The biologists who stumble into their world are not the first humans to do so, but they may be the most inconvenient.

## Time Period

Present day. The human world is contemporary. The frog world operates on timescales that make human history look like a weather report.

## The Civilization: The Threnn

The frogs call themselves the **Threnn** (approximate transliteration; the actual word involves a sub-vocal vibration and a color-shift that humans cannot produce). The name roughly translates to "the wet continuity" or "those who persist through moisture." They find both translations inadequate but have stopped correcting humans about it.

### Age

Approximately 200 million years of continuous civilization, with precursor intelligence stretching back further. They predate flowering plants. They watched the dinosaurs die. They found it instructive.

### Geographic Reach

Hidden settlements worldwide, connected by quantum displacement. Total population: estimated 4-6 million across all settlements. They have never been numerous. They consider this a feature.

**Major settlements:**
- **The Amazon Basin** (center of civilization, largest population, seat of the Marsh Federations)
- **Siberian Permafrost Warrens** (cold-adapted Threnn, culturally stoic, considered humorless by Amazon frogs)
- **Central African Rift Pools** (oldest continuous settlement after the Amazon, strong archival traditions)
- **Southeast Asian Canopy Clusters** (smallest populations, most technologically experimental)
- **Northern European Bog Networks** (mostly dormant, activated seasonally, reputation for melancholy)

The Amazon frogs consider themselves the spiritual and cultural center of Threnn civilization. The Siberians think this is pretentious. The Africans think both groups talk too much. These opinions have persisted for roughly 80 million years.

## Communication

### Native Language: Threnn-song

Threnn communication operates on four simultaneous channels:

1. **Vocalization:** Clicks, croaks, harmonic overtones. Carries semantic content. Roughly analogous to human speech but with a wider tonal range.
2. **Vibration:** Sub-vocal pulses transmitted through water, stone, and wet surfaces. Carries emotional and relational context. Humans can sometimes feel it as a faint buzzing in the chest or teeth.
3. **Chromatophore shifts:** Rapid color changes across the skin. Carries nuance, irony, emphasis, and social signals that have no vocal equivalent. Functions somewhat like facial expression but with the precision of punctuation.
4. **Chemical signaling:** Pheromone and mucus-based. Carries mood, health status, and identity markers. Humans can smell it faintly. It smells like wet copper and crushed herbs.

Full Threnn-song uses all four channels simultaneously. Translating into human language is like transcribing an orchestra into a single whistle. The frogs find this limitation charming in the way one finds a child's drawing charming.

### Human Languages

The Threnn have been monitoring human civilizations for millennia. Their linguists (a respected but somewhat eccentric specialization) maintain fluency in dozens of human languages. They find human languages structurally fascinating and communicatively primitive -- "like building a cathedral with only four bricks," as one archivist reportedly described English.

Most Threnn who interact with humans speak with precise, slightly formal diction. They learned from monitoring, not from conversation, so their speech occasionally reflects outdated idioms, academic registers, or phrases absorbed from radio broadcasts, scientific papers, and overheard arguments. Noll's English, for instance, has a faintly Victorian administrative quality. Krrik speaks with the clipped impatience of someone who learned the language mainly from engineering journals.

They are aware that talking frogs are inherently absurd to humans. They do not care. They found humans absurd first.

## The Main Settlement: Vreth-in-the-Root

The primary Amazon settlement. Located beneath and within the root systems of an ancient stretch of rainforest, extending roughly twelve kilometers along a river system that appears on no human map. The settlement is partially subterranean, partially arboreal, partially aquatic. Human categories of "underground" and "above ground" do not cleanly apply.

### Anchor Image

Simultaneously temple, fungus garden, laboratory, and village. A place that is ancient, alive, communal, and slightly unclean.

### Architecture

Nothing in Vreth-in-the-Root was built. Everything was grown, coaxed, cultivated, or persuaded. The distinction between architecture and horticulture does not exist here.

- **Walls and corridors:** Shaped from living root systems, hardened with mineral secretions over centuries. Warm to the touch. Faintly damp. Some walls pulse imperceptibly, still growing.
- **Bioluminescent pathways:** Cultivated fungal and algal networks embedded in floors and walls. Dim blue-green light, shifting subtly with air currents. Not bright. The Threnn see well in low light and find human lighting requirements baffling and aggressive.
- **Water channels:** A continuous network of flowing water threading through every level of the settlement. Serves as transportation (for small objects and messages), temperature regulation, communication medium (vibrations travel through water), and ambient sound. The constant sound of moving water is the settlement's heartbeat.
- **Furniture:** Crude. Root-shaped seating, stone ledges, moss pads. Functional and uncomfortable by human standards. The frogs do not sit the way humans sit. Furniture is an afterthought in a civilization that squats, floats, and clings.

### Key Locations

**The Speaking Chambers:** A series of partially flooded amphitheaters where political discussions, legal proceedings, and community meetings occur. Attendees sit half-submerged in warm mineral water. Acoustics are designed to carry vibration as well as sound. Chromatophore displays are visible to all. Humans attending these sessions find them disorienting -- part town hall, part sensory immersion, part fever dream.

**The Pools:** Communal bathing and social spaces. Different pools serve different functions: hot mineral pools for dispute mediation, cool running pools for casual socializing, still deep pools for meditation. The pools are where most real decisions get made, despite official proceedings happening in the Speaking Chambers. Everyone knows this. Nobody officially acknowledges it.

**The Archive of Pem:** A vast organic library maintained by Archivist Pem and predecessors stretching back millions of years. Knowledge stored in living tissue cultures, spore patterns, crystallized mucus formations, song recordings encoded in fungal networks, and biological imprints. The archive smells like old mushrooms, mineral water, and something faintly electrical. Parts of it are alive in ways that make "library" a misleading word. Retrieving information sometimes requires singing to it.

**Krrik's Laboratory:** A sprawling, cluttered workspace near the settlement's eastern edge. Contains organic computation arrays (cultivated neural moss beds), quantum displacement apparatus (looks like a ring of wet stones with fungal threading -- deeply unimpressive visually), voss research samples, and half-finished experiments in various states of decay. Krrik considers tidiness a sign of insufficient ambition. The lab smells of ozone and fermentation.

**Residential Warrens:** Networks of small, interconnected chambers where frogs sleep, eat, mate, argue, and live. Communal by default. Individual nesting spaces exist but are considered slightly eccentric. The warrens are noisy, damp, and alive with the sounds of domestic life: croaking, splashing, the clicks of conversation, occasional disputes about food storage.

**Tadpole Nurseries:** Communal aquatic chambers where young are raised collectively. Warm, shallow, carefully maintained. Staffed by rotating caretakers. The nurseries are one of the few spaces in frog society treated with genuine, unironic reverence. Even Talla does not criticize the nurseries.

**The Pruning Staging Grounds:** An open area near the settlement's western perimeter where equipment, personnel, and ritual preparations for the Pruning are organized. During non-Pruning periods, it functions as a gathering space and competitive vibration-game arena. Currently active and tense.

**Fermentation Cellars:** Underground chambers where various insect, algae, and fungal preparations are cultured, fermented, and stored. A source of endless culinary debate. Oona spends considerable time here. The smells range from pleasantly earthy to aggressively biological.

## Technology: The Primitive-Impossible Contrast

Every major technological achievement exists alongside something humans would consider embarrassingly basic. This is not a failure of development. The Threnn simply prioritized differently, and their priorities make perfect sense from inside their biology and philosophy -- and almost no sense from outside.

### Voss

**What it is:** A living substrate found in certain deep forest root systems. It resembles a pale, faintly luminous mycelial network threaded through soil and stone. To the touch, it feels like warm, slightly wet leather that breathes. It is technically a symbiotic organism -- part fungal, part mineral, part something the frogs have never fully classified and do not seem bothered by not classifying.

**What it does:** Voss generates energy. Continuously. It converts ambient radiation, geothermal heat, and organic decay into a stable, usable energy output. The mechanism is only partially understood even by Krrik. The frogs have used it for so long that understanding it completely feels unnecessary, like humans fully understanding electricity before flipping a light switch.

**How the frogs use it:** Casually. Voss powers everything: the bioluminescent networks, the organic computation arrays, the quantum displacement systems, temperature regulation, water flow. It is integrated into the settlement the way wiring is integrated into a human building -- mundane, omnipresent, and noticed only when it malfunctions. Frogs do not treat it as sacred, guarded, or dramatic. It is infrastructure.

**Why humans never found it:** They were not looking. The substrate exists in root systems humans classify as unremarkable. It operates at energy levels and frequencies human instruments are not calibrated to detect. It has been there the entire time. The frogs find this equal parts amusing and unsurprising.

**The catch:** Voss is alive, and it grows. Normally this growth is slow and manageable. During cosmic radiation peaks -- roughly every 30-40 human years -- the growth accelerates dramatically. This is the basis of the Pruning.

**Primitive counterpart:** The energy is infinite, but it is distributed through channels that leak, require constant manual maintenance, and occasionally grow in the wrong direction, flooding someone's sleeping chamber with warm luminescent fungus.

### Organic Computation

**What it is:** Biological computing networks grown in cultivated moss beds. Neural tissue cultures, fungal mycelial networks, and engineered bacterial colonies form processing arrays of extraordinary density and speed.

**How it works:** Information is encoded in chemical gradients, electrical impulses, and molecular structures within living tissue. The arrays are grown, not manufactured. They learn, adapt, and occasionally develop unexpected behaviors. Krrik once had to "argue" an array out of a recursive loop by altering its nutrient solution.

**Capabilities:** Processing power exceeding anything human technology has achieved. Pattern recognition, modeling, prediction, and data storage at scales humans would find incomprehensible. Used for Pruning calculations, quantum displacement coordination, climate modeling, and archival data processing.

**Primitive counterpart:** The arrays must be fed, watered, and maintained like gardens. They attract insects. They smell. They occasionally die if someone forgets to adjust humidity levels. The most powerful computational system on Earth is tended by a frog with a watering vessel.

### Quantum Displacement

**What it is:** The ability to move matter across distances instantaneously. The Threnn use it for travel between settlements, communication, material transport, and -- historically -- exploration of other star systems.

**How it works:** Partially understood. Involves precise manipulation of quantum states using voss energy channeled through organic computation arrays. The apparatus looks like a ring of wet stones with fungal threading. Deeply unimpressive to look at. Krrik describes the mechanism using terminology that sounds like physics filtered through biology filtered through poetry. Reed finds this maddening.

**Practical use:** Routine travel between global settlements. Cargo transport. Emergency communication. The frogs treat it the way humans treat commercial flights -- useful, occasionally inconvenient, not especially remarkable.

**Limitations:** Requires functional voss networks at both origin and destination. Cannot displace living matter through solid barriers. Precision decreases with distance. Interstellar displacement requires enormous energy expenditure and careful calculation. Short-range displacement within the settlement is trivial.

**Primitive counterpart:** The displacement platforms are operated on a first-come basis with no formal scheduling system. Disputes about queue priority are common and petty. The most advanced transportation technology on Earth is subject to the same social dysfunction as a bus stop.

### Interstellar Travel

The Threnn have visited other star systems. Several dozen, over the course of their history. They went mostly out of curiosity.

**What they found:** Rocks. Gas. Ice. Microbial life on a few worlds. One planet with complex multicellular organisms that were, in Krrik's assessment, "ambitious but structurally confused." No other civilizations. No intelligence. A great deal of empty space.

They found the universe disappointing. Not in a dramatic, existential way -- more in the way one finds a heavily advertised restaurant merely adequate. They still go occasionally. The trips take enormous energy and planning. The results rarely justify the effort. The Siberians maintain an active interstellar exploration program. The Amazon frogs consider this a harmless eccentricity.

**Primitive counterpart:** A civilization capable of crossing light-years has no agreed-upon system for labeling food stores and regularly loses track of communal tools.

### Medicine

**What it is:** Advanced biochemistry and biological engineering. The Threnn have extended their natural lifespans considerably and can treat injuries, diseases, and genetic conditions with precision humans will not achieve for centuries.

**Capabilities:** Regeneration of lost limbs (slow but reliable). Cancer is managed trivially. Infectious diseases are uncommon thanks to environmental engineering. Neurological repair. Lifespan extension from a natural 5-8 years to 25-35 years.

**Limitations:** They still die. 35 years is the rough upper limit. Aging is slowed but not stopped. The frogs have accepted this with a philosophical resignation that ranges from genuine serenity to quiet desperation, depending on the individual. Vesh calls death "a scheduling formality." Talla calls it "the one honest thing about being alive."

**Primitive counterpart:** Despite advanced medicine, the frogs still suffer from common fungal infections that resist treatment, digestive complaints from experimental fermentation, and seasonal skin irritations that they manage with folk remedies of dubious efficacy. Oona swears by a particular moss compress for joint pain. Krrik considers this barely distinguishable from superstition.

### Memory Transfer

**What it is:** The system by which knowledge, experience, and cultural memory are transmitted across generations. Critical for a short-lived species maintaining a 200-million-year civilization.

**Methods:**
- **Biological imprints:** Chemical and neural patterns encoded in organic substrates. A dying frog can deposit key memories into a living tissue archive. The fidelity is imperfect -- memories arrive with emotional coloring, gaps, and occasional distortions that the archivist must interpret.
- **Spore records:** Information encoded in the molecular structure of cultivated spores. Durable. Long-lasting. Low resolution. Used for factual records, not experiential memory.
- **Song archives:** Knowledge encoded in complex harmonic sequences. Requires trained singers to read and reproduce. The most emotionally rich storage medium but the most vulnerable to drift and reinterpretation.
- **Direct neural sharing:** Temporary consciousness-linking between living individuals. Used for urgent knowledge transfer, mentorship, and the consciousness-merging rituals. Intimate, disorienting, and culturally significant.

**Primitive counterpart:** Despite these systems, an enormous amount of daily knowledge is transmitted through gossip, apprenticeship, and oral tradition -- the same way humans did it for most of their history. The archive is vast but curated. Most of what a frog knows, they learned from their neighbor.

## Government: The Marsh Federations

### Official Structure

The Threnn govern through **distributed consensus with rotating coordinators.** Settlements are organized into pools (neighborhoods), pools into districts, districts into the settlement assembly. Decisions flow upward through discussion, vibration-polling, and chromatic consensus displays. Coordinators at each level rotate on short cycles (typically 1-2 frog years). No one holds permanent office. Power is, officially, everyone's and no one's.

The coordinators are called **"temporary coordinators of distributed harmonies."** The full title is used in all official proceedings. It takes longer to say than most of the decisions it authorizes.

### How Decisions Are Actually Made

In practice: the Speaking Chambers debate, the Pools decide. Informal networks of experienced, respected, or simply persistent frogs shape outcomes before they reach formal consensus. The most influential frogs are those who have coordinated multiple times, maintained wide social networks, and mastered the art of appearing to facilitate discussion while steering it. Everyone participates in theory. In practice, about 30% of the population drives most decisions, and everyone knows which 30%.

### The High Speaker

**High Speaker Noll** occupies a role that is not, officially, a leadership position. The High Speaker is the coordinator of inter-settlement relations, the voice of the Marsh Federations in dealings with other global settlements, and the facilitator of civilization-wide decisions such as Pruning coordination.

Noll insists he is a facilitator. He facilitates with the precision and authority of someone who has been facilitating for most of his adult life and whose facilitation has a way of producing the outcomes he prefers. He is not a ruler. He is something more durable than a ruler: he is the person everyone defers to while maintaining they defer to no one.

### Dispute Resolution

No formal courts exist. Disputes are mediated in warm mineral pools by respected community members. The theory is that warm water promotes hormonal calm and mutual openness. The practice is that disputes still get heated, but the participants are wet, which makes dramatic gestures less effective.

Legal testimony is delivered through song, vibration, or chromatophore display, depending on the nature of the claim. Emotional testimony carries legal weight. This strikes humans as bizarre. The frogs consider human reliance on verbal testimony -- a single channel, easily manipulated -- to be naive.

### Dissent

Dissent is tolerated in the way a family tolerates an outspoken member at dinner: with patience, deflection, and the quiet assumption that the dissenter will eventually come around. Persistent dissent is handled through social pressure rather than formal sanction. The community does not punish critics. It exhausts them with kindness, inclusion, and the implication that their concerns have been heard, valued, and gently set aside.

Talla-of-the-Third-Pool has been heard, valued, and gently set aside for most of her adult life. She is not fooled.

## Economy

### Structure

Communist in the literal sense: no currency, communal ownership of resources, labor organized by need and ability. Voss energy is abundant and free. Food is cultivated communally. Housing is communal. The concept of personal wealth does not exist in a form humans would recognize.

### Specialization

Clear specializations exist: engineers, archivists, ritual guides, nursery caretakers, fermentation specialists, displacement operators, Pruning teams, linguists, medical practitioners. These are not formally assigned. Frogs gravitate toward aptitudes and are mentored into roles. Changing specialization is common given short lifespans -- a frog might be a nursery caretaker in youth and an archivist in later years.

### Status

Officially flat. Practically not. Archive workers carry quiet prestige. Engineers are respected but considered socially odd. Ritual guides hold spiritual authority that translates into political influence in ways nobody officially tracks. Nursery caretakers are universally valued and universally underprioritized in resource discussions. Fermentation specialists are either admired or considered obsessive, depending on the quality of their most recent batch.

Oona summarizes frog economics accurately: "Nobody owns anything. Some people just seem to have better sleeping chambers and shorter wait times at the displacement platforms."

## Spirituality: The Wet Way

The Wet Way is the frogs' spiritual framework -- not quite a religion, not quite a philosophy, more like an entire way of being that has been refined over 200 million years. If a reader wanted to practice it, they could. It is internally consistent, practically applicable, and only slightly dependent on having permeable skin.

Its roots are Taoist: the universe has a flow, and the highest wisdom is to stop resisting it. Its mood is nihilistic: nothing means anything inherently, which is liberating rather than depressing. Its lifestyle is hippie: communal, nature-embedded, consciousness-expanding, anti-materialist, unconcerned with ownership or status, and deeply convinced that the boundaries between beings are less solid than they appear.

The frogs call it **The Wet Way** (approximate translation; the Threnn term involves a sustained harmonic implying "the path that flows through permeable things"). It is not institutional. There are no temples separate from daily life, no clergy distinct from the community, no scripture separate from the archive. The Wet Way is woven into eating, bathing, working, arguing, mating, dying, and defecating. Especially defecating. Vesh considers this consistency a point of pride.

---

### The Core Concepts

The Wet Way has a handful of named ideas that recur in daily speech, ritual, debate, and humor. These are the concepts a reader should absorb across the book:

**The Puddle**

What the frogs call the self. "You are a puddle" is not an insult. It is a description of nature. A puddle forms when conditions align -- rain falls, ground dips, water collects. The puddle has a shape. It reflects things. It is real while it lasts. But it was never separate from the water it is made of. When it evaporates or overflows or seeps into the ground, it has not died. It has stopped being a puddle and resumed being water.

Every frog is a puddle. Every frog knows this. Some find it comforting. Some find it terrifying. Most find it obvious, in the way that breathing is obvious -- true, constant, and not worth panicking about unless you stop to think about it, which the Wet Way gently discourages.

Vesh's version: "The self is a temporary swamp event. You are a puddle with opinions. I mean this as a compliment."

**The Croaking**

What humans might call God, except it is not a being, not a creator, not a judge, and not watching. The Croaking is the sound the universe makes when it is happening. Everything croaks -- rocks croak slowly, water croaks by flowing, stars croak by burning, dying frogs croak by dying, moss croaks by growing, the voss croaks by glowing. To live is to croak. To die is to stop croaking individually and resume croaking as part of everything else.

The Croaking is not worshipped. You do not pray to it. You participate in it. You ARE it, temporarily, in the same way a wave is the ocean temporarily. The wave does not pray to the ocean. It rises, moves, and returns.

When asked about a creator, Vesh's standard response: "Who made the rain? The rain made the rain. That is the nature of rain."

The Croaking is sometimes invoked casually: "The Croaking is loud today" (said on a day when everything feels alive and connected). "The Croaking is quiet" (said on a day when everything feels flat and separate, which the frogs consider a temporary spiritual weather pattern, not a crisis).

**The Almost**

The central spiritual experience and aspiration. During the merging rituals, when individual frogs synchronize their breathing, their body chemistry, and their awareness, there is a moment -- brief, unreliable, unmistakable -- when the chord of separate selves nearly resolves into a single unified consciousness. Individual boundaries thin. Shared perception flickers. The puddles almost remember they are water.

It has never fully resolved. In 200 million years of trying. Not once.

This is either the great failure of the Wet Way or its deepest teaching. The central theological debate:

- **The Resolvers** believe full merging is possible and the Threnn simply have not achieved it yet. Every Almost is a step closer. The mission is real.
- **The Tryers** believe the Almost IS the point. The value is in the trying, not the achieving. Full resolution would mean the end of individuality, which would mean no one left to appreciate the chord. "The chord is the trying" is their mantra.
- **The Skeptics** (Talla's position) believe the Almost is a psychochemical artifact of synchronized breathing in mineral-rich water, and the theological framework built around it is a 200-million-year rationalization of a pleasant group bath.

All three positions have been argued continuously for longer than mammals have existed. None has won. The frogs consider this appropriate.

**The Flow**

The Taoist core. The universe has a direction -- not a purpose, not a plan, but a current. Water flows downhill. Life flows toward complexity. Consciousness flows toward connection. Resisting the flow is possible but exhausting and ultimately pointless, like a puddle trying to flow uphill.

The highest virtue in the Wet Way is **going with the flow** -- not passivity, but active non-resistance. Doing what the situation calls for without imposing your preferences on it. The closest human concept is wu wei, but the frogs arrived at it independently and from a different biological direction: when you breathe through your skin, the idea that you are separate from your environment is difficult to maintain.

In practice, "going with the flow" means: eat when hungry, sleep when tired, argue when the argument matters, stop arguing when it doesn't, grieve quickly, love without clinging, defecate without embarrassment, and die without making a fuss. The frogs manage most of these most of the time. The dying-without-making-a-fuss part is harder than the philosophy suggests, which Vesh acknowledges by not acknowledging it.

**Permeability as Virtue**

The highest spiritual state is openness -- being permeable to the world passing through you. Closed systems die. Open systems participate. This is both biological fact (the frogs literally breathe through their skin, absorb chemical signals, and release their own) and spiritual principle.

A "dry self" is a frog who is too closed off -- too attached to individual identity, too resistant to communal experience, too insistent on boundaries. Not exactly an insult, more like a concerned observation: "She has been very dry lately" means she is clinging to selfhood, resisting the communal flow. Talla has been called dry her entire adult life. She considers it a compliment.

A "wet self" is not a goal so much as a natural state that most frogs return to when they stop trying to be separate. The breathing circles are, in one sense, just a structured way of getting wet again.

### The Creation Story: The First Wetness

The Wet Way's cosmogony is barely a cosmogony. It goes like this:

*Before the First Wetness, there was dryness. The dryness was not alive. It was not dead either, because death requires life first. The dryness just was. Then the dryness got wet. This was an improvement. From the wetness came movement. From movement came pattern. From pattern came awareness. From awareness came the question "why?" From the question came the realization that there is no why, only wet. This was also an improvement.*

That is the entire creation story. The frogs consider it complete. Occasionally a young frog at a naming ceremony asks, "But who made the dryness wet?" and is told, with patient amusement: "The wetness made the dryness wet. That is the nature of wetness." If the young frog persists ("But where did the wetness come from?"), they are told: "From the dryness. They are the same thing in different moods."

Vesh can expand this into a forty-minute lecture. The core theology, however, fits on a leaf.

### Sayings, Proverbs, and Recurring Phrases

These should appear naturally in dialogue and narration throughout the book. They are the texture of the religion -- the phrases a reader will remember and possibly start using:

- **"Wet in, wet out."** The lifecycle acknowledgment. Equivalent to "ashes to ashes." You came from moisture, you return to moisture. Said at mournings, at namings, and when someone drops something in the water.

- **"May you dissolve gently."** The farewell and death blessing. May your death be easy, may you release your puddle-shape without clinging, may you return to the water with grace. Also used casually when leaving a party: "Dissolving gently. The fermented grubs are terrible tonight."

- **"The puddle forgets the rain."** Said when someone is too self-focused, too wrapped up in their individual perspective. A gentle reminder that you came from something larger and will return to it.

- **"Croak and be croaked through."** The closest thing to a prayer. Participate in existence and let existence participate in you. Some frogs say it at morning wetting. Others say it reflexively before difficult moments, the way a human might say "here goes nothing."

- **"The chord is the trying."** Said by those who believe the merging mission's value is in the attempt, not the achievement. Also used as consolation after any failure: "Well, the chord is the trying."

- **"Every puddle thinks it invented water."** Said to the arrogant. A humility reminder. Also applicable to civilizations.

- **"Who made the rain? The rain made the rain."** The cosmological shrug. Used to shut down unanswerable questions.

- **"The dryness was not alive. Then it got wet. This was an improvement."** The creation story, often quoted with amusement, especially when something goes well. Also applicable to cocktails.

- **"A dry self cracks."** Warning against emotional closure. If you stay too closed, you break. Permeability is structural integrity.

- **"Flow, don't push."** The wu-wei principle in four syllables. Said by parents to children, by coaches to athletes, by Vesh to everyone. Talla's response: "Easy to say when the current goes your direction."

### Daily Practices

**Morning Wetting:** Most frogs begin each day by sitting in water and consciously breathing through their skin for a few minutes. Not meditation exactly -- more like a daily reminder that the boundary between self and world is permeable. Some take it very seriously, closing their eyes, synchronizing with nearby frogs. Others do it while eating breakfast and gossiping. Oona does both simultaneously and considers this entirely consistent with the Wet Way's teachings about integrating the sacred and the mundane.

**Mealtime Acknowledgment:** Before eating, many frogs pause briefly. Not a prayer -- more like a nod. An acknowledgment that what they are about to eat was also croaking recently and that the eater will eventually be eaten in turn. The phrase, if any is spoken, is simply: "Wet in, wet out." Some frogs skip this. Nobody judges them. Well, some frogs judge them quietly.

**Casual Invocations:** The Croaking and the flow are referenced constantly in ordinary speech, the way humans reference luck, fate, or weather. "The Croaking is strong today" (things feel connected). "Against the flow on that one" (that decision was forced). "Very dry of you" (you're being rigid). These are not prayers. They are idioms. The religion is the language.

### Rituals

**Breathing Circles:** Regular communal gatherings in warm pools. Participants synchronize their breathing, which alters blood chemistry and loosens ego boundaries. The experience ranges from mildly pleasant (like a warm bath with friends) to profoundly disorienting (shared sensory perception, blurred identity). Frequency: daily for the devout, weekly for most, "when I remember" for Oona, never for Talla. The circles are part spiritual practice, part social event, part functional Pruning maintenance. These distinctions used to be clear. They are not clear anymore. Nobody is sure whether this is a problem.

**The Mourning Dissolution:** When a frog dies, the community gathers at a pool within hours. The dead frog's body is placed in the water. As it releases its final chemical signature -- the last biological broadcast of a life -- the living frogs absorb it through their skin. For a few seconds, each frog in the pool experiences a flash of the dead frog's final emotional state. This is not metaphor. It is chemistry. The dead frog's last feeling passes through the living like a ripple.

The experience varies: some final states are peaceful, some confused, some irritated (one elderly frog's last feeling was apparently annoyance about the temperature of the pool, which became a joke that lasted several generations). After the flash, the water is cycled, the biological imprint is deposited in the archive by Pem, and life resumes. The whole thing takes about an hour.

Prolonged individual grief is gently discouraged, not because the dead don't matter but because holding onto the shape of a dissolved puddle is considered spiritually unhealthy: "The puddle has returned to the water. Do not hold the shape of what has flowed away." In practice, some frogs grieve longer than others, and nobody actually stops them. The encouragement is philosophical. The enforcement is nonexistent. The Wet Way is tolerant of its own ideals being imperfectly followed.

**The Naming Croak:** When a tadpole completes metamorphosis, the pool-neighborhood gathers. A sustained communal vibration "introduces" the new frog to the settlement's acoustic identity -- essentially saying "this one is here now" in a way that every frog in range can feel. The new frog produces their first individual croak. It is heard by everyone. "Wet in," the community says. The new frog does not say "wet out." That comes later.

**The Deep Soak:** An occasional (not regularly scheduled) ritual where a group of frogs submerge in mineral pools for extended hours, entering a state of reduced ego boundaries. Not the full merging -- more like loosening the edges of the puddle without dissolving it entirely. Somewhere between meditation, a hot spring, and a very mild psychedelic experience. Some frogs find it transcendent. Others find it boring. Oona finds it "pleasant but long" and usually falls asleep halfway through, which Vesh insists is also a valid form of spiritual practice.

**The Full Merging:** The most intense ritual, performed by experienced practitioners (and operationally by displacement operators during the Pruning). Deeper than breathing circles, more sustained than the Deep Soak. The goal is the Almost -- the moment when the chord nearly resolves. Participants report shared perception, dissolved ego boundaries, and a collective emotional state that persists for hours afterward. Some participants lose coherence and need medical attention. A very small number do not return to individual consciousness. They are honored and not discussed.

### Theological Debates (Never Resolved)

These arguments have been running for millions of years. Different frogs hold different positions. The Wet Way is not monolithic.

1. **Is the Almost a failure or the point?** Resolvers vs. Tryers. The oldest debate. No consensus. Probably never will be. The frogs consider this appropriate.

2. **Did the merging mission arise from spiritual aspiration or Pruning necessity?** The rituals and the planetary defense are now inseparable. Which came first? Does it matter? Krrik says it matters enormously. Vesh says it doesn't matter at all. Both are correct in ways that infuriate the other.

3. **Is permeability spiritual truth or biology rationalized into religion?** The frogs breathe through their skin. Their philosophy of openness grows from this biology. Is the Wet Way profound wisdom, or is it what happens when a species with permeable skin builds a theology? Hart asks this question. No frog has ever given her a satisfying answer.

4. **Can you practice the Wet Way without attending breathing circles?** Talla says yes. The mainstream says it is complicated. Noll says of course, participation is voluntary, while presiding over a system where non-participation is tracked and socially penalized.

5. **Are the frogs who dissolve in merging and don't return saints or casualties?** The official position: they gave the ultimate gift, dissolving their puddle into the larger water. The unofficial position: they are dead and the system that killed them calls it an honor. Both positions are held simultaneously by most frogs without apparent contradiction.

6. **Is the Wet Way true?** Vesh, asked directly: "Of course not. Truth is a dry concept. The Wet Way is useful, which is wetter and more honest. All religions are stories. Ours is a good one. It keeps us alive, keeps us connected, and only occasionally kills the participants. By the standards of religious history, this is exceptional."

### How Different Characters Relate to the Wet Way

- **Vesh:** The public philosopher and true believer -- or the best performer of belief. He embodies the Wet Way so completely that it is impossible to tell where his conviction ends and his performance begins. He may secretly fear death despite preaching dissolution. This is either hypocrisy or the most honest thing about him.
- **Noll:** Uses the Wet Way politically. He believes in it the way a shrewd administrator believes in the constitution -- it is the operating system of the civilization, and its value is proven by the civilization's survival. Whether it is "true" is not a question Noll considers relevant.
- **Oona:** Practices the Wet Way the way most people practice anything -- casually, habitually, with genuine feeling at certain moments and complete indifference at others. Morning wetting while gossiping. Mourning dissolution with real tears. Breathing circles when she remembers. The Deep Soak when her joints hurt. She does not think about theology. She lives in it.
- **Talla:** The critic. She considers the Wet Way a beautiful set of ideas that have been weaponized for social control. She does not reject the philosophy -- she rejects the mandatory breathing circles, the social pressure, the convenient fusion of spirituality and state power. "Flow, don't push," she quotes. "Then stop pushing me into the pool."
- **Krrik:** Considers the spiritual framework mostly irrelevant and the rituals primarily a technology. The merging works because of biochemistry and neural synchronization, not because of the Croaking. He participates in breathing circles under protest during Prunings, and his annoyance at having to do so is genuine and visible. Vesh tells him that his resistance is itself a form of spiritual practice. Krrik tells Vesh to eat an insect.
- **Pem:** Sees the Wet Way through the lens of deep time. Religions evolve. The Wet Way of today is not the Wet Way of a million years ago. The archive contains versions of the theology that would be unrecognizable to modern practitioners. Pem preserves all versions. Pem judges none. Pem considers the current version "adequate for its generation."

### What Makes the Wet Way Practicable for a Human Reader

If a reader wanted to take the Wet Way home with them, the portable version would be:

1. You are not permanent. You are a temporary shape that water has taken. This is not sad. It is the nature of water.
2. The universe is not designed for you. It is not designed at all. It is alive, and you are part of it, and that is enough.
3. Stay open. Closed systems die. Let the world pass through you. Breathe. Feel. Be affected. Affect others. The boundaries between you and everything else are thinner than you think.
4. Meaning is not found. Meaning is what happens when you stop looking for it and start participating. Croak and be croaked through.
5. Death is not an ending. It is a return. Wet in, wet out.
6. The chord almost resolves. It never fully resolves. The trying is the practice. The Almost is enough.
7. Flow, don't push.

## The Pruning

### What It Is

The cyclical crisis that has defined Threnn civilization for its entire existence. Roughly every 30-40 human years (every 15-20 frog-years, approximately once per generation), cosmic radiation peaks trigger accelerated voss growth. The normally slow-growing substrate erupts into rapid, aggressive expansion. If unchecked, the voss would consume the Amazon basin within months and the planet within years. It has to be cut back.

### The Mechanism

The Threnn "prune" the voss using a combination of:

1. **Quantum displacement:** Overgrown voss material is displaced -- removed from the root systems and scattered into deep ocean trenches, sub-arctic permafrost, or (in extreme cases) off-planet. This requires enormous energy and precise coordination.
2. **Synchronized consciousness:** The displacement operations require neural synchronization among hundreds of operators working in concert. This synchronization is achieved through the same rituals used in the consciousness-merging mission. The operators must function as a collective mind to coordinate displacement at the necessary scale and precision.
3. **Biological modulation:** Certain biochemical treatments can slow voss growth temporarily, buying time for displacement operations. These treatments are Krrik's specialty and are the subject of ongoing research.

### The Blurred Line

The consciousness-merging rituals predate the Pruning protocols. Or the Pruning protocols predate the rituals. Or they co-evolved. The archive records are ambiguous on this point. What is clear is that the spiritual practices and the planetary defense technology are now functionally inseparable. You cannot perform the Pruning without the rituals. You cannot perform the rituals without reinforcing the spiritual framework.

Talla considers this a suspicious coincidence. She argues that the Pruning is used to justify mandatory participation in consciousness-merging, which in turn reinforces the ideological unity that keeps the current power structure intact. "Very convenient," she says, "that the thing that saves us is also the thing that requires us to dissolve ourselves."

She is not entirely wrong. She is also not entirely right. The voss growth is real. The danger is real. The Pruning works. Whether it requires this particular form of consciousness-merging, or whether alternative approaches exist, is a question nobody in power seems interested in exploring.

### The Current Pruning

More severe than usual. The cosmic radiation peak is stronger than any in living memory (which, given frog lifespans, means stronger than any in the last 30-35 years; the archive records suggest comparable events have occurred before, roughly every 200-300 years). The voss growth is faster, denser, and more geographically distributed than standard Pruning protocols are designed to handle.

This has created:
- **Operational pressure:** More displacement operations required, over a longer period, with greater precision.
- **Political pressure:** Noll has called for increased breathing circle participation and accelerated consciousness-synchronization training. Talla sees this as a power grab disguised as emergency response.
- **Scientific pressure:** Krrik is working on modified biological modulators to slow the growth, but the voss is behaving in ways his models do not predict.
- **Existential pressure:** If this Pruning fails, the consequences are planetary. The frogs have always succeeded before. They have never faced one quite like this.

### The Humans' Role

Minor but specific. The biologists' primate research includes years of data on canopy behavior, root system observations, and environmental monitoring in the Amazon basin. Reed's team has inadvertently documented voss growth patterns from the surface -- subtle changes in root density, soil chemistry, and canopy health that the frogs, observing from within, had not correlated. This outside perspective -- a surface-level biological observation that illuminates a voss behavior pattern the frogs had not considered -- provides a small but genuine contribution to Krrik's modulation research.

The humans do not save the frogs. They hand them a puzzle piece the frogs did not know they were missing. The frogs do the rest.

### Pruning Infrastructure

- **Staging grounds:** Open area with displacement platforms, coordination pools, supply stores, and medical stations.
- **Specialized teams:** Displacement operators (the most demanding role, requiring sustained consciousness-synchronization), modulators (Krrik's biochemists), surveyors (who map voss growth in real time), and support staff.
- **Protocols:** Detailed, rehearsed, and updated for each Pruning cycle. The current protocols are being revised on the fly due to the abnormal severity.

## Frog Biology

### Physical Basics

Small. The largest Threnn are roughly 8-10 centimeters in length. Most are 5-7 centimeters. They are physically frogs -- amphibians with permeable skin, powerful hind legs, wide mouths, and prominent eyes. Their intelligence resides in brains that are, relative to body size, extraordinarily dense and structurally unlike any amphibian brain humans have studied.

The contrast between their physical smallness and their intellectual and technological power is inherent and permanent. A creature that fits in a human palm can discuss quantum mechanics, has visited other solar systems, and maintains a 200-million-year continuous civilization. This is absurd. The frogs do not find it absurd. They find human size requirements absurd -- "so much organism for so little thinking," as Vesh once observed.

### Lifespan

25-35 years with medical intervention. Natural lifespan would be 5-8 years. The extension is impressive but limited. A frog born today will be dead before a human child starts secondary school.

This shapes everything:
- **Politics:** Leadership cycles are short. Institutional memory resides in the archive, not in individuals. There is no equivalent of a human elder statesman who has governed for decades.
- **Education:** Rapid. Mentorship-intensive. A frog is functionally adult within 2-3 years and must acquire specialized knowledge quickly.
- **Relationships:** Intense and compressed. Emotional bonds form fast and are not expected to be permanent. Monogamy exists but is not normative. The concept of "growing old together" applies to a much shorter span.
- **Architecture:** Built to outlast individuals, not to serve them. The settlement is designed for the civilization, not for any particular generation. Individual comfort is secondary to communal continuity.
- **Ambition:** Personal legacy is a minor concern. Generational contribution matters more. A frog who completes one significant project in a lifetime has achieved something notable.
- **Death:** Frequent, expected, and processed communally. Mourning is ritualized and efficient. The Threnn cannot afford prolonged grief -- there is not enough time.

### Respiration and Permeability

Threnn breathe through both lungs and skin. Cutaneous respiration accounts for roughly 40% of gas exchange. Their skin is permeable to water, dissolved gases, and chemical signals. They are, in a literal biological sense, less bounded than humans. Their environment enters them. They enter their environment.

This permeability is the physiological basis of their philosophy. The boundary between self and world is not metaphorical for a frog -- it is genuinely blurred. Their comfort with dissolution, merging, and shared consciousness has biological roots. A human who breathed through their skin might develop a similar worldview. Or they might simply find it unpleasant.

### Chromatophore Communication

Threnn skin contains sophisticated chromatophore networks that produce rapid, controlled color changes. These function as a communication channel (see Threnn-song above) but also serve social and legal functions. Chromatophore patterns are individually unique, like fingerprints. Color changes during legal testimony are considered more reliable than vocalization because they are harder to consciously control -- a frog can lie with their voice more easily than with their skin. This is not absolute. Skilled liars exist. They are respected and distrusted in equal measure.

### Reproduction

Still aquatic. Eggs are laid in communal nursery pools. Tadpole development takes roughly 6-8 months. Tadpoles are raised communally -- parentage is known but not prioritized. All adults share nursery duties on rotation. The transition from tadpole to frog (metamorphosis) is celebrated as a community event.

Mating involves complex vocal and chromatophore displays. The frogs have opinions about what constitutes an attractive display. These opinions vary and are debated with the same intensity humans apply to fashion or music. Oona has strong views on the subject that Marsh finds fascinating and slightly bewildering.

## Secrecy

### How They Stay Hidden

The Threnn have remained undetected by humanity through a combination of:

- **Geography:** Settlements are located in areas humans find difficult to access -- deep rainforest, permafrost, remote bogs, dense canopy.
- **Scale:** They are small. Their architecture is organic and visually indistinguishable from natural root systems, fungal growth, and geological formations to the untrained eye.
- **Quantum displacement:** Allows rapid evacuation and relocation if human activity approaches. Monitoring stations at settlement perimeters detect human presence at considerable distance.
- **Active monitoring:** The Threnn have observed human activity for millennia. They know human settlement patterns, transportation routes, research methodologies, and satellite coverage. They position themselves in gaps.
- **Selective intervention:** On rare occasions, the Threnn have subtly redirected human activity away from settlements -- minor environmental modifications, induced navigational confusion (chemical signals that produce disorientation in mammals), or displacement of evidence.

### Philosophical Basis

Secrecy is not purely strategic. It is ideological. The Threnn believe that being known changes a thing. Observation alters the observed. Discovery creates entanglement. Contact produces obligation. These are not abstract metaphysical positions -- they are practical conclusions drawn from 200 million years of watching species interact.

The Threnn watched predators and prey co-evolve. They watched parasites and hosts reshape each other. They watched human civilizations discover each other and observed what followed. Their conclusion: knowledge of another is a form of power over them, and power over another eventually becomes power wielded against them.

Hiding is not cowardice. It is an intimacy protocol. They remain unknown because remaining unknown is the only way to remain free.

### Historical Precedent

Humans have discovered the Threnn before. Rarely -- perhaps a dozen times in recorded Threnn history. In every case, the humans were absorbed into frog society. They were treated well. They were given homes, roles, companionship. They were not allowed to leave.

Some adapted. Some did not. The archive preserves their biological imprints alongside frog records. They are remembered with the same ritual care as deceased Threnn. Whether this is honor or erasure depends on who you ask.

None ever returned to human society. The Threnn consider this regrettable but non-negotiable. "Discovery is a form of entanglement," Noll explains, as if describing a weather pattern.

## The Global Network

### Coordination

Global settlements communicate and travel via quantum displacement. Coordination happens through periodic inter-settlement assemblies (held via displacement-linked shared pools), ongoing information exchange through the archive network, and informal social ties maintained by frogs who relocate between settlements.

### Cultural Differences

- **Amazon (Vreth-in-the-Root):** Largest, most politically influential, most spiritually active. Considers itself the cultural center. Strong emphasis on the consciousness-merging mission.
- **Siberian Permafrost Warrens:** Cold-adapted. Culturally pragmatic. Active interstellar exploration program. Considers the Amazon frogs' spiritual intensity excessive. "They croak about oneness. We travel to other stars. Draw your own conclusions."
- **Central African Rift Pools:** Oldest continuous archive. Culturally conservative. Strong oral tradition. Considers both the Amazon and Siberian settlements somewhat juvenile.
- **Southeast Asian Canopy Clusters:** Small, experimentally inclined. Pioneered several organic computation innovations. Considered eccentric by other settlements. Maintain the least rigid social structures.
- **Northern European Bog Networks:** Seasonally active. Culturally introspective. Produce disproportionately many poets and fermentation specialists. Known for a melancholic temperament that other settlements find either charming or unbearable depending on the season.

The settlements disagree about many things. They cooperate on Prunings. They gossip about each other constantly.

## Daily Life

### Food

Insects (cultivated and wild), algae preparations, fungal cultures, and fermented preparations of all three. Fermentation is both a food preservation method and a cultural obsession. Different fermentation techniques, durations, and ingredient combinations are debated with the passion humans reserve for wine, politics, or religion. Oona's fermented grub-and-algae paste is locally renowned. Krrik considers all fermented food equally disgusting and subsists primarily on fresh insects, which he considers a principled dietary position.

### Housing

Organic warrens. Communal sleeping is the default -- clusters of frogs in shared chambers, body heat pooled, ambient sound of breathing and water. Individual nesting spaces exist and are not stigmatized but are considered mildly unusual. "Sleeping alone is not shameful," Oona explains. "It is simply strange, like eating without tasting."

### Childcare

Communal. Tadpoles are raised in shared nurseries. All adults contribute. Biological parents maintain bonds but do not have primary responsibility. The nurseries are staffed by the most patient members of the community, who are compensated with nothing but social respect and priority pool access, which turns out to be more effective than currency.

### Work

No formal employment. Clear specializations, loose expectations, and the general assumption that everyone contributes according to ability. In practice, some frogs work significantly harder than others, and everyone knows who they are. The frogs who do less are not punished. They are simply discussed.

### Leisure

- **Debate:** Recreational argument is perhaps the Threnn's most popular pastime. Topics range from fermentation technique to cosmological philosophy to whether the Siberians are actually as humorless as they seem.
- **Pool-soaking:** Extended communal bathing in mineral pools. Social, restorative, and occasionally the setting for low-grade consciousness-linking that the participants may or may not acknowledge as spiritual practice.
- **Fermentation hobbyism:** Experimental fermentation of novel substrates. Competitive. Results range from delicious to hazardous.
- **Competitive vibration games:** Teams produce complex vibrational patterns in water channels. Other teams attempt to decode, replicate, or counter them. Rules are elaborate and disputed. Oona follows two teams and has opinions.
- **Singing:** Recreational singing, separate from archival or ritual functions. Community song circles. The acoustic properties of the settlement make group singing a literally immersive experience -- vibrations travel through water, stone, and bodies simultaneously.

### Waste

Open-air defecation in moss trenches at the settlement's periphery. The moss is cultivated specifically for waste absorption and is replaced regularly. The system is functional, low-maintenance, and deeply unimpressive. A civilization that has visited other stars has not developed indoor plumbing. The frogs do not consider this a contradiction. They consider indoor plumbing an odd human fixation on controlling biology rather than coexisting with it. Hart considers this an epidemiological nightmare. She is not entirely wrong.

## Sensory Identity

### Visual

Dim. Bioluminescent. Blue-green light shifting with air currents. Organic surfaces that are never quite dry, never quite still. Faint glow from voss substrate in walls and floors. Shadows that breathe. The settlement exists in permanent twilight -- not dark, not bright, but luminous in a way that makes human eyes adjust slowly and never completely.

### Auditory

Water. Always water -- flowing, dripping, pooling, channeling. Low harmonic vibrations that travel through stone and liquid, felt in the body as much as heard. The clicks and croaks of Threnn conversation, layered and overlapping. Occasional bursts of song. The settlement sounds like a living instrument being played very quietly.

### Olfactory

Fermentation. Mineral water. Living moss. Wet stone. Mucus (not unpleasant -- closer to the smell of clean rain on warm earth). Spores. Ozone near Krrik's laboratory. The faint copper-and-herb scent of Threnn chemical signaling. Underneath everything, the warm, organic smell of voss -- like bread rising in a greenhouse.

### Tactile

Damp. Every surface carries a film of moisture. Warm -- the voss substrate and geothermal activity keep the settlement at a constant comfortable temperature for amphibians (cool for humans). Textures are organic: smooth stone worn by water, rough root surfaces, spongy moss, the faint tackiness of fungal coatings. Nothing is sterile. Everything is alive or recently alive.

### The Composite Impression

A place that feels ancient, grown, communal, alive, and slightly unclean. Simultaneously temple, fungus garden, laboratory, and village. Beautiful in a way that is inseparable from its strangeness. Unsettling in a way that is inseparable from its beauty. A human visitor would feel, simultaneously, that they had entered a cathedral and a compost heap. The frogs would not understand why this is a contradiction.

## Major Sources of Tension

1. **The Pruning and its political implications:** Is it purely a survival necessity, or is it used to enforce ideological conformity?
2. **The consciousness-merging mission:** Sublime aspiration or collective self-deception? And is participation truly voluntary?
3. **Hidden power structures:** The gap between the Threnn's egalitarian ideals and their practical governance.
4. **Short lives, long civilization:** The tension between individual experience and generational continuity.
5. **The human discovery:** Five biologists who now know too much, in a civilization that considers knowledge a form of contamination.
6. **Talla's dissidence:** A critic whose rebellion may be necessary for the civilization's health or destructive to its survival, depending on timing.
7. **The severity of the current Pruning:** For the first time in living memory, failure is a genuine possibility.

## World Rules That Cannot Be Broken

1. The primitive-impossible contrast is permanent and universal. The frogs never become uniformly advanced.
2. Voss is casual, mundane infrastructure. It is never treated as sacred or dramatic by the frogs.
3. Frog lifespans cap at roughly 35 years. This shapes everything and cannot be handwaved.
4. The frogs are physically small. Their intellectual and technological power never changes their physical scale.
5. Secrecy is non-negotiable. Humans who discover the Threnn do not leave.
6. The Pruning is real and necessary. It is not a fabrication or conspiracy, whatever Talla suspects about its political uses.
7. The consciousness-merging rituals are both spiritual practice and functional technology. Neither aspect can be cleanly separated.
8. The frogs do not need the humans. The humans' contribution is minor and specific.

## Hidden Truths and Mysteries

- **What voss actually is:** Not fully understood even by the frogs. Its origin, its relationship to cosmic radiation, and its apparent semi-consciousness are open questions.
- **Whether full consciousness-merging is possible:** 200 million years of trying. Never fully achieved. Is it an asymptotic goal, a necessary fiction, or genuinely attainable?
- **What happened to the previous humans:** The archive records them. Biological imprints exist. But the details of how they lived and died among the Threnn are not fully disclosed. Pem knows. Pem is careful about what he shares.
- **The true limits of quantum displacement:** Krrik knows things about what displacement can and cannot do that he does not discuss publicly. Some limitations are political, not physical.
- **Whether the Siberians are actually exploring or escaping:** Their interstellar program may have motivations beyond curiosity.
- **The origin of Threnn intelligence:** The evolutionary divergence that produced sapient amphibians 200 million years ago is not fully explained in the archive. The earliest records are fragmentary. Something happened. The frogs are not sure what.
