# User Feedback

## 2026-03-20: Cross-Dialogue and Group Interaction

The author emphasized that the book must not feel like a series of one-on-one interviews. Key direction:

- Conversations happen with 3, 4, 5+ participants — humans and frogs mixing freely
- The pairings are gravitational tendencies, not rigid assignments
- Characters are multidimensional and must interact meaningfully with characters outside their primary pairing
- Frogs talk to each other in front of humans; humans talk to each other in front of frogs
- Group scenes are where the book's energy lives
- Background characters (unnamed frogs) must populate scenes to make the settlement feel alive
- Simulate normal day-to-day conversation: people wander in and out, topics shift, someone arrives with news
- The world is alive and should feel like real social life, not staged encounters

This is a hard constraint. Every chapter should be checked against it.
