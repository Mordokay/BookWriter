## Compliance Summary

Chapter 2 is strong. It follows the brief closely, maintains continuity with Chapter 1, respects the world bible and character canon, and complies with all hard constraints. ## Issues Found

### Minor Issues

1. **Noll's title and role not signaled.** The brief specifies that Noll is "High Speaker -- Temporary Coordinator of Distributed Harmonies for the Marsh Federations." The chapter introduces him only as "Noll, who was old, and grey-green." This is acceptable for a first appearance -- overloading his title here would violate constraint 17 (no exposition dumps) -- but future chapters must establish his role. No fix needed here; flagging for downstream awareness.

2. **The Pools vs. the monitoring station.** The brief calls for two frog scenes: (a) a monitoring team reports to Noll at a monitoring station, then (b) a Pools discussion with several frogs present. The chapter merges these into a single scene set in "a chamber lined with living root." The monitoring report and the Noll/Talla debate happen in the same location. This is a structural simplification of the brief, not a contradiction. The effect still works -- the reader gets the surveillance report, the Noll/Talla debate, and the reference to escalation to the Pools ("The matter would go to the Pools," line 143). However, the brief's intent was to show both the monitoring station AND the Pools as distinct locations. The merged version means the Pools deliberation scene described in the brief (half-submerged frogs, warm water, multiple voices) was compressed into the single chamber scene. This is a minor deviation. The scene still lands. No revision required, but note that the Pools as a location have been referenced but not yet depicted. Future chapters should show the Pools directly.

3. **Number of frogs in the deliberation.** Constraint 15 requires scenes with 3+ characters. The frog section has Noll, Talla, three monitors, and one additional small monitor who asks a question. This satisfies the constraint. However, the brief asked for "several frogs present" at the Pools discussion and an "unnamed monitor" who "insists they are too close." The chapter has the monitors report the humans' position (satisfying the surveillance function) and one small monitor ask a question/objection (line 135), but nobody specifically "insists they are too close" -- that information is embedded in the report itself. This is a minor compression, not a violation.

4. **Marsh's stone reference.** The chapter handles Marsh's stone well -- she touches something in her trouser pocket twice during dinner (lines 173-174) without revealing it. This is consistent with Ch1 where she pocketed the stone silently. However, the text says "her trouser pocket" without specifying which pocket. In Ch1, she simply "put it in her pocket" (line 207). Consistency maintained; no issue.

5. **Chapter 1 established that three people heard the harmonic (Marsh, Quill, Hart). Reed dismissed it as tinnitus.** In Chapter 2, all five hear the harmonic clearly at the climax (line 193: "The five of them stood in the warm clearing"). This is the intended escalation per the brief. However, during the walk, only Hart explicitly confirms hearing the sound (line 101: "Three people... The sound is real. I hear it right now"), and Quill hears it in the narration (line 103). Reed and Vale do not confirm hearing it during the walk, which is consistent -- they hear it unmistakably only when it intensifies at dusk. Good escalation. No issue.

6. **Depth of the underground chamber.** The chapter states the frog chamber is "seven meters underground" (line 119). The world bible says the coring rig is rated for eight meters, and Reed wanted to core to twelve or fifteen. Placing the frog monitoring station at seven meters creates a nice implicit tension: if Reed had successfully modified the coring rig in Ch1, he might have punched into frog infrastructure. This is not contradictory -- it is good setup. Flagging it as an established fact future chapters must respect.

### No Issues Found With

- Character names, ages, nationalities: all consistent with characters.md.
- Physical descriptions: Noll's muted green-grey coloring, damaged forelimb held slightly inward, and larger-than-average size are all consistent. Talla's dark (almost black-green) coloring, smaller size (4.5 cm implied by "smaller and darker than the others"), and pale spots across her back that flare white when she speaks are all consistent with characters.md.
- Communication methods: The frog section describes clicks, croaks, harmonics, color shifts, chemical signals in the water, and vibration through the water -- all four channels of Threnn-song per the world bible, without ever using the term "Threnn-song."
- Warm soil temperature: consistent with Ch1 (elevated by two degrees) and escalating appropriately.
- Copper-and-herb smell: consistent with the world bible's description of Threnn chemical signaling ("smells like wet copper and crushed herbs").
- Camp equipment and setup: water filtration unit, field kits, tarp, tents -- all consistent with Ch1's established camp inventory.
- The secondary anomaly site / new camp: the team has moved southeast from the original camp, following Reed's data. The new clearing is distinct from the Ch1 camp location. Consistent.

## Facts Established (New Canon for Future Chapters)

These facts are introduced in Chapter 2 and must be respected going forward:

1. **The team has moved southeast** from the original camp, walking approximately two hours deeper into the forest following Reed's anomalous root density readings.
2. **Copper concentrations in the soil** are roughly 8x the upper threshold for tropical hardwoods, plus an unidentified organic compound Hart's field kit cannot recognize.
3. **The soil is warm enough to feel through boot soles** (Hart's description, line 43).
4. **The copper-and-herb smell is intensifying** as the team moves deeper. By the new camp, it is "pervasive, almost pleasant" (line 175).
5. **Root structures terminate abruptly** at the clearing where they made the second camp, as if something underground prevents their growth. The termination is not tapered -- it stops sharply (lines 156-158).
6. **The new camp is in a rough circle of open ground** where the trees have pulled back. The soil is exposed, reddish-brown, and warm to the touch.
7. **Quill publicly proposed** that the underground phenomena could be biological and organized. Marsh supported the hypothesis by citing the Oregon honey fungus as precedent. The group did not dismiss it.
8. **Hart confirmed hearing the harmonic** (line 101), making three confirmed listeners during the walk (Marsh, Quill, Hart) and all five at the dusk event.
9. **The frog monitoring station** is located approximately seven meters underground, in a chamber lined with living root, lit by bioluminescent growth, with a basin of mineral water used for communication.
10. **Noll received the monitoring report:** five humans, moving southeast, past the third marker, carrying instruments.
11. **Noll decided to escalate to the Pools** rather than act immediately. His manner is calm, administrative, procedurally cautious.
12. **Talla proposed immediate countermeasures:** chemical disorientation, path rearrangement, accelerated outer growth to close the humans' route. She was overruled.
13. **The "preparations" (Pruning) were referenced** as context for why the breach matters -- the timing is bad. The word "Pruning" was not used in the frog section (correct per brief).
14. **The harmonic became undeniable at dusk.** All five biologists heard it clearly. The sound is sustained, complex, layered, shifting in pitch, and coming from underground. It did not fade. It continued through the chapter's end.
15. **Marsh touched the stone in her trouser pocket twice** during dinner but did not reveal it to anyone.
16. **The root structures along the walk** are described as "braided, interlocked, running in patterns that did not look like growth. They looked like infrastructure" (line 111). This extends the "engineered" root observation from Ch1.
17. **The insects went quiet** when the harmonic intensified (line 193). Environmental response to the harmonic is now established.
18. **Reed's plan to reach "the secondary anomaly site"** was stated (line 105) -- this is the destination the team was walking toward, and the clearing where they camped may or may not be that site. Future chapters should clarify.
19. **Talla's emotional tells are established:** her pale spots flare when she speaks with intensity (line 137: "Pale spots across her back that flared white as she spoke") and dim with visible effort when she suppresses emotion (line 145). Her chemical signals (crushed herbs sharpening) broadcast frustration she cannot fully suppress.
20. **Noll's formal register is established:** his skin shifts to "steady muted blue" for formal speech (line 131: "The color of formality"). His damaged forelimb shifts almost imperceptibly during responses (line 142).
