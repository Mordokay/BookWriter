## Issues

### Issue 1
- **Severity:** MODERATE
- **Location:** Line 41 -- "waist-deep in the current"
- **Problem:** The ch04 brief and Ch. 3 establish the stream as "knee-deep" (ch04 brief scene 2: "The stream is knee-deep"). World.md describes "a fast, narrow stream at the bottom of a ravine between Zones 2 and 3" without specifying depth precisely. In the chapter prose, the water rises from knee-deep at the edges (line 37: "The water rose above her knees") to "waist-deep" at the center (line 41). The brief says "knee-deep" without qualifying edge-vs-center. The chapter's interpretation -- knee-deep at edges, deeper at center with a carved trough -- is physically plausible for a fast mountain stream. However, "waist-deep" is notably deeper than the brief's "knee-deep" specification, and Ada being waist-deep while crossing creates a discrepancy with the brief.
- **Why it matters:** The brief is the approved structural plan for this chapter. The deviation is within the range of reasonable creative elaboration (streams are deeper at center), but it should be acknowledged. The deeper water does serve the narrative: it makes the fall and submersion more plausible and more dramatic.
- **Suggested fix:** No mandatory change. The depth variation (shallower at edges, deeper at center) is naturalistic and serves the fall scene. If strict brief adherence is preferred, the center depth could be reduced to "thigh-deep" and the fall could occur from a shifting stone rather than depth, which would still allow submersion given the current's force. However, the current version is defensible.

### Issue 2
- **Severity:** MODERATE
- **Location:** Line 121 -- "The fists opened -- not the involuntary opening of the swarm, but a deliberate extension"
- **Problem:** Per running-state.md, Ada's hand default at Ch. 3 close is fists. The ch04 brief specifies that in processing, Ada "examines her arms" and the interior voice "tries to reassemble." The brief does not mention a deliberate hand-opening attempt. This is not a contradiction -- the brief does not prohibit it -- but the addition of a deliberate fist-opening attempt is a new motif beat not specified in the planning documents. The beat is well-written and serves the motif arc (showing that involuntary opening [swarm] differs from deliberate testing [Ch. 4], with the right hand's pain forcing a return to fist). The concern is that it slightly complicates the clean motif trajectory: Ch. 1 (involuntary fist-closing), Ch. 2 (involuntary opening), Ch. 3 (no opening), Ch. 4 (deliberate opening attempted, fails). The "deliberate" quality risks muddying the motif's arc, which has been operating at the involuntary/body-knowledge level.
- **Why it matters:** The fist/open-hand motif is one of the story's key tracking devices. Adding a deliberate attempt here introduces a cognitive element (Ada choosing to open her hands) into what has been a purely somatic motif. The beat works within this chapter, but future chapters should not treat this as a precedent for Ada consciously managing the motif.
- **Suggested fix:** No mandatory change. The beat is well-integrated and the physical pain forcing the hand back to fist preserves the body-over-mind dynamic. However, if the motif's purity is a priority, the deliberate opening could be rephrased as semi-automatic: "The fists loosened -- not the involuntary opening of the swarm, but the tentative easing of a hand testing whether it still needed to grip. The right hand's web of spines answered with a sharp bolt, and the fingers curled back without her permission." This keeps the motion in the body's register rather than Ada's conscious choice.

### Issue 4
- **Severity:** MINOR
- **Location:** Line 103-105 -- Pulsing shelf fungi on standing dead trees
- **Problem:** The fungi are described as "shelf fungi" glowing and pulsing at heartbeat rhythm. Per world.md, the Zone 3 bioluminescence description says: "Mushroom clusters on standing trees pulse in slow rhythms (approximately matching a resting heartbeat)." The world doc does not specify these as shelf fungi -- it is a general description. The chapter's Continuity Notes identify them as "pulsing shelf fungi" but do not assign a species. Per world.md's Flora notes, the bioluminescent species cataloged are: Omphalotus olearius (Zone 1), Mycena chlorophos (Zone 2+), Panellus stipticus (dead hardwood, faint greenish glow). Panellus stipticus is a shelf fungus (bracket-like growth on hardwood) and is bioluminescent, making it the most likely candidate for the pulsing shelf fungi described. However, the pulsing behavior is not documented for any real bioluminescent fungus -- in nature, bioluminescent fungi emit a steady glow, not a rhythmic pulse. Per world.md's psychedelic layer: psilocybin amplifies pattern recognition and creates rhythmic perception. The pulsing could be Ada's psilocybin-amplified perception imposing rhythm on a steady glow. The chapter supports this reading (Ada "counted without deciding to count" -- suggesting the pattern-recognition amplification).
- **Why it matters:** Minor world-building precision. The pulsing is defensible as psilocybin perception, which is how world.md frames it ("pulse in slow rhythms" appears in the Zone 3 description, implying the pulse is part of how Ada perceives the zone). No contradiction, but worth noting for downstream consistency: the pulsing should intensify or synchronize further in later chapters rather than being repeated at the same intensity.
- **Suggested fix:** No change needed. The pulsing is correctly framed as perceived rhythm under psilocybin, matching world.md's Zone 3 description.
