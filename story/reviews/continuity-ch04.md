## 2. Issues Found

### Issue 1: Krrik's Physical Description Missing (MINOR)

**Canon:** Mottled brown-green, perpetually dusty with spore residue, one shorter hind leg, eyes always focused on something other than whoever is talking.

**Chapter 4:** Krrik appears at lines 141-159 but receives no physical description. He is characterized entirely through dialogue and the narrator noting his expression.

**Impact:** Low. This is his first significant appearance, and the brief explicitly lists his physical details as canon to preserve. At minimum, his distinctive mottled coloring and spore-dusted appearance should be established on first introduction.

**Recommendation:** Add 1-2 physical details when Krrik first appears (line 141). Suggested insertion: his mottled brown-green coloring, the spore residue, and ideally the shorter hind leg or the eyes-focused-elsewhere detail. These are his most distinctive markers.

### Issue 2: Brief Specifies Krrik "Grudgingly Allows Reed to Touch Things" -- Chapter Has Krrik Refusing (DEVIATION)

**Brief (Scene 3 and Characters Present):** "Grudgingly allows Reed to touch things" and "Appears at the laboratory entrance looking annoyed at the disruption. Grudgingly allows Reed to touch things."

**Chapter 4:** Krrik flatly refuses to let Reed touch the moss bed (lines 143-151). Reed pulls his hand back. He does not touch anything.

**Impact:** Low-medium. The chapter's version is arguably stronger -- Krrik's refusal is well-motivated (contamination, thermal disruption, ongoing computation), characterful, and consistent with Krrik's canon personality ("impatient," "annoyed that everyone keeps turning engineering into religion"). The refusal creates a more dramatic scene than grudging permission would. However, it is a deliberate deviation from the brief.

**Recommendation:** ACCEPT the deviation. The refusal is better writing and better characterization. Flag for the chapter planner that the brief was overridden, but do not revise.

### Issue 3: The Pools Are Not Visited (DEVIATION)

**Brief (Scene Sequence item 4):** "The Pools and residential warrens. Brief glimpses of domestic life. At the Pools, some frogs react to the humans with fascinated curiosity; others pointedly ignore them."

**Chapter 4:** The Pools (communal bathing/social spaces) are not visited as a distinct location. The residential warrens are visited. The Speaking Chambers are visited. Krrik's lab entrance is visited. But the Pools as described in the brief and world.md are absent.

**Impact:** Low. The chapter already covers four distinct locations and is near its target word count. The domestic-reaction details the brief wanted from the Pools (fascinated curiosity, pointed disinterest) are delivered instead through the residential warrens scene (line 99: "One of them turned a vivid, fascinated yellow. The other two pointedly did not look."). The function is fulfilled; the specific location is different.

**Recommendation:** ACCEPT. The chapter successfully delivers the intended texture without the Pools. The Pools can be introduced in a later chapter.

### Issue 4: Quill's Question Differs from Brief (MINOR DEVIATION)

**Brief (Scene 6):** "Quill asks whether they ever considered helping" (in the 300,000-year revelation cascade).

**Chapter 4:** Quill says nothing during the 300,000-year revelation (line 273: "Quill said nothing. His hands opened and closed at his sides."). His single speaking moment is the earlier consensus exchange with Vale (lines 215-231).

**Impact:** Low. The chapter's choice -- Quill silent during the biggest revelation, hands opening and closing, no words -- is more powerful and more consistent with his established near-silence arc. The brief had him ask "whether they ever considered helping," which would have been a strong line, but the silence is arguably better for his character trajectory (total silence in ch03, one question in ch04, building toward full engagement).

**Recommendation:** ACCEPT the deviation. Quill's silence during the revelation is the stronger choice.

### Issue 5: Five-Year Cycles vs. Eleven Years (ARITHMETIC)

**Chapter 4, line 121-123:** Noll says the coordination cycle is five years and he is in his third cycle. Vale responds: "Eleven years. Three cycles."

**Arithmetic:** Three five-year cycles = 15 years. But ch03 already established Noll has been coordinating for 11 years (line 203 of ch03: "I have been coordinating for this settlement for eleven years"). The 11 years is canon from ch03.

**Analysis:** This is NOT an error. Noll says he is "in" his third cycle, meaning partway through it. If the first two cycles were complete (10 years) and he is one year into his third cycle, the total is 11 years. Vale's response of "Eleven years. Three cycles" correctly acknowledges this -- he is counting total elapsed time, not total completed cycles. The dialogue is internally consistent: two completed cycles (10 years) plus one year into the third = 11 years total.

**Status:** PASS -- the arithmetic works on close reading.

### Issue 6: "An Epidemiological" -- Grammar (TRIVIAL)

**Chapter 4, line 269:** Hart says "is a epidemiological" -- this sentence appears to be cut off, which is intentional (she is interrupted by the cascade of reactions), but the article should be "an," not "a."

**Analysis:** The text reads "is a epidemiological." The "a" before a vowel sound should be "an." However, looking more carefully, the line reads "is a epidemiological" which appears to be "is an epidemiological" with a missing "n."

**Wait -- re-reading:** The actual text is "is a epidemiological." This should be "is an epidemiological."

**Recommendation:** Fix "a epidemiological" to "an epidemiological" on line 269.

## 4. Facts Established in Chapter 4

These are new canonical facts introduced in this chapter that must be tracked going forward:

1. **Settlement formally named:** Vreth-in-the-Root (first time humans hear the name).
2. **Noll's full title confirmed in dialogue:** Temporary Coordinator of Distributed Harmonies for the Marsh Federations.
3. **Coordination cycle length:** Five years per cycle. Noll is in his third cycle. Total elapsed: 11 years (consistent with ch03).
4. **Noll's framing of his tenure:** "The general feeling has persisted." He frames continuation as community consensus, not personal ambition.
5. **Vreth-in-the-Root age:** Approximately 14,000 years.
6. **Global population:** 4-6 million Threnn across several dozen settlements worldwide.
7. **Inter-settlement connection:** "Connected by displacement" (quantum displacement, stated without the "quantum" prefix).
8. **Civilization age:** Approximately 200 million years. They predate flowering plants.
9. **Awareness of humanity:** Approximately 300,000 years.
10. **Noll's reason for non-contact:** "Discovery is a form of entanglement. We preferred to remain disentangled. Until, of course, you made that impossible."
11. **Speaking Chambers:** Amphitheater carved from living root, concentric rings, central basin of dark water. Approximately 40 frogs observed in a resource allocation session for the western perimeter.
12. **Oona Reedmoss attached to the tour uninvited.** Nobody objected. Noll absorbed her presence without acknowledging it.
13. **Oona's domestic critiques:** Third Pool district has unresolved drainage problems. Sleeping chamber allocation produces outcomes nobody is satisfied with, adjusted informally. She defines "harmony" sarcastically.
14. **Sanitation system:** Open biological waste processing trenches in residential corridors. Living organic material processes waste. Noll calls it "our relationship with biology." Hart calls it "a disease vector."
15. **Third Pool district:** Referenced by Oona as having worse sanitation trenches, an absent maintenance coordinator, and drainage problems. (Talla's home district per canon -- connection not yet made explicit to the humans.)
16. **Krrik's moss computing claim:** The moss bed "contains more processing power than every computer your species has ever built." It is alive, currently solving a calibration sequence, requires precise hydration, and is vulnerable to biological contaminants and thermal disruption from humans.
17. **Krrik referenced "the last human who was here."** Noll immediately deflected. Topic deferred. This is a foreshadowing seed for the absorbed-humans revelation.
18. **Reed attempted to touch Krrik's moss bed and was refused.** Krrik's refusal was motivated by contamination and thermal disruption concerns.
19. **Vale's working theory:** No system distributes power without concentrating it somewhere. The question is whether the hierarchy admits it exists. He considers Noll "a mandate wearing a modest hat."
20. **Noll acknowledged managing the information sequence:** "You are correct that I am managing the sequence in which you receive information. I would hope this is understood as courtesy rather than concealment."
21. **Quill's one exchange:** Asked Vale whether distributed consensus could actually work. Vale said no. Noll listened but chose not to respond.
22. **Marsh gave Reed a page from her notebook** when he had nothing to write on.
23. **Small uniform objects in water channels:** A frog in a water channel carefully arranged pale, rice-grain-sized objects into the flow. Deliveries. (Continues the detail from ch03.)
24. **Oona offered the fermentation cellars** as the next destination at chapter's end.
25. **Vale's height:** Six foot one. (First explicit measurement.)
