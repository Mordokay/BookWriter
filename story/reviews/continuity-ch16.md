## Issues Found

### Critical

1. **"Ten days ago" timeline mismatch (line 17).**
   Ch 16 says the Siberian lead is "the same scarred female who had disrupted the planning session ten days ago." Ch 16 takes place on Day 24 (per continuity notes and timeline). The Siberian delegation first arrived on Day 8-9 (Ch 6), which is 15-16 days before Day 24. The full Siberian delegation arrived on Day 17-18 (Ch 12), which is 6-7 days before Day 24. Neither matches "ten days ago." If the reference is to the Ch 6 planning session disruption, it should read "fifteen" or "sixteen days ago." If it refers to a session during the full delegation's arrival in Ch 12, it should read "six" or "seven days ago." The most likely intended reference is the Ch 6 Speaking Chambers session (the first time the Siberians disrupted a planning session), which would require changing "ten" to approximately "sixteen."

   **Recommended fix:** Change "ten days ago" to "sixteen days ago" (referencing the Day 8-9 arrival in Ch 6), or "six days ago" (referencing the Day 17-18 full delegation arrival in Ch 12). The Ch 6 reference is the stronger match because that chapter explicitly describes the Siberian leader disrupting a planning session with blunt data presentation, and Reed was present for it.

### Minor

2. **"Sixty million years" of Siberian displacement (line 27).**
   The Siberian lead says "We coordinate displacement technically, without the synchronization requirement. We have done so for sixty million years." Timeline.md places quantum displacement development at "~60-40 Million Years Ago." This means the technology is between 40 and 60 million years old, and the Siberian claim of "sixty million years" is at the absolute upper bound. Given that (a) the technology was developed across a span and the Siberians would not necessarily have had it from the start, and (b) the Siberians pioneered interstellar displacement 30-20 MYA, suggesting the base technology was well-established before that, the claim is plausible as a round-number boast. However, it would be more conservative and internally consistent to say "forty million years" or "fifty million years."

   **Recommended action:** No prose change required. This is within the tolerance of a frog making a rhetorical point in a political setting. The Siberian lead would round up, not down. Flag for awareness only.

3. **"Eighty million years" rivalry figure (line 113 and continuity notes).**
   Ch 16 references the Amazon-Siberian rivalry as "eighty million years" old. World.md says "roughly 80 million years." However, timeline.md places the start of the rivalry at ~50 million years ago (the quantum displacement era, ~60-40 MYA: "now approximately 50 million years old with no resolution in sight"). Ch 6 (via Oona) also says "about eighty million years." This is a pre-existing discrepancy between world.md and timeline.md, not introduced by Ch 16. Ch 16 is consistent with world.md and Ch 6.

   **Recommended action:** No Ch 16 change. The discrepancy lives in timeline.md vs. world.md and should be reconciled at the world-file level. World.md's "80 million years" is the figure used in all prose and should be treated as canon.

4. **Timeline.md Day 24 entry describes wrong chapter content.**
   Timeline.md's entry for "Day 24 (Ch. 16)" reads: "Marsh notices increased mourning rituals for elderly frogs dying under Pruning preparation stress. She visits Pem to ask about historical Pruning mortality..." This describes what is now Ch 21 content (per the chapter outline). The actual Ch 16 content -- the packed inter-settlement coordination meeting with Siberians -- is described under Day 17-18 (Ch 12) in timeline.md. This is a pre-existing timeline.md synchronization issue, not a Ch 16 prose error.

   **Recommended action:** Update timeline.md so Day 24 (Ch 16) reflects the actual chapter content: the packed Speaking Chamber meeting, Siberian-Amazon cultural clash, Talla's partial Siberian alignment, Reed's surface data gaining strategic importance, confirmation of worst Pruning in centuries.

## New Facts Established in Ch 16

1. **Largest Speaking Chamber** can hold two hundred frogs in tiered mineral pools. Reed counts 63+ attendees at this session.

2. **Siberian chief engineer** introduced: smaller than other Siberians, mottled grey with dark blue flecks at joints. Focused, impatient. An engineer-type Reed recognizes. Grudgingly acknowledges Reed's surface data.

3. **Surface-level voss emergence** has never been recorded in approximately 150 million years of monitoring data. Reed's surface observations are the only documentation of this phenomenon. This is the chapter's most significant scientific escalation.

4. **Pruning severity data by region:** Siberian monitoring network confirms 19% divergence from circulated projections (global). Amazon eastern sectors: 23% above projections in last six days (Krrik). Siberian northern quadrant: 17% (Siberian engineer). Rift Pools: 21% (Siberian engineer). Canopy Clusters: data not reported (chronic tardiness attributed to spiritual beliefs about timely reporting; a 40-million-year-old complaint).

5. **Siberians coordinate displacement technically without consciousness-merging.** Their accuracy exceeds the Amazon approach (exact factor withheld by the Siberian lead to avoid humiliation). This is the first explicit confirmation that the spiritual framework is operationally optional.

6. **Siberians brought additional computation arrays** currently being cultivated in transit pools; operational in two days.

7. **Collective chromatic fear display:** 60+ frogs from both settlements shift to the same pale washed green for 3-4 seconds. The hum resolves to a single frequency. Even the Siberians show a faint flush at throats and forelimbs. This is the first time a collective involuntary emotional display has been depicted at this scale.

8. **Talla publicly sides (partially) with the Siberians** against the Amazon consensus on mandatory consciousness-merging. Politically significant: the chamber goes actually quiet. The Siberian lead reorients toward Talla with fractional respect (Siberian body language).

9. **Vesh invokes the Almost** in his spiritual rebuttal: "The Almost is what it is." The merging is framed as purpose, not tool. The eating-without-tasting analogy is his central metaphor.

10. **Vesh-Talla tension** is visible to Quill: they sit three rows apart and do not look at each other. History implied, not explained. (Consistent with character notes: "the splinter in the harmony.")

11. **Olive-green background frog** interjects about Canopy Clusters' 40-million-year chronic reporting tardiness being a "spiritual rigidity" objection. (Background frog, alive world.)

12. **Noll uses Talla's full formal name** ("Talla-of-the-Third-Pool") in the coordination record, a procedural acknowledgment that functions as soft containment.

13. **Reed's data presentation method:** Transcribed onto dried moss sheet from Krrik, because human electronics do not function in settlement humidity. Krrik's attitude toward electronic data storage: "touching, in the way a child's drawing is touching."

14. **Ending dynamic:** Krrik and the Siberian engineer arguing productively at the thinking moss after the chamber empties. Reed walks toward them with his data. Science proceeds despite unresolved politics.
