## Issues Found

### Critical

**1. Timeline.md is stale and contradicts the actual chapter sequence.**
Timeline.md (lines 204-205) places the Pruning displacement operations at Day 27-28 and assigns them to Ch 18. In the actual manuscript, Ch 18 ("Voluntary Participation") is a political chapter about mandatory breathing circles, and the major displacement operations are depicted in Ch 23 at Day 35-36. The timeline.md was written for an earlier chapter plan and was never updated to reflect the expanded 27-chapter structure. **This is not a Ch 23 error -- it is a stale reference document.** Recommend updating timeline.md to reflect the actual chapter sequence.

**2. Talla's Ch 22 reference to "seventeen operator casualties across two displacement waves" implies prior displacement operations occurred off-page between Ch 19 and Ch 22.** Ch 23 depicts what reads as a fresh, large-scale displacement event ("The displacement began" on line 63), but Talla already cited seventeen casualties from two previous waves. This is internally consistent IF Ch 23 is understood as a later, escalated wave (the third or beyond), not the first. However, Ch 23's prose never explicitly acknowledges the prior waves. The line "the staging grounds had been an open space the last time Reed saw them" (line 3) implies Reed has not seen the staging grounds configured for displacement before, which creates mild tension with the fact that prior displacement waves already occurred. **Recommendation:** Consider adding a brief reference (one clause) to the prior waves, e.g., acknowledging that the previous operations were smaller-scale or that Reed was not present for them. Alternatively, accept as Reed's POV -- he may not have visited the staging grounds during the earlier, smaller waves.

### Minor

**3. Vesh described as "emerald green" (line 103) but gold flecks not mentioned.** Character sheet specifies "Vivid emerald green with gold flecks that intensify during spiritual speech." Ch 20 (line 79) showed the gold flecks dimming during a crisis moment. In Ch 23, Vesh is singing among operators -- a spiritual/functional act where gold flecks should arguably be present. Not a contradiction (Reed may not be close enough to see them, or they may be dimmed by exhaustion), but a missed opportunity for a visual callback.

**4. Staging grounds location: Ch 19 (line 145) places them at "the settlement's western perimeter." Ch 23 does not specify the staging grounds' location within the settlement.** No contradiction, but the western-perimeter detail from Ch 19 is not reinforced. Trivial.

**5. "Seventh reformulation" (line 73): Ch 19's continuity notes (line 215) confirm "seven reformulations shown in sequence." The reference to "the seventh reformulation" in Ch 23 is consistent, but Ch 19's prose shows the reformulations degrading (12 days, then 3 days, then 41 hours). Ch 23 says the seventh "held" during the displacement. This implies the seventh reformulation is the same one described in Ch 19, now deployed alongside the displacement. Consistent, but the reader may wonder whether Krrik reformulated further between Ch 19 and Ch 23.** A minor logical question, not a contradiction. The compounds were "reformulated overnight" (line 127), which could be read as an eighth reformulation or as a refinement of the seventh. Consider clarifying.
