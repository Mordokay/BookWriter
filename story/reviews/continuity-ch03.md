## Issues Found

### 1. Delegation Count Discrepancy (Minor -- Brief vs. Chapter)

**Brief states:** "Eight or nine small, calm, impossibly articulate creatures" in the delegation. The brief's character list also specifies "6-7 delegation frogs: Behind Noll, visible, some curious, some disapproving."

**Chapter has:** 7 frogs total in the delegation -- Noll (1) + 2 flanking coordinators + 4 behind them.

**Assessment:** The brief suggested 8-9 total with 6-7 behind Noll; the chapter delivers 7 total with 4 behind. This is a minor deviation. The chapter's continuity notes document 7 consistently. The scene works at 7. This does not require revision but should be noted for downstream continuity -- the number 7 is now canon.

### 2. Quill's "Hesitation Framed as a Question" (Minor -- Brief vs. Chapter)

**Brief states:** Under "Key Dialogue Moments," the descent argument should include "Quill: hesitation framed as a question."

**Chapter has:** Quill's hesitation is purely physical -- his mouth opens and closes, his hands open and close at his sides. He does not speak a question.

**Assessment:** This is a deliberate creative choice that strengthens the chapter's commitment to Quill's total silence. The brief also states under "Constraints and Warnings" that "Quill's silence is important. He does not speak." The no-speech interpretation is the stronger read. The chapter's own continuity notes confirm: "Quill did not speak for the entire chapter." This is an acceptable deviation from the brief's dialogue section in favor of the brief's constraint section.

### 3. Noll's Pronoun (Observation, Not Issue)

The prose initially refers to Noll as "it" (lines 127-129, 147) before any gender is established. The pronoun shifts are consistent with Marsh's POV -- she would not know Noll's gender immediately. Noll is later referred to as "he" in the narration (line 203 onward) and in the continuity notes. This is natural and correct for the POV.

**No canon contradictions found. No timeline inconsistencies detected.**

## Facts Established (New Canon from Chapter 3)

These facts are now canon and must be maintained in subsequent chapters:

1. **The entrance:** A fissure at the base of a strangler fig. Seam runs north-south, ~4 meters. Walls smooth and beveled, not naturally eroded.
2. **The passage:** Descends at ~30 degrees. Smooth walls with small parallel grooves at ankle height (frog foot traffic). Water channel along left wall carrying uniform objects downstream.
3. **Air transition:** Surface heat falls away. Cooler, wetter. Fermentation smell (Marsh identified). Copper intensifying (Hart noted). Temperature drops ~10 degrees over ~20 meters.
4. **Bioluminescence:** Fungal networks in walls. Dim blue-green. Shifts with air currents, brightening and dimming in slow pulses. Bright enough to see by (barely) in the junction chamber and deeper.
5. **Junction chamber:** Several passages meet. Ceiling ~4 meters. Water channels converge at central basin. Root walls shaped -- "not quite architecture and not quite horticulture."
6. **Noll's delegation:** 7 frogs. Noll (8 cm, muted green-grey, damaged forelimb, steady blue during speech) + 2 flanking coordinators (shifting brown-grey) + 4 behind (less controlled, flickering curiosity/alarm).
7. **Noll's voice:** Small. Precise consonants. Full vowels. Faintly Victorian. "Colonial filing clerk from 1890." Learned English from monitoring, not conversation.
8. **Noll's key disclosures:** (a) Biologists' movement guided for 3 days via chemical gradients, adjusted root structures, atmospheric changes. (b) Settlement population: ~1,200. Global population: "somewhat larger." (c) Noll's age: 28 frog-years. Coordinating for 11 years. ~4,000 meetings attended.
9. **"Four bricks":** Noll's reference to English being limited -- echoes world.md archivist quote about building a cathedral with four bricks. He catches himself: "Forgive me. That is an internal reference."
10. **English as "architecturally limited":** Noll describes human language as encoding meaning in one channel (sound). Frogs find this "quaint."
11. **Onlooker frogs:** ~30+ residents. Colors include muted greens, browns, rust, pale gold, near-black violet. Conversational color-shifting. Clicks and harmonics traveling through water channels.
12. **Contamination concern is mutual:** Noll notes the biologists are "very large, very warm," breathing out CO2 and foreign microorganisms. Frog physicians are "extremely unhappy."
13. **"Dissolve gently":** First appearance. Said casually by Noll when transitioning to move. Marsh heard it, filed it, did not understand it. Registers as an idiom from an unknown language.
14. **Marsh's stone:** Still in her pocket. Still warm. She touched it at the chapter's end. Nobody else knows about it.
15. **Route back lost:** The turns accumulated. Marsh cannot reconstruct the path to the surface.

## Voice Check

### Noll
Noll's voice is excellent. Measured, courteous, administratively precise, faintly Victorian. Consistent with character sheet. Key markers present: balanced phrases, diplomatic qualifiers ("I regret the discourtesy"), soft deflection ("Perspectives vary more than you might expect"), and the controlled patience of someone who has attended 4,000 meetings. His "guiding vs. herding" distinction is perfectly in-character.

### Reed
Reed's characteristic speech is well-rendered. Pile-up clauses, "wait, wait, wait," mid-sentence derailments, glasses-related business. His eruption at first contact (line 135) is a strong example -- questions breeding questions, no pauses, reaching toward a half-dozen disciplines simultaneously.

### Vale
Vale's clipped, suspicious, politically oriented voice is consistent. Arms-folded posture noted. His immediate demand to know who is running the contact, who authorized it, and what the agenda is matches his character sheet precisely. His recovery from shock faster than anyone else is well-observed and consistent with running-state.

### Hart
Hart's focus on contamination risk, air quality, and practical safety is exactly right. "Nobody touch anything. Nobody breathe through your mouth." Short, flat, practical. The French exclamation (untranslated) is a nice touch consistent with her French-Canadian background.

### Quill
Quill's silence is maintained throughout. Physical details (hands at sides, mouth closed, eyes tracking) convey interior processing without narrating it. The final moment between Quill and Marsh -- "something passed between them that was not a word" -- is effective and restrained. No violations of his character sheet.

### Marsh (POV)
Marsh's POV is consistent: cataloguing details, noticing the lived-in quality, filtering through sensory observation. She notices the small objects in the water channel, the worn grooves, the onlooker frogs' behavior, Quill's silence. Her tiebreaker line ("We're scientists... the question answers itself") is dry, practical, and in-character.
