# Continuity Review -- Chapter 11: Dissolve

## Summary

Chapter 11 is the climax of the book and handles it with remarkable discipline. The dissolution, integration, and emergence sequence tracks Ada's accumulated physical and emotional state with high fidelity. Callbacks to all seven animal encounters are woven into the integration passage without becoming a checklist. The three types of tears (Ch. 7 exhaustion, Ch. 9 grief, Ch. 11 relief) are cleanly distinguished. The timeline, light conditions, and environmental details are almost entirely consistent with canon.

However, there are two critical errors that must be fixed before revision. The frog is misidentified as a "Pacific tree frog" (line 49) when canon consistently establishes it as a Western toad (Anaxyrus boreas). The frog's pupils are described as "vertical" (line 85) when canon consistently specifies "horizontal pupils." Additionally, the frog's body is called "small" in two places (lines 99, 123), contradicting the canon description of "Large -- three to five inches, with a heavy, squat body." These species-level errors would break continuity with every prior frog appearance.

## Issues Found

### Critical (must fix)

1. **Wrong frog species (line 49).** "The croak of a Pacific tree frog in the coldest hours before dawn" -- the frog is a Western toad (Anaxyrus boreas) throughout the canon (world.md, characters.md, running-state.md, chapters 1, 3, 6, 8). A Pacific tree frog (Pseudacris regilla) is a completely different species: tiny (1-2 inches), smooth-skinned, with toe pads. The Western toad is large (3-5 inches), warty, heavy, squat. This line should read something like "The croak of a frog" or describe the sound without naming the species (since Latin/common species names in prose are discouraged by style-guide.md anyway). Alternatively, simply remove the species identifier: "A single croak in the coldest hours before dawn."

2. **Wrong pupil orientation (line 85).** "The vertical pupils were steady." Canon consistently specifies "gold eyes with horizontal pupils" (world.md, characters.md, running-state.md, ch01, ch03, ch08 continuity reviews). Western toads have horizontal pupils. This must be changed to "horizontal pupils."

3. **Frog described as "small" (lines 99, 123).** Line 99: "its small body pivoting." Line 123: "the frog moved ahead, small and deliberate." Canon describes the Western toad as "Large -- three to five inches, with a heavy, squat body" and "unexpectedly big" (ch01 brief). In earlier chapters, the frog is consistently described as large and heavy. "Small" directly contradicts this. Replace with language reflecting the toad's actual size, or use neutral phrasing. Note: "small throat" on line 49 is less problematic (the throat is proportionally small even on a large toad) but should be reviewed alongside the species fix.

### Minor (should fix)

1. **Rain jacket zip state unmentioned.** Ch. 10 established the rain jacket as "half-unzipped" (line 21 of ch10) and "twisted" (line 21 of ch10). Ch. 11 mentions the jacket settling around her when she stands (line 115) but never references the zip state. The jacket was partially unzipped for warmth management in Zone 4. This is a minor omission but worth a brief reference for physical state fidelity.

2. **Nose crusted detail absent.** Running-state and Ch. 10 both track "nose crusted with mucus/tears/soil paste" as part of Ada's face state. Ch. 11 does not mention the nose at all. The face is described with tear tracks, mud, salt, and swollen eyes, but the crusted nose (established Ch. 9, unchanged Ch. 10) is omitted. A minor physical detail that should persist.

3. **Insect chorus not referenced in prose.** Timeline.md specifies the insect chorus should be thinned in the coldest hours (Ch. 10-11). The chapter's continuity notes mention this, but the prose itself contains no reference to insects or their absence. Even a single phrase noting the quiet (or the absence of the insect sounds that had been present earlier) would strengthen the sound continuity.

4. **Jaw unclenching timing.** Line 79 describes the jaw unclenching as though it is happening for the first time: "The tension that had lived there since she was old enough to read a room before entering it." However, running-state notes "Jaw unclenched (since Ch. 8)." The running-state entry refers to Ch. 8 as when the jaw first unclenched. If the jaw was already unclenched in Ch. 8, then Ch. 11's description of jaw unclenching needs to be framed as a deeper release (going further than the Ch. 8 loosening), not as though the tension was still fully present. The prose could be adjusted to indicate this is a second, deeper release, or the continuity notes in Ch. 8 should be re-examined for what exactly was released there versus here.

5. **"Ten hours of darkness" (line 107).** "One from below, earned through ten hours of darkness." By timeline, Ch. 11 runs approximately 3:30-5:00 AM. Sunset was around 5:00 PM. That is 10-12 hours of darkness, so this is approximately correct but imprecise. At the first gray light (~5:00 AM), it would be closer to 12 hours since sunset. Consider "through the night's darkness" or adjust the number.

### Suggestions (optional improvements)

1. **Backpack recovery narration.** The backpack reappearance (line 117) is handled well with the spatial-folding explanation, which is consistent with world rules about the forest rearranging. The three possible explanations offered (spatial logic folded, Ada circled, network geometry) maintain the ambiguity. However, given the emphasis in Ch. 10's continuity notes that the backpack was "LEFT BEHIND" and "ABANDONED" in the boar's clearing (a deliberate, conscious act), the reappearance could benefit from one more sentence acknowledging that Ada left it deliberately and it is here anyway. The current handling is adequate but the emotional weight of the original leaving is somewhat glossed.

2. **Web silk references could be slightly more present.** Ch. 10 established silk residue in hair, on face, on hands, on jacket, on right knee denim. Ch. 11 mentions "the silk in her hair swaying" (line 111) during the rising sequence, which is good. The continuity notes track it comprehensively. But the silk could appear one more time in the early lying-down section (it's on her face and hands, which are pressed against the mycelium mat) to strengthen the carry-forward from Ch. 10.

3. **Underground water vibration.** The chapter brief calls for "Underground water felt as vibration" as a key environmental feature. The prose does not explicitly mention underground water. The hum is described thoroughly and the "vibration in her bones" is present, but the specific underground water detail from world.md and the brief is absent. A single reference would ground the hum in its multiple sources (mycelium + water).
