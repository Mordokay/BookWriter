The actual Ch 9 ("Show Me the Mechanism") is the Reed-Krrik lab chapter, matching the timeline's Day 11-12 entry. **Recommendation:** Update timeline.md so Day 13 corresponds to Reed's lab chapter (Ch 9), Day 14 to Hart's fungal audit (Ch 10), and Day 15 to the pool evening (Ch 11). 2. **Oona's Tupi trader reference -- minor spoiler management question.** Oona references "the trader from the river" who "ate them for twenty-two years and never stopped making it [the face]." This is consistent with timeline.md (Tupi trader, ~800 years ago, 22 human years among the Threnn). However, per the chapter outline, detailed information about absorbed humans is supposed to arrive primarily in Ch 15 through Archivist Pem. Oona's casual mention here is brief and works as ambient world-texture (she references the trader only in relation to grubs, not his fate or absorption), so it does not preempt Ch 15's revelation. **No change required**, but note that Ch 15 should not treat the Tupi trader's existence as a surprise -- the reader has already heard him mentioned.

3. **"Day 2" ambiguity in Quill's water experience.** The chapter states Quill went in the water "on his second day" and the continuity notes say "Day 2." In context, this likely means Quill's second day inside the settlement (Day 4 overall, since entry was Day 3). The chapter outline places the biologists' entry to Vreth-in-the-Root on Day 3. The chapter's own continuity notes say "Day 15 of the biologists' time in the settlement (Day 12 since entering Vreth-in-the-Root)." The parenthetical is slightly ambiguous: "Day 12 since entering" from Day 3 would be Day 15, which checks out (15 - 3 = 12). Quill's "second day" water experience on approximately Day 4-5 is plausible and unreferenced elsewhere, so no conflict exists. **No change required**, but future chapters should not contradict this established backstory.

## New Facts Established in Ch 11

1. **The Puddle doctrine delivered in full for the first time.** Vesh's speech: "You treat your self as a possession... We treat the self as weather." The self as a temporary water-shape that forms when conditions align and is never separate from its source.

2. **The Croaking introduced.** The sound the universe makes when it is happening. Stone croaks slowly, water by flowing, dying frogs by dying. "You are not the note. You are the singing. The singing does not stop."

3. **The Almost introduced and named.** 200 million years of attempting consciousness-merging. The chord of separate selves nearly resolving. Never once fully resolving. "The chord is the trying. The Almost is enough."

4. **Elderly frog's doctrinal correction.** The archive preserves the original phrasing: "The self is a borrowing from the wet." Vesh's weather metaphor is his dramatization. They have had this argument before. Establishes that the Wet Way has textual variants and internal doctrinal debates.

5. **Merging deaths revealed.** Pruning displacement operations require deep consciousness-merging. Some operators do not come back. "The work demands it. We do not discuss this at breathing circles. It would be unkind."

6. **The pool's collective silence and rapid recovery.** After Vesh's admission about merging deaths, the pool goes quiet for several seconds, then conversations resume collectively and without discussion -- a communal agreement to acknowledge and immediately cover what was said.

7. **Quill's prior water experience.** He went in the pool water once, on his second day in the settlement, and felt his thoughts go porous. Hart later attributed this to the mineral content being mildly psychoactive. This is why he now sits at the pool edge with feet on dry stone.

8. **Vesh's gold flecks dim when genuinely paused.** When the young frog asks about fear, Vesh's gold flecks dim slightly -- a detail not seen before, suggesting the question touched something real. Consistent with his chromatophore tells (gold flecks expand when moved, drain when afraid).

9. **Vesh's framing of human religions.** "Denial of death dressed up as transcendence" as a description of every human religion. "We do not deny death. We deny that the note was ever the point."

10. **Quill's nihilism pushback and Vesh's counter.** "Nihilism says nothing matters. We say nothing matters the way you think it matters." Mattering is the moment of reflection, not the duration of the puddle.

11. **Oona's reference to the Tupi trader and the grubs.** "The trader from the river" ate fermented grubs for 22 years and never stopped making the face. Consistent with timeline.

12. **The heckler frog:** "Vesh thinks he is the Wet Way. The Wet Way thinks Vesh is a puddle with a speaking problem." Establishes that Vesh is both revered and teased by the community.

13. **Talla's "municipal drugging" line.** Confirms the pool water is mildly psychoactive and that important conversations happen in water that loosens objections. Establishes Talla's framing of the pools as a control mechanism.

14. **Background pool population.** The rust-brown frog, the heckler, the singing trio in the center pool, the elderly doctrinal frog, and the young frog are all distinct background characters who give the pool scene its social texture.
