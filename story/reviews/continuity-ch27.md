### Minor

**1. Vesh's "twenty-two years" leading breathing circles vs. canon age of 22 frog-years**

Line 55: "a ritual guide who had been leading breathing circles for twenty-two years"

Characters.md line 226: "Approximate age: 22 frog-years (mature, past his physical prime, fully in his philosophical prime)"

If Vesh is 22 frog-years old and has been leading breathing circles for twenty-two years, he has been leading them since birth. This was flagged as Issue 2 in the Ch 5 continuity review (where Vesh claimed "twenty-two years teaching"). The Ch 5 review recommended changing to "fifteen years" or "most of my life."

The issue is mild because this is narrator observation (not Vesh's direct claim), and it could be read as approximate or as Vesh having been part of the breathing circle tradition since infancy in some capacity. But the arithmetic is the same problem as Ch 5.

**Suggested fix:** Change "twenty-two years" to "most of his life" or "nearly twenty years." Alternatively, if the intent is that Vesh has literally been involved since birth, the phrasing "leading breathing circles for as long as he had been alive" would make the totality feel intentional rather than accidental.

**2. Nurseries absent from the montage locations**

The chapter outline lists the setting as "fermentation cellars, nurseries, Speaking Chambers, Krrik's laboratory, Third Pool district, the breathing circle pool." The prose visits all of these except the nurseries as a distinct stop. The nursery entrance is mentioned in passing (line 7: "the smooth stone near the nursery entrance"), and Oona's line references "my tadpoles" (line 21), but Marsh is seen only in the fermentation cellars, not the nurseries.

This is an extremely minor deviation from the outline. The chapter brief in plot.md says "Marsh in the fermentation cellars and nurseries with Oona," but the chapter compresses the montage effectively and the nursery is implied through proximity and dialogue. No canon contradiction -- just a condensation choice.

**No fix required.** The chapter is better for its compression.

**3. The chapter outline specifies "Noll tolerates this with visible discomfort" (Vale's documentation work), but Ch 27 shows Noll's discomfort through the amber flicker at his wrist**

The outline says Noll "tolerates" Vale with "visible discomfort." The prose renders this as: "Noll floated at the pool's far end, his muted green-grey steady, his damaged forelimb tucked inward, tolerating the proceedings with a discomfort so controlled it was visible only in the faint amber flicker at his wrist."

Characters.md line 205: "When genuinely uncertain, a faint amber flicker crosses his damaged forelimb. Only Pem has known him long enough to notice."

The canon states that the amber flicker is something "only Pem has known him long enough to notice." In Ch 27, the narrator (Quill's POV) describes this flicker. After weeks of immersion, Quill's ability to read subtle frog chromatophore tells has been established progressively (Ch 5, 11, 20, 24, 25). His perception of this detail could be read as evidence of his absorption -- he now reads Noll at a depth previously reserved for Pem. This is arguably a strength rather than a flaw.

**No fix required.** The narrator's ability to perceive this tell is itself thematic evidence of Quill's transformation.

### The Almost Mechanics -- Ch 20 vs. Ch 27

Ch 20 describes the full merging ritual: 40-50 frogs, ceremonial pool, chromatophore synchronization, breathing alignment, croaking converging into a single chord, the membrane thinning, then receding. Quill observes from the upper stone ledge. The Almost arrives as a threshold approached and not crossed.

Ch 27 describes a breathing circle (not a full merging ritual): ~20 frogs, shallow warm pool, Quill participating in the water. He feels the frogs as "presences" at a frequency he "can almost hear," a "chord made of twenty bodies breathing in warm water." The chord "did not resolve." Human neurology cannot bridge what the frogs bridge, but something is felt "at the edges."

The distinction is correctly maintained. Ch 20 is a full merging ritual with operators -- intense, collective, nearly achieving the Almost. Ch 27 is a regular breathing circle -- gentler, communal, with Quill now in the water rather than observing from above. The Almost is present as ambient spiritual reality, not as an operational event. The "almost" register is consistent: the chord approaches but does not resolve in both cases, with the breathing circle version being milder and more personal. The progression from observer (Ch 20) to participant (Ch 27) is the arc's completion.

**Status: CONSISTENT.**
