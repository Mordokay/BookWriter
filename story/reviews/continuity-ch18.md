## Issues Found

### Critical

### Minor

1. **Day-count arithmetic in continuity notes (line 138).**
   The chapter's own continuity notes state "Day 27-28 of the biologists' time (Day 24-25 since entering Vreth-in-the-Root)." The biologists entered the settlement on Day 4 (Ch 3). Day 27 minus Day 4 = Day 23 since entering. The correct parenthetical should be "Day 23-24 since entering," not "Day 24-25."
   - **Severity:** Cosmetic (affects continuity notes only, not prose).
   - **Recommended action:** Correct the parenthetical in the continuity notes to "(Day 23-24 since entering Vreth-in-the-Root)."

2. **Timeline.md Day 27-28 entry describes wrong chapter content.**
   The timeline.md entry for Day 27-28 (Ch 18) reads: "The Pruning operations begin. Hundreds of synchronized operators coordinate quantum displacement of voss across hemispheres. Three operators are pulled from the pools unconscious; one does not recover." This describes events from the chapter outline for Ch 23 (the Pruning climax), not Ch 18. The actual Ch 18 covers the packed Speaking Chambers session, Vale's breathing circle experience, Talla's growing dissidence, and the political escalation.
   - **Severity:** Minor (timeline.md was written as an outline before chapters were drafted; this is a known pattern -- similar mismatches were flagged for Ch 16 and Ch 17 in their continuity reviews).
   - **Recommended action:** Update timeline.md Day 27-28 (Ch 18) to reflect actual chapter content: packed Speaking Chambers session (140+ frogs), Noll's announcement of expanded breathing circles ("enhanced community participation"), Talla's public objection deflected by unnamed coordinator, Vale's breathing circle experience (30 frogs, brief ego-loosening), Quill present in the circle, Hart's report on Talla's Third Pool meetings (40-45 frogs, growing), Reed's arrival with news of Krrik's failing modulation compounds.

3. **Outline beat partially unfulfilled: "Hart counts absences."**
   The chapter outline specifies that Vale, Hart, and Marsh observe the Speaking Chambers session from different angles: "Vale watches language become command, Hart counts absences, Marsh reads chromatophore anxiety." In the prose, Vale's political lens and Marsh's chromatophore reading are both present. However, Hart counting absences is not shown -- Hart appears only in the debrief scene and at the Talla meeting report. She is present in the chamber (she and Marsh are on the upper ledge with Vale), but her specific observational function (counting absences) is not dramatized.
   - **Severity:** Minor (the three-angle observation is partially delivered through Vale and Marsh; Hart's political intelligence role is fulfilled via the Third Pool meeting report instead).
   - **Recommended action:** Consider whether a brief Hart observation during the Speaking Chambers scene (e.g., noting who is absent, or noting attendance has grown) would strengthen the three-angle approach. Not required for continuity but would fulfill the outline beat.

4. **Consistency audit recommendation partially fulfilled: Quill's attendance should be "commented on."**
   The consistency audit for Ch 1-17 (recommendation #7) states: "Track Quill's breathing circle attendance as a visible marker for other characters. His growing participation should be noticed and commented on by at least one other biologist in Ch 18, per the outline." In the chapter, Vale notices Quill in the breathing circle (lines 53-57) and registers it internally with genuine weight. However, no biologist comments on it aloud during the debrief. Reed's "You went to a breathing circle" (line 120) is directed at Vale, not at Quill. The observation is present but the verbal comment -- one biologist saying something to or about Quill's participation -- is absent.
   - **Severity:** Minor (Vale's internal registration is effective and arguably more powerful than a spoken comment; the restraint fits Vale's character). However, the thread of other biologists noticing Quill's drift should surface explicitly in dialogue before Ch 20.
   - **Recommended action:** Either (a) add a single line during the debrief where one biologist mentions Quill's presence at the circle (Hart or Reed would be natural choices -- Hart already noted his absence from quarters in Ch 17), or (b) ensure this spoken acknowledgment occurs in Ch 19 instead. The thread cannot remain unspoken past Ch 20 without feeling like a dropped observation, given that five scientists share quarters.

## New Facts Established in Ch 18

1. **Speaking Chambers session: 140+ frogs.** Largest attendance shown in the story so far (Ch 16 had 63+). The escalation in attendance signals crisis-level mobilization.

2. **Breathing circles expanded to daily sessions (morning and evening) at four locations.** Announced by Noll as "enhanced community participation." Technically a recommendation, functionally mandatory.

3. **Unnamed coordinator character.** Older female frog, muted olive coloring, second tier. Delivers institutional deferral: "Have you considered that your objections might be better raised after the emergency has passed?" Represents the system's absorptive capacity -- not Noll, but Noll's system operating through ordinary frogs.

4. **Vale experiences consciousness-synchronization.** Breathing circle: 30 frogs + Vale, warm circular pool, dim bioluminescence, chemical air (sweet metallic). Vale's ego-loosening lasts fewer than 10 seconds. Described as the boundary between self and water becoming unclear, presences in the water sensed as warmth and chemical signatures, separate intentions moving like currents. Physical, not mystical. His body responds without consent. Hands trembling afterward. Skin oversensitized.

5. **Noll was not in the breathing circle but waited outside.** Delivered "You felt it" with genuine warmth. Vale did not respond. Noll then walked past him toward the pool. The administrator genuinely believes in the system.

6. **Quill confirmed as regular breathing circle participant.** Eyes closed, breathing with the frogs, expression "still and settled." Vale observes this without comment. This is the first direct confirmation of Quill's participation (Ch 17 only showed his absence from quarters).

7. **Talla's Third Pool meetings: growing attendance (40-45 frogs).** Two attendees publicly supported Noll in the Speaking Chambers earlier the same day. The gap between official consensus and actual mood is widening.

8. **Krrik's modulation compounds failing.** Eastern growth sectors burned through the latest application in 3 days (projected: 10). Krrik has reformulated twice. The voss adapts faster than the formula can change. Growth rate 11% above Ch 16 projections.

9. **Vale's private admission (final paragraph): the experience was "not unpleasant."** The horror is that his body betrayed his analysis. The mechanism that dissolves the self and saves the planet also feels good. This is the chapter's emotional payload and sets up Vale's internal conflict going forward.
