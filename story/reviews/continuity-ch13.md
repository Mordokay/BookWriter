## New Facts Established in Ch 13

These should be recorded in running-state.md and treated as canon going forward:

1. **Voss growth rate quantified:** 2 cm in 4 days along outer corridors, faster near water channels, slower where stone is denser. Visibly pushing through wall joints and across ceilings. Warm to the touch, warmer than surrounding stone.
2. **Maintenance crew reduction:** 6 teams last cycle, 4 this cycle. 2 reassigned to staging-ground support. Using chemical retardant that lasts 6-8 days per application. Retardant effectiveness reduced this cycle due to faster growth.
3. **Breathing circle attendance tracking:** A recessed alcove off the main corridor connecting outer residential sections to the communal pool complex. A single frog on a moss cushion with a stone tablet and hardened-reed stylus, recording names, pool affiliation, and frequency. The system is presented as neutral data collection; the infrastructure enables coercion regardless of stated intent.
4. **Relocated families:** Approximately 200 frogs moved from the western perimeter to narrower outer sections when staging grounds expanded. Three weeks in residence. Food distribution not adjusted. Stress colors visible in adults. Children pressing closer to adults than in the main nursery areas.
5. **Hart's watch died on Day 9.** She now uses the fungal light shift as a clock. (New detail, not established in prior chapters. Not contradicted by anything.)
6. **Hart took physical samples:** Voss scraping and retardant paste, stored in sealed containers in her field kit.
7. **Oona sends fermented grubs to relocated families every other day.** She notes the coordinators have not adjusted food distribution.
8. **The retardant paste:** A dark chemical paste applied to the base of voss intrusions. Disrupts the substrate's nutrient uptake. Effect lasts 6-8 days. Designed for standard expansion rates; less effective against accelerated growth.
9. **Hart's verdict established as a political position:** "The frogs are not lying about the Pruning. But they are using it. And the people who see that most clearly are the ones with the least power to do anything about it."
10. **Talla's chain metaphor:** "That is the cruelty of it. The chain is also the rope that pulls you from the water." Talla admits she has no clean alternative to the system she critiques.
11. **Marsh's position on care vs. control:** "Both things can be true. Care and control. At the same time. In the same system." She does not concede to Hart and Talla's harder reading but goes quieter.
