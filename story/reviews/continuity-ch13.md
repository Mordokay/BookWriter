## Priority 1 (Must Fix)

### 1.1 Scratches misattributed to plural "forearms" (line 69)

The inventory paragraph reads: "She had the scratches on her **forearms** and the swollen ridge on her right hand..."

Per running-state.md, the devil's club scratches are on her **left forearm only** (wrist to elbow). The right hand has a separate injury (swollen ridge between thumb and index finger), not scratches. "Forearms" (plural) implies both forearms are scratched, which contradicts canon. The earlier reference on line 23 says "the scratches on her forearm" (singular, correct), so this is an inconsistency within the chapter as well.

**Fix:** Change "the scratches on her forearms" to "the scratches on her left forearm" on line 69.

### 1.2 Pale cream dorsal stripe not mentioned in frog description

Per characters.md and world.md, the Western toad has a "pale cream dorsal stripe." Every prior full appearance (Ch. 1, Ch. 3, Ch. 6, Ch. 8, Ch. 11) has included this identifying detail in the frog's description. The Ch. 13 frog description (line 41) says "Three or four inches of mottled olive and gold. Warty, heavy" but omits the dorsal stripe entirely.

This is the 7th and final appearance. The dorsal stripe is a continuity-critical identifying marker that confirms this is the same toad. Its absence in the farewell scene is a canon omission.

**Fix:** Add the pale cream dorsal stripe to the frog's description on line 41. Suggested insertion after "mottled olive and gold": "the pale cream stripe down its back."

## Priority 2 (Should Fix)

### 2.1 Line 23: "scratches on her forearm" lacks left-arm specificity

The breeze passage reads: "It found the scratches on her forearm, the inflamed lines from wrist to elbow where she had pushed the rain jacket sleeve up." This is the left forearm per canon, but the text does not say "left." While the detail about pushing the sleeve up is accurate (Ch. 12: she pushed the rain jacket sleeve up to examine them), and readers who have been tracking will know which arm, the specificity should be preserved for consistency. The inventory paragraph on line 69 (once fixed per P1.1) will say "left forearm," so the earlier reference should match.

**Fix:** Change "the scratches on her forearm" to "the scratches on her left forearm" on line 23.

### 2.2 Hair and web silk not referenced in prose body

Running-state.md records: "Hair matted, web silk strands catching growing light" and "Web silk residue in hair, on hands, on jacket (persisting from Ch. 10, not fully removed)." The Ch. 13 continuity notes (line 103) correctly list "Hands: still dark with soil, web silk on backs, mycelium residue on palms." But the prose body of the chapter never mentions Ada's matted hair or the web silk in her hair/on her hands. Ch. 12 explicitly described "Her hair was matted, plastered to her temples and jaw, strands of web silk catching the growing light in faint silver lines." Ch. 13 should carry at least one of these details forward, especially in the inventory paragraph (line 69) which otherwise catalogs her physical state thoroughly.

**Fix:** Add a brief reference to hair or web silk in the inventory passage (line 69) or elsewhere in the prose. For example, in the inventory sequence: "She had her hair matted to her temples, web silk catching the light in her strands."

### 2.3 Rain jacket sleeve detail in line 23 implies sleeve is pushed up

Line 23 says: "the inflamed lines from wrist to elbow where she had pushed the rain jacket sleeve up." In Ch. 12, Ada pushed the sleeve up briefly to examine her scratches, then the text implies she lowered it (she tugged the zipper up against the cold). The running-state does not record the sleeve as being pushed up at the start of Ch. 13. The phrasing "where she had pushed" is past tense and could read as referencing a prior action (correct), but it could also imply the sleeve is currently up. This is ambiguous rather than wrong.

**Fix (minor):** Optionally clarify that the breeze found the scratches through the gap where the sleeve had ridden up, or note that the sleeve was pushed up again in the meadow's open air. Or leave as is if the past-tense reading is deemed clear enough.

### 2.4 Mycelium residue on palms not mentioned in prose

Running-state records "mycelium residue on palms" (from Ch. 11, lying on the mat). This is tracked in the chapter's own continuity notes (line 103) but never appears in the prose body. Given that Ada examines the stone in her palm (lines 63-65) and the inventory paragraph (line 69) is detailed, this is an omission of a persistent physical detail.

**Fix:** Consider a brief mention when Ada holds the stone in her open palm (line 63), such as the stone against the mycelium-pale residue on her palm, or include it in the inventory passage.

### 2.5 "the way" connector used 6+ times in prose

Per style-guide.md, the "the way" connector should be limited (Ch. 12 humanizer pass flagged a limit of 3). The Ch. 13 prose uses "the way" as a connector at least 6 times:

- Line 27: "the way the hum had entered it in Zone 4"
- Line 29: "the way a held breath responds to an open window"
- Line 33: "the way a bell lives in the metal after the ringing stops"
- Line 61: "the way a hand finds a familiar shape in the dark"
- Line 83: "the way the stream was in the stones it shaped, the way the mycelium was in the roots it connected, the way the night was in the dawn it had become" (3 in one sentence)

Total: at least 7 instances. This significantly exceeds the style guide's established limit and risks becoming a rhythmic tic.

**Fix:** Reduce to 3 maximum. The triple "the way" on line 83 is a deliberate rhetorical pattern for the closing passage and may be the strongest candidates to keep. Consider cutting or reformulating the instances on lines 27, 29, and 61.

## Priority 3 (Minor)

### 3.1 Filter word: "She could feel" (line 33)

Line 33 includes: "She could feel the meadow under her boots and the air on her skin and the light on her face and the forest at her back." Per style-guide.md, "she felt" and equivalent filter words should be minimized. The close POV already implies Ada's perception. This could be rendered directly: "The meadow was under her boots and the air was on her skin..."

However, the phrasing here is part of a longer rhetorical passage about permeability, and "She could feel" carries a slightly different weight (capacity, not mere perception). This is borderline.

**Fix (optional):** Consider rephrasing to remove the filter word, or keep if the emphasis on capacity is intentional.

### 3.2 "Fourteen hours ago" is slightly imprecise

Line 41: "A lifetime ago. Fourteen hours ago." Per timeline.md, Ada arrived at the trailhead around 5:00 PM and the first frog sighting on the nurse log was during Ch. 1 (5:00-6:00 PM). If Ch. 13 takes place at approximately 6:15-7:00 AM, the elapsed time is approximately 12-14 hours, not a precise fourteen. "Fourteen hours ago" is within the plausible range (if frog was seen ~5:15 PM and this moment is ~7:00 AM, that is ~13.75 hours), so this is acceptable. However, Ch. 12's revision notes deliberately made time references vague ("Hours of walking" instead of "twelve hours") to avoid precision risks.

**Fix (optional):** Consider "Thirteen hours ago" or "Half a day ago" for greater accuracy, or keep "Fourteen" as an approximation Ada would not precisely calculate.

### 3.3 Frog called "frog" and "toad" interchangeably

The prose alternates between "frog" (lines 39, 44-45, 49, 52, 57, 75) and "toad" (lines 43, 57). This is consistent with prior chapters (the animal is consistently called "the frog" in narration even though it is taxonomically a toad, with "Western toad" used for the naturalistic description). The alternation in Ch. 13 is intentional and effective. No fix needed, but flagging for awareness.

### 3.4 Temperature not explicitly stated

The brief specifies "low 30s F" and the chapter's continuity notes (line 182) confirm this. The prose renders the cold through sensory detail (cold air, dew, the warmth of sunlight as remarkable) without stating a number. This is consistent with the style guide's approach and is correct. No fix needed.

### 3.5 Long-sleeve shirt not mentioned

Running-state records Ada wearing a "long-sleeve shirt (soaked through beneath rain jacket)." The inventory on line 69 mentions the rain jacket, jeans, and boots but omits the long-sleeve shirt beneath the jacket. This is a very minor omission since the shirt is not visible and rarely referenced in prior chapters.

**Fix (optional):** Not necessary. The rain jacket is the visible layer and is correctly described.
