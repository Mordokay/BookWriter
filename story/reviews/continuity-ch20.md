## Issues Found

### Critical

**1. Quill cannot have witnessed the junior engineer's fear display in Krrik's lab (line 81)**

The prose reads: "the translucent pale of total fear that Quill had seen once before, in Krrik's lab, on a junior engineer whose body had abandoned every other signal to broadcast only this."

This references the junior engineer's involuntary color drain at the end of Ch 19 (line 197). However, Ch 19 is Reed's POV chapter and its own continuity notes confirm "Quill not present" (Ch 19, line 227). The running state for Ch 19 likewise lists Quill as absent ("Not present in Ch 19. Deliberately absent — his arc is at the breathing circles."). Quill was not in Krrik's lab during this event. He could not have seen it.

**Fix:** Replace the callback with one Quill actually witnessed. Options:
- The collective fear display at the Siberian coordination meeting (Ch 16, line 178): "63 frogs shift simultaneously to the same pale washed green." Quill was present (Ch 16, lines 9, 153, 180–181). Rewrite to something like: "the translucent pale of total fear that Quill had seen ripple through sixty frogs in the Speaking Chambers, a color the body chose when every other signal became irrelevant."
- The mourning dissolution (Ch 12): Pem's translucent skin flickering with Lenn's colors — less apt, but Quill was present.
- Simply describe the first-timer's fear display without a cross-chapter callback if neither alternative fits tonally.

The chapter's own embedded continuity notes (line 122) call this "Same involuntary fear display seen in Ch 19" but fail to flag the POV impossibility. This should be corrected in the notes as well.

### Minor

**2. Timeline.md (Day 32 / Ch 20) describes a different scene than what was written**

Timeline.md line 214–215 reads: "Day 32 (Ch. 20): As the settlement begins to recover, Noll invites the biologists to the Pools. He delivers the ultimatum: remain as honored witnesses or undergo dignified cessation."

The actual chapter is the merging ritual, consistent with the chapter outline (chapter-outline.md lines 517–539) and the plot summary (plot.md lines 113–115). The timeline entry is stale — it reflects an earlier structural plan where the ultimatum fell at Ch 20. The ultimatum content appears to have shifted to a later chapter.

**Fix:** Update timeline.md Day 32 entry to reflect the merging ritual as written. Move the ultimatum description to wherever it now falls in the chapter sequence.

**3. Elderly observer's line: "thirty of these" — implied age check**

The elderly frog says she has witnessed thirty full merging rituals. If Pruning cycles occur roughly every 30–40 human years (world.md line 422) and the full merging is performed during Prunings, thirty rituals would imply an age of 900–1,200 years. Frog lifespan is 25–35 years with medical intervention (characters.md / world.md line 472–474).

However, the Full Merging ritual description (world.md line 344) says it is "performed by experienced practitioners (and operationally by displacement operators during the Pruning)." This implies full mergings also occur outside Pruning cycles — as ongoing spiritual practice, not only during emergencies. If full mergings occur multiple times per year (plausible given the ritual culture), thirty in a 25–35 year life is easily achievable (roughly one per year). The chapter itself frames this as a Pruning-context merging but does not state that full mergings only happen during Prunings.

**Verdict:** Consistent, but only if the reader understands full mergings are not limited to Pruning cycles. No fix needed, but a brief anchor in her dialogue or description ("thirty of these in my life" rather than implying thirty Prunings) could prevent misreading.

**4. Young first-timer: outline says "visible tears" but chapter shows flickering/color drain**

The chapter outline (line 528) specifies: "A young frog watching for the first time has tears — or the frog equivalent — visible on his skin." The chapter instead shows nervous flickering that later becomes total color drain. The spirit of the beat is preserved (young observer's emotional overwhelm), but the specific detail of tears/frog-equivalent is absent.

**Verdict:** Acceptable deviation. The color drain is arguably more consistent with established frog physiology (chromatophore communication) than tear-analogues, which have not been established as a frog display. No fix required.

**5. Vesh's teaching recalled: "weeks ago, in the warm pools upstairs, between mouthfuls of fermented grub" (line 61)**

The Almost was introduced in Ch 11 at the communal pools (Day 15). Ch 20 is Day 32, making this 17 days ago — "weeks ago" is accurate (just over two weeks). The pools in Ch 11 are described as communal warm pools on the social level, above the deep ceremonial pool in Ch 20 — "upstairs" is directionally correct. Vesh eating insects during philosophical discourse is established throughout (characters.md line 229, Ch 11 line 67). "Fermented grub" specifically: Oona brings fermented grubs in Ch 11 (line 67). Vesh eating them mid-speech is consistent with character.

**Verdict:** PASS. All details check out.

## New Facts Established in Ch 20

1. **The deepest ceremonial pool** is physically described for the first time: natural chamber beneath the settlement's lowest level, roughly oval, warm water with sub-surface bioluminescence from fungal networks, tiered stone ledges on three sides worn smooth by generations, narrow approach corridor not shaped for human proportions.

2. **Full merging ritual procedure shown in detail:** breathing synchronizes first (cluster near center aligns, spreads outward), then individual colors merge into a single shifting shade, then individual croaking converges into a chord. The chord climbs to a threshold (the Almost), then recedes. Individuals re-emerge at different speeds — dazed, euphoric, or exhausted.

3. **Operator loss during merging:** A young female displacement operator goes unresponsive (flat colors, open unfocused eyes). Medical frogs remove her efficiently. Vesh does not look. The crowd collectively decides not to see. The loss is absorbed into the pool's routine. Consistent with canon ("honored and not discussed") but now dramatized.

4. **Community response to operator loss:** Deliberate collective non-reaction. Medical frogs move with practiced efficiency (they have done this before). Observers maintain position. Nobody speaks about it. The absence of reaction is itself a cultural mechanism.

5. **Quill's self-boundary erosion:** After the ritual, alone in his chamber, Quill cannot locate where he ends and the world begins. The edges of his selfhood have moved. He did not participate in the merging — he watched — but the observation itself changed the observer. This is a new stage in his arc (from observation to involuntary transformation).

6. **Elderly female observer:** Unnamed, faded green, translucent with advanced age. Has witnessed thirty full mergings. Delivers the line: "They never get easier to watch. The first one you think it is beautiful. By the tenth you know it is. That does not help."

7. **Young male first-timer observer:** Skin flickers between green and nervous yellow (cannot control broadcast). Steadied by an older female frog. Goes to total color drain (translucent pale) when the operator is lost. Helped out by the older female afterward; colors returning in uncertain patches.

8. **Hart's absence registered physically by the frog community:** Observers' colors shift with small ripples of amber when they count three humans instead of the expected complement. A brief dismissive color exchange follows. The frogs noticed and moved on.

9. **Vesh's opening ritual words:** "The chord was here before us. The chord will be here after us. We are the temporary voices that join it."

10. **Pool chemistry as shared medium:** The copper-and-herb chemical output of forty bodies dissolves into the water, creating a medium that is "not just water anymore" — the frogs, the water, the stone, and the vibration approach being a single thing. This extends canon descriptions of frog permeability into the ritual context.
