# Continuity Review -- Chapter 12: Stream

## Summary
Clean, with minor issues. The chapter demonstrates thorough continuity tracking across physical state, object management, emotional arc, sound progression, and callbacks. The zone transition, frog behavior, psilocybin decline, and dawn light progression are all handled with care. A handful of small issues merit attention, none of which would confuse a reader but which could strengthen accuracy.

## Issues Found

### Critical (must fix)

None.

### Minor (should fix)

1. **"Eight hours under the psilocybin" is understated.** Line 25 states: "Eight hours under the psilocybin, the chemical receding now like a tide." Ada ingested at approximately 5:00 PM (Ch. 1). Chapter 12 begins at approximately 5:00 AM. That is 12 hours since ingestion and roughly 11.5 hours since onset (~5:30 PM). Even counting only the intense phase (approximately 7:00 PM to 3:00 AM per timeline.md), that is 8 hours of high intensity, but the psilocybin has been active for far longer. The phrasing "Eight hours under the psilocybin" implies total duration rather than peak duration. The chapter brief and running-state both say "8+ under psilocybin," which originated the figure, but the actual pharmacological arc shows effects spanning 11+ hours. Consider revising to something like "the psilocybin receding after hours she could not count" or a more accurate figure, or reframing the eight hours as the intense period (e.g., "eight hours at the peak's mercy" or similar).

2. **"Twelve hours of walking, climbing, crawling, kneeling, lying on the ground" is slightly high.** Line 25. Ada began walking at approximately 5:30-6:00 PM (after onset, when she left the nurse log in Ch. 1). By 5:00 AM, that is 11-11.5 hours of being active in the forest. "Twelve hours" overshoots by 30-60 minutes. This is minor since Ada has no watch and experienced time is distorted, but the narrative voice is observational here rather than unreliable. Consider changing to "hours of walking" without a specific number, which would avoid any discrepancy while preserving the weight of the passage.

3. **Wood sorrel absent from the zone transition.** The chapter brief specifically lists "the first plants that are not fungi or fern: wood sorrel (Oxalis oregana) with heart-shaped leaves" among the key environmental features for this chapter. World.md describes Zone 5 as having "wood sorrel and wild violet." The chapter does not mention either plant. While the clearing itself is Ch. 13's territory, the brief places wood sorrel in Ch. 12's transition. Adding a brief mention of wood sorrel appearing along the stream bank or forest floor as Ada enters Zone 5 would strengthen the zone transition and align with the brief.

4. **Varied thrush simile drops "wet" from callback.** Ch. 1 describes the varied thrush's call as "like a finger drawn along the rim of a wet glass" (line 9). Ch. 12 echoes it as "the same quality of a finger drawn along the rim of glass" (line 85), omitting "wet." This may be intentional (Ada recalling the quality rather than the exact simile), but the near-exact echo creates an expectation of precision. The "wet" detail matters because it creates the specific resonance quality being described. Consider restoring "a wet glass" for full bookend symmetry.

### Suggestions (optional)

1. **Rain jacket zipper state unmentioned.** Running-state specifies the rain jacket has been "unzipped partway since Ch. 8 Zone 4 warmth." Ch. 12 mentions the jacket is on and describes its external condition (streaked, snagged, zipper clogged with web silk) but does not specify whether it is still partially unzipped. Since Ada is now in cold pre-dawn air outside Zone 4's warmth, noting whether the jacket is zipped or still hanging open could add physical specificity. A character in the low 30s F with a soaked shirt underneath might notice.

2. **Dawn gold at 6:15 AM may be early for late autumn PNW.** The chapter closes with "A pale gold had entered the east, warm and directional, the first light that came from a specific place" and "The gold touched the tops of the tallest firs." In late autumn Pacific Northwest, sunrise occurs around 7:15-7:30 AM; directional gold light would typically begin around 6:45-7:00 AM. Gold light at 6:15 AM is 30-45 minutes early. However, this is a pre-existing timeline issue established by the chapter-to-time mapping in timeline.md, and the story operates within "experienced time" where Ada's perception is unreliable. Flagging for awareness rather than as an error requiring correction.

3. **Banana slug and Pacific giant salamander absent.** The chapter brief mentions "Banana slugs (Ariolimax columbianus) on nurse logs" and "A Pacific giant salamander possibly glimpsed near the stream" as ambient wildlife for the Zone 5 transition. Neither appears in the chapter. These are listed as optional in the brief, so their absence is not an error. Including one or both would add sensory detail to the zone transition and reinforce the shift from the animal-demon territory to ordinary forest life.

4. **Ankle injury from Ch. 2 unmentioned.** Running-state tracks "Ankle rolled but held (Ch. 2)" as a persistent condition. After 12 hours of walking, this injury would likely still produce some discomfort. It has not been mentioned in recent chapters either, so this is a long-standing omission rather than a Ch. 12-specific issue, but the physical reckoning scene (where Ada examines her body) would be a natural place for a brief reference if desired.
