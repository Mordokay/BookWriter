1. **Timeline.md Day 25-26 (Ch 17) entry describes wrong chapter content.**
   Timeline.md's entry for "Day 25-26 (Ch. 17)" reads: "Talla publicly refuses to attend breathing circles, the most overtly political act in the settlement's recent memory. Her informal meetings call for transparency about Pruning mortality data and examination of alternative methods. Noll visits the biologists' quarters and requests they speak publicly to help unify the community. All refuse or deflect." None of this occurs in Ch 17 as written. The actual Ch 17 content is Hart's nighttime walk through the settlement: displacement operators drilling, fermentation argument, the cleaner Revvi, and the Vesh encounter. This is the same category of pre-existing timeline.md desynchronization flagged in the continuity-ch16 review (issue #4). The timeline.md entries for Ch 15-17 appear to reflect an earlier chapter outline that was subsequently revised. The prose is internally consistent; the file that is out of date is timeline.md.

   **Recommended action:** Update timeline.md Day 25-26 (Ch 17) to reflect actual chapter content: Hart's insomnia walk through the nighttime settlement, the displacement operator drill (12 frogs, one falling behind), the fermentation argument (four frogs, beetle larvae), the cleaner Revvi's monologue about civic knowledge, and the Vesh encounter at the meditation pool (unguarded vulnerability, then performance restored). No Talla, no Noll, no public refusal occurs in this chapter.

2. **"Dead in a few years" may slightly compress Vesh's remaining lifespan (line 131).**
   Ch 17 describes Vesh as "a creature who would be dead in a few years." Vesh is 22 frog-years (characters.md line 226). Threnn lifespan with medical intervention is 25-35 years (world.md lines 148, 472-474). At 22, Vesh has between 3 and 13 years remaining. "A few years" (typically read as 3-5) matches only the lower end of that range. However, this is Hart's subjective impression -- she is looking at a frog who, without his performance, "looked old" and tired, and whose exact age she may not know. The biologists know the upper bound (35 years, per Ch 9, Ch 15), but not necessarily Vesh's specific age. Hart's assessment is filtered through what she sees: a small, grey, depleted frog in dim light. The phrasing is defensible as characterization rather than factual claim.

   **Recommended action:** No change required. Flag for awareness. If precision is desired, "a creature who would be dead in a decade or less" would cover the full range without losing the emotional weight, but the current phrasing works within Hart's limited-perspective narration.

3. **"Two-hundred-million-year-old architecture" (line 73).**
   Hart describes the settlement's architecture as "the settlement's two-hundred-million-year-old architecture." Vreth-in-the-Root is the primary Amazon settlement, and Threnn civilization is approximately 200 million years old (world.md line 17, timeline.md line 7). However, the settlement itself has likely been rebuilt, grown, and modified continuously over that span -- it is living architecture that grows and changes. "Two-hundred-million-year-old architecture" could be read as the continuous tradition rather than the specific stone, which is consistent with a civilizational age. This is a matter of reading, not error.

   **Recommended action:** No change required. The phrasing reflects the age of the architectural tradition, not a claim that specific stones are 200 million years old.

## New Facts Established in Ch 17

1. **The cleaner Revvi.** Older female frog, faded green to sage-grey, has cleaned Corridor Nine for 12 of her 20 years. Knows every resident's patterns: families in the eastern alcoves argue on a 14-day cycle (also their mating cycle), an engineer from the computation banks checks nutrient flow at the same hour every night, a young displacement trainee cries three nights out of five. Revvi once told Vesh his breathing circles were too long and that he talked too much about the cosmos and not enough about drainage. Her key line: "Pem keeps the dead. I keep the living." Named by Vesh at the chapter's end.

2. **Nighttime bioluminescence behavior.** Brighter at night without active frogs to scatter and absorb it. The blue-green intensifies on bare walls. Shifts toward amber at dawn edges. Described as a "dawn sequence."

3. **Practice pools.** Three corridors from the residential quarter, down a sloping passage into a wide, shallow cavern with a low ceiling. Used for displacement coordination drills.

4. **Night-shift displacement operators.** Twelve frogs drilling in synchronized chromatophore patterns (green, gold, pale violet). One young female falling behind, half a beat slow, compensated for by her neighbor. The same young frog is referenced by Revvi as crying three nights out of five.

5. **Fermentation argument (ongoing).** Four frogs in a shallow alcove: large olive female (advocates 18 degrees for first 4 days, gradual reduction to 14 over 6), dark smaller male (objects, says her method produces mineral-sediment taste, has been saying so for 9 years), pale young moderator (advocates 16.5 degrees, published in the fermentation collective's seasonal review), elder male (14 of his 21 years fermenting, invokes the moon as a variable). The argument has run at least 9 years with no resolution.

6. **Vesh's unguarded state.** When alone and unperforming, Vesh's colors drain to a flat grey-green. He looks old, small, tired, and afraid. The performance returns in physical stages: posture straightens, colors warm, gold flecks surface, insect caught, voice resumes. This is observed from distance by Hart, not confessed. Consistent with the character sheet's core tension ("may secretly be afraid of death") and chromatophore tell ("drain to flat grey-green when afraid, immediately overrides with deliberate brightness").

7. **Hart-Vesh register established.** Not natural conversation partners. Different dialects of pragmatism. Key exchange: "Insomnia is the self refusing to practice dissolution" / "The pools are contaminated and the beetle is dead" / "Two excellent reasons to try both." Hart's question about the Pruning's cost yields Vesh's observation: "You sound like Talla."

8. **The outer edge of the settlement.** Corridors narrow to single-body width, bioluminescence dims to a faint pulse. Walls warm and rough, still growing. The settlement does not end -- it tapers from civic infrastructure into something like a forest edge. Water channels carry fragments of stripped-down voices.

9. **Meditation pool.** Small, set into an alcove off a corridor on the settlement's outer edge. Still water. Faint blue-green glow at pool edges only.

10. **Morning transition.** Bioluminescence shifts from blue-green toward amber. Water channels quicken. Bodies enter water. First croaking rises from the warrens -- low, scattered, joining into a ragged collective chorus. The frogs start each day by absorbing the world through their skin.

## Cross-Chapter Continuity Threads

- **Quill's absence.** His corner is empty at the chapter's opening and closing. He has been "coming back later and later." This is consistent with his arc across Ch 11, 12, and 15: attending breathing circles, drifting toward frog spiritual practice. Hart notices and does not interrogate. The absence creates a silent thread -- the reader knows more about where Quill is than Hart does.

- **The attendance clerk.** Hart's line "your dissenters get logged by a clerk in an alcove" (line 165) correctly references the breathing circle attendance logging station she discovered with Talla in Ch 13 (lines 69-91). The Ch 13 clerk sat on a moss cushion with a flat stone tablet and stylus, recording participation with pleasant, mild compliance. Hart's memory is accurate and appropriately compressed.

- **Displacement operators under strain.** The night drill scene (12 operators, one falling behind) is consistent with the escalating Pruning preparations shown in Ch 14 (increasingly complex coordination exercises, operators emerging disoriented) and Ch 16 (confirmed worst Pruning in centuries). The visible exhaustion bridges into Act 2B's crisis.

- **The young operator who cries.** Seen falling behind in the drill (line 27) and referenced by Revvi as crying three nights out of five (line 87). Two separate observations of the same frog, connecting Hart's walk without explicit narration. Clean internal threading.

- **Vesh-Talla tension.** Vesh's "You sound like Talla" (line 162) carries weight from Ch 16 (Quill observed a tense exchange between them), and from the character sheet (Vesh calls Talla "the splinter in the harmony"). The comparison is ambiguous -- neither compliment nor insult -- consistent with their old, complicated relationship.

- **Hart's relationship with the settlement.** The chapter builds on Ch 10 (Talla's counter-tour of system failures) and Ch 13 (the system-failure walkthrough). Hart has been the character most resistant to being moved. This chapter's nocturnal vignettes erode that resistance through accumulation, not argument. The ending ("the settlement is beautiful at night") is earned by the cleaner scene, the operator drill, and the fermentation argument -- mundane life that Hart cannot dismiss because it operates in her register (systems, maintenance, contamination).
