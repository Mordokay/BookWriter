**Action required:** Update the timeline entry for Day 14 / Ch 10 to match the chapter as written. The Talla-guided tour content should be mapped to whichever chapter it now belongs in (Ch 13 is the likely candidate based on the outline).

### Minor

**2. "Eleven days" count -- correct but could be misread.**

Hart says: "I have been here eleven days" (line 11). The continuity notes clarify this is Day 14 overall (11 days since entering Vreth-in-the-Root on Day 3). The math checks: Day 14 minus Day 3 = 11 days inside the settlement. This is correct.

However, "here" is ambiguous -- it could mean "in the Amazon" (which would be Day 14) or "in the settlement" (which is 11 days). In context, Hart clearly means the settlement, so this reads correctly. No fix needed, but flagging for awareness.

**3. Oona's "sixteen years" of winning domestic arguments.**

Line 191: "Oona turned to her with the full force of a personality that had been winning domestic arguments for sixteen years."

Oona's age is 16 frog-years (characters.md, line 310). The phrasing implies she has been winning domestic arguments for her entire life, which is comedic exaggeration. Technically, she would not have been winning "domestic arguments" as a tadpole or juvenile. If strict biological realism is desired, this could be "most of her adult life" or "twelve years" (assuming maturity around 3-4 frog-years). As written, it reads as a humorous characterization beat and is not jarring. Minor.

Line 167/191: Oona says she has been applying the compress for nine years. At 16 frog-years old, this means she started at age 7, which is plausible for a middle-aged frog. No issue.

**5. Lifespan reference: "25-35 years" not explicitly stated in chapter text.**

If it is intended as a new reveal for this chapter, it should be verified that an earlier chapter already established this fact, or it should be woven into the text. Check: this information is available in world.md (medicine section) but should be tracked for when it first appears in chapter prose.

**6. Old practitioner compared to Pem -- accurate but Pem has not appeared recently.**

Line 91: "her skin faded to a translucent grey-green that reminded Hart of Pem." Pem's description (characters.md, line 340) is "translucent grey-green." The physical comparison is accurate. The implication that Hart has met and remembers Pem is consistent with the tour in Ch 4 where Pem's archive was visited. No issue.

**7. "Forty percent" respiration figure stated in dialogue.**

Line 139: The old practitioner says "Roughly forty percent of our respiration happens through the skin's surface." This matches world.md exactly (line 486): "Cutaneous respiration accounts for roughly 40% of gas exchange." Consistent. Note: the jargon watch in the brief says to avoid "cutaneous respiration" -- the chapter correctly uses "breathe through our skin" and "respiration happens through the skin's surface" instead. Good.

**8. Quill silent in the doorway -- consistent with outline's "not present as POV" note.**

The outline says "Quill's arc position: Not present as POV. Hart's chapter, Hart's world." Quill does appear at the end of the chapter, listening from the doorway without speaking. The brief also specifies "Quill (end of chapter): Listening from the doorway." This is consistent. ## New Facts Established in Ch 10

These facts are introduced in this chapter and should be tracked for downstream continuity:

1. **Treatment chambers described in detail:** Open connected pools stepped into root-wall system, warm and shallow, ~2 meters across, separated by low stone ridges worn smooth by centuries. Air smells of mineral salts and wet copper with bruised leaves. Patients visible to passersby. Practitioners wear a "professional green" (muted steady green = white coat equivalent).

2. **Named practitioner: Tivven.** Treats a crushed forelimb with a regenerative paste. Asks about and is horrified by human surgery. Uses "Flow, don't push" as medical advice.

3. **Unnamed younger male practitioner.** Argues human surgery has "brutal logic" -- going through the wall of the skin when you cannot signal through it.

4. **Unnamed older female practitioner.** Translucent grey-green skin (reminiscent of Pem). Delivers "All medicine is violence managed with good intentions." Provides the most articulate expression of the permeability trade-off. Defines frog health as "quality of connection, not absence of disease."

5. **Brenk** -- Oona's neighbor, squat olive frog with stiff joints (3 years). Treated in warm pool. Accepts Oona's compress without concern.

6. **Surgery horror exchange:** Human surgery described to frogs for the first time. Anesthesia, incisions, sutures, sterile recovery. Tivven horrified ("You cut the body open? On purpose?"). Second practitioner sees engineering logic. This is a new cross-cultural medical exchange that may be referenced in later chapters.

7. **"A sealed frog"** -- described by frogs as a diagnosis, said like "terminal." A sealed frog is safe, clean, and cut off from chemical signaling, breathing circles, synchronization, and merging. The frogs consider this worse than infection.

8. **Moss compress: partial biochemical validation.** Krrik confirms three anti-inflammatory compounds in the moss mucilage layer, absorbed through skin at concentrations dependent on exposure duration and individual permeability. Neither fully validates nor fully dismisses the compress. Oona claims victory. Tivven disputes. Krrik does not notice.

9. **Dosing philosophy disagreement among practitioners:** Two older practitioners argue about lower concentrations over longer periods vs. higher concentrations with shorter exposure. Long-running, comfortable argument.

10. **Generational health debate:** Old practitioner says every generation thinks things are worse; archive data says otherwise; nobody reads the data.

11. **Pruning staging injuries in treatment chambers:** Stress fractures from jumping drills, glassy-eyed disorientation from extended synchronization practice, strained backs from equipment carrying. Volume exceeds normal chamber capacity. Staff stretched thin.

12. **Hart's verdict:** "The frogs' relationship with disease is not negligence, it is philosophy. And I do not know which is worse." -- Delivered at the biologists' quarters debrief. Crystallizes the chapter's central revelation.

13. **Hart writes notes in French** when processing something difficult -- first dramatization of this character trait.

14. **Debrief scene at biologists' quarters:** Hart, Reed, Marsh present throughout. Vale arrives mid-sentence. Quill listens from the doorway without speaking. The water channels' low vibration humming through the floor.
