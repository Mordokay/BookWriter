# Continuity Review -- Chapter 10: Web

## Summary

Chapter 10 is strong on continuity. Ada's physical state, injuries, clothing, and emotional progression carry forward accurately from Chapter 9. The backpack handling is consistent with the running-state (left on the ground beside Ada, now deliberately abandoned). The environmental details -- bioluminescence-only lighting, no moonlight, near-silence, Zone 4 warmth with encroaching cold -- are all consistent with canon. One minor wording inconsistency exists with the stag's characterization, and a few small items are worth flagging for revision.

## Issues Found

### Critical (must fix before proceeding)

None.

### Minor (should fix in revision)

1. **Stag watching descriptor inconsistent.** Line 73: "The stag had watched with cold assessment." Ch. 5 characterizes the stag's quality as "cold precision" (its voice, line 81: "The voice was cold. Formal. Each word placed with the precision of a chess piece"). Ch. 9 revised away from "cold assessment" to "cold scrutiny" specifically to align with Ch. 5, and the Ch. 9 prose uses "cold precision" in the boar voice comparison (line 79). Ch. 10 should use "cold precision" rather than "cold assessment" to maintain consistency across the stag's characterization.

2. **Ch. 9 continuity notes vs. running-state on backpack.** Ch. 9's own continuity notes section (line 205) says "backpack on (wet)" but its prose clearly shows the backpack slid from Ada's shoulder (line 101). The running-state correctly says "backpack off." Ch. 10 correctly treats the backpack as on the ground beside Ada. The error is in Ch. 9's continuity notes, not in Ch. 10, but it should be corrected in Ch. 9 to prevent future confusion.

3. **Boar musk smell timing.** Line 29 notes the boar's musk "still clung to her rain jacket, faintly, already fading." This is plausible given the boar lay against Ada in Ch. 9, but the boar was beside her back, not her front. The musk on the rain jacket is reasonable (the jacket was twisted around her while she lay on the ground with the boar), but should be verified against Ch. 9's description of the boar-Ada contact (boar's flank against her back).

### Suggestions (optional improvements)

1. **Shirt beneath rain jacket unmentioned.** The running-state tracks a "long-sleeve shirt (soaked through from stream submersion Ch. 4, beneath rain jacket)." Ch. 10 mentions the rain jacket multiple times but never references the soaked shirt beneath. This is not an error -- the shirt is simply not visible or relevant in this chapter's action -- but a brief sensory note about the cold wet fabric against her skin when she rises (line 17-21) could deepen physical continuity.

2. **Rolled ankle unmentioned.** The ankle rolled in Ch. 2 has been tracked in the running-state. Ch. 10 does not reference it. Again, not an error at this stage (it is a minor, persistent injury), but if Ada's legs are shaking and protesting during the stand-up sequence, noting a twinge from the ankle could add texture.

3. **Hands "black with soil" could be reinforced during web-handling.** The running-state says Ada's hands are "black with soil and broken mycelium." Ch. 10 references this once (line 19: "Her hands were black. Soil in every line of her palms, under every nail") but does not mention how the dark-soiled hands look against the luminous web silk. A small visual detail -- the contrast of black hands against gossamer threads catching bioluminescent light -- could strengthen the physical continuity and the imagery simultaneously.

4. **"Soaked shirt" and temperature.** When the cold reaches Ada at the chapter's close (line 151: "The cold reached her now"), referencing the wet shirt under the jacket as a vector for the cold could tie together the clothing continuity and the temperature beat.

5. **Web mandala as dark mirror of mycelium -- could be more physically distinct.** The chapter brief calls for the web mandala to be a "dark mirror" of the mycelium network. Ch. 10 does this conceptually (line 117: "A net over an ocean. A pattern imposed on a pattern that did not require it") but could benefit from one more sensory distinction: the web catches light from below (the mycelium) and holds it in rigid geometry, while the mycelium's light flows and pulses freely. The contrast between rigid pattern and flowing pattern would strengthen the foreshadowing for Ch. 11.
