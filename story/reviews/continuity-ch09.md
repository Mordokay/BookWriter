## CRITICAL Issues (Must Fix)

### 1. Typo in prose -- "turned toite" (line 79)

The boar's voice description contains a corrupted word: "turned toite and been mortared into the foundation." This appears to be a typo. Likely intended: "turned toite" (as in a mineral/geological suffix suggesting calcification), or possibly "turned toite" as a continuation of the calcification/fossilization metaphor. Whatever the intent, "toite" is not a word and will stop readers cold. Must be corrected. Possible fixes: "turned toite," "turned toite" (suffix completing "calcified, fossilized"), or rewritten as "turned toite and mortared" or "turned toite and been mortared into the foundation." If "ite" is intended as a mineral suffix (calc-ite, gran-ite), the construction needs to be clearer, e.g., "calcified, fossilized, turnedite-hard and mortared into the foundation." Alternatively, if the intent was "turned to stone," simply use that.

**Location:** Line 79 of ch09.md.

## MINOR Issues (Nice to Fix)

### 6. "Root-hollow where she had stopped running" (line 23) -- ambiguous reference

The frog's blink is described as "The same deliberate gesture from the root-hollow where she had stopped running." In Ch. 8, Ada was not literally running when she arrived at the root-hollow. She was walking, following the owl's path into Zone 4. The "stopped running" phrasing works metaphorically (the midpoint reversal, ceasing to flee/resist), but could be misread as literal. A clearer callback might reference the root-hollow where she sat and the frog taught, or the root-hollow where the midpoint reversal happened. Consider: "from the root-hollow where she had finally stopped" or "from the root-hollow where she had sat and listened."

**Location:** Line 23 of ch09.md.

### 7. Stag's eyes characterized as "cold assessment" (line 45)

Ch. 9 lists the prior animals' eyes: "The wolf's eyes had been amber and fixed, predatory focus. The stag's had been cold assessment. The owl's had been mirrors." The wolf and owl descriptions are accurate. The stag's eyes in Ch. 5 are described as "dark, glassy" (Continuity Notes) and the stag watched "without opinion, without mercy, without the possibility of looking away." The stag's register was more "cold precision" and mirror-like impersonality than "assessment." "Cold assessment" is close but slightly recharacterizes the stag's gaze as evaluative rather than reflective. Consider "cold impersonality" or "cold scrutiny" to match Ch. 5 more precisely.

**Location:** Line 45 of ch09.md.

### 8. Forest's grief-response deviation from world.md (lines 125, 201)

World.md's response table states that when Ada grieves, "Rain begins or intensifies. Water collects. Fog thickens. The forest becomes soft, damp, enveloping." Ch. 9 deliberately contradicts this: "The forest did not respond with rain or wind or closing canopy. It held. Steady. Unchanged." The chapter brief and the Continuity Notes confirm this was an intentional creative choice. The deviation works dramatically (the forest holding steady is more powerful than mirroring grief), but it should be acknowledged as a conscious departure from the established world rule. If this pattern is sustained in future grief moments (Ch. 11 tears), the world.md table may need a revision note acknowledging that Zone 4's forest response differs from earlier zones, or that the forest's grief-response evolves as Ada's relationship with it deepens.

**Location:** Lines 125, 201 of ch09.md; world.md response table.

### 9. Wolf's voice omitted from boar voice comparison (line 79)

Line 79 distinguishes the boar's voice from three prior animals: "Not the stag's cold precision. Not the owl's flat clarity. Not the snake's slow intimacy." The wolf also spoke (three lines, including "I'm tired too"), but its voice is not listed in this comparison. The wolf's voice was described as "direct, blunt, low" (characters.md) and the words "arrived the way the others had arrived." The omission is not an error -- the wolf may not have a voice quality as distinctly remembered -- but including it would complete the set. The swarm did not speak coherently, so its omission is appropriate.

**Location:** Line 79 of ch09.md.

### 10. "Thirty years of forward motion" (line 99) -- age precision

Characters.md states Ada is "late twenties to early thirties." "Thirty years of forward motion without pause" is used as a round number for her whole life. If Ada is 28-29, thirty years slightly overshoots. If she's 30-32, it works. The phrase reads naturally and isn't technically wrong (it could refer to roughly her lifespan), but in a story that tracks details carefully, it might be worth adjusting to "years of forward motion" without specifying a number.

**Location:** Line 99 of ch09.md.
