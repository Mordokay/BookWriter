## Issues Found

1. **Marsh's macaw-bite scar not mentioned.** Characters.md specifies "Has a small scar across the bridge of her nose from a macaw bite she considers a professional credential." The chapter brief lists this as a canon fact to preserve. The prose never references it. This is not necessarily a problem for Chapter 1 (not every physical detail must appear in the first chapter), but it should be introduced early. Flag for Chapter 2 or 3.

2. **Reed's physical description is incomplete.** Characters.md describes Reed as "Wiry, restless, never entirely still -- always tapping something, adjusting his glasses, or examining whatever is closest to his hands." The chapter captures the glasses-pushing and the restlessness well, but does not establish "wiry" as a physical marker. Again, not strictly required in Chapter 1, but worth noting.

3. **"Began to move" filter word (line 59).** "Quill's hands began to move" -- the humanizer checklist flags "began to" as a filter/distancing word when the action simply happens. Could be "Quill's hands moved" for directness. However, the "began to" here arguably conveys the specific quality of a gradual onset, so this is borderline. Minor.

4. **"As if" count: 1 instance (line 47).** Well within the 3-per-chapter maximum. No issue.

5. **"The way" simile connector: 3 instances (lines 83, 189, 205).** This hits the maximum of 3 per chapter. Not a violation, but at the ceiling. The instances are:
   - Line 83: "the way a man might repeat a restaurant's daily special" -- strong, character-revealing simile. Keep.
   - Line 189: "in the way groups disperse when an argument has exhausted itself" -- functional. Fine.
   - Line 205: "warm the way a living thing is warm" -- essential for the stone description. Keep.

6. **No em-dashes in prose.** Pass. The only "--" in the file is the horizontal rule on line 215 (section divider). Clean.

7. **No semicolons in prose.** Pass. Zero instances.

8. **AI-tell word check: 0 flagged words in prose.** Clean. No instances of delve, tapestry, myriad, visceral, palpable, ethereal, ephemeral, testament, cacophony, symphony, gossamer, iridescent, luminescent, ineffable, primordial, etc. The word "luminous" does not appear in prose (only in continuity notes). "Luminosity" appears once on line 205 ("any category of luminosity she could name") -- this is not on the AI-tell list and is used in a context where the POV character is actively categorizing, so it works.

9. **AI-tell phrase check: 0 flagged phrases in prose.** No instances of "a testament to," "hung heavy in the air," "the gravity of," "washed over," "something shifted in," "not for the first time," etc. Clean.

10. **Bogota reference (line 135).** Quill mentions "I've been worried about tinnitus since Bogota." This is a small background detail not established in the character sheet or timeline. It implies Quill had a previous field experience in Bogota. This is fine for characterization but should be logged as an established fact for continuity. Future chapters must not contradict this.

11. **Reed says "I am not a postdoc" (line 69).** This is consistent with his character sheet (age 34, professor's salary per Vale's line on line 71). No issue.

12. **Altitude reference (line 145).** Hart says "We're at two hundred meters above sea level." This establishes the camp's elevation. The world file does not specify exact elevation. This fact is now canon and must be respected in future chapters.
