### Minor

1. **"Three weeks" watching Vesh (line 45).**
   Ch 24 prose: "he had spent three weeks watching Vesh hold himself with the particular uprightness of a frog who has decided that the universe is fundamentally acceptable."
   The chapter's own continuity notes place this at Day 36-37 of the biologists' time. Quill met Vesh on Day 7 (Ch 5), which puts their acquaintance at approximately 30 days (~4.3 weeks). However, "three weeks" is defensible if interpreted as the period since Quill began watching Vesh with deep spiritual attention -- his first real exposure to the Wet Way was Day 15 (Ch 11, the breathing circle), and Day 36 minus Day 15 is exactly 21 days. This reading works, but the phrasing "three weeks" could confuse a close reader who tracks the timeline from Day 7. Consider changing to "weeks" (unquantified) to avoid the ambiguity, or accept as-is since the more specific reading (from Ch 11 onward) holds.

2. **Oona's partner timeline -- no contradiction, but worth tracking.**
   Ch 24 (line 29): "When my partner died, I kept putting two portions in the bowl for three months."
   Ch 7 (line 137): "That was four years ago."
   Characters.md (key moment 2, line 323): "four years ago."
   Ch 24 does not specify a timeline for the death. No contradiction. The prior continuity review (Ch 7) flagged a discrepancy between Ch 7's "four years ago" and an earlier version of characters.md that said "two years ago." Characters.md has since been updated to "four years ago." Ch 24 is consistent with the corrected canon.

3. **Olive female vs. "young frog" in Oona's comfort scene.**
   Line 17: "A compact olive female took position at the waterline." She is an operator's partner.
   Line 23: "the compact olive female at the waterline."
   Line 27: Oona sits beside her. Line 29: Oona shares the partner-death detail.
   Line 103: Quill reflects on "the young olive female at the waterline eating a grub beside Oona."
   The frog is described as "compact olive female" in lines 17-23, then as "young olive female" in line 103. The word "young" is introduced late and never established earlier. This is not a hard contradiction (she could be both compact and young), but the shift from "compact" to "young" in the internal monologue is a minor descriptor inconsistency. Consider aligning: either introduce "young" earlier or use "compact" in line 103.

## New Facts Established in Ch 24

1. **Recovery pools described in detail.** Shallow, wider than bathing pools, stone-lined, warm, treated with herbal compounds producing a copper-and-herbs smell. Used for post-operation operator recovery. Partners and friends attend. Medical frogs distribute sharp herbal preparations at practiced intervals.

2. **Vesh's private grief color.** Deep indigo-brown, involuntary, lasted approximately three seconds. Not the emerald performance, not gold spiritual intensity, not the grey-green fear glimpsed in Ch 17. This is a new chromatic register: private, unperformed. Surfaced after attending the unresponsive operator. The emerald reasserted deliberately.

3. **Vesh's posture crack.** After attending the unresponsive operator, a small settling in shoulders, fractional lowering of head. Described as the first visible crack in his performative serenity. Quill notices because he has been watching Vesh closely since Ch 11.

4. **Unresponsive operator.** Young female. Colors cycling in a random sequence with no pattern -- not damaged signal (static) but signal with no sender. Body alive, eyes open, self gone. Distinct from the Ch 23 operator who died: this operator is alive but has not returned to individual consciousness. Vesh attends her longest. Medical frogs watch with professional steadiness.

5. **Vesh's six-word moral arithmetic.** "Enough to matter. Not enough to stop." -- in response to how many operators have dissolved and not returned across generations.

6. **Vesh's full philosophical statement.** "You are wondering if we are monsters. That is the wrong question. We are a species that has found a way to survive that costs us exactly what it should. Everything worth preserving costs something it should not."

7. **Oona's "two portions" grief detail.** "When my partner died, I kept putting two portions in the bowl for three months. Nobody told me to stop. I stopped when I stopped." New domestic-specific detail about her grief period. Consistent with the four-years-ago timeline (Ch 7) and Oona's character (domestic, specific, unsentimental).

8. **Hart and Talla: wordless shared labor.** Cleaning the staging grounds together without dialogue. Passing stone vessels, folding cloths. A new expression of their relationship: beyond shared skepticism, into shared physical presence.

9. **Quill's triple-emotion crisis.** Envy, pity, and fear of the frogs collapsed into a single unnamed sensation. He envies their communal grief; he pities the cost of their system; he fears wanting what they have. The three feelings are indistinguishable by chapter's end. This is described as irreversible transformation.

10. **Quill's sealed-boundary awareness.** He looks at his human hands and registers that his skin does not change color, does not broadcast emotion, does not absorb grief chemistry. The boundary of his self ends where his skin begins. He envies the frogs' permeability.

11. **Settlement in post-Pruning recovery.** Cleaning crews restoring staging grounds and corridors. Voss scar tissue visible on walls. Frogs in corridors eating in silence, exhaustion visible. Domestic rhythms resuming at reduced energy. Collective processing of wounds is efficient and practiced.
