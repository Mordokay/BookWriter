## New Facts Established in Ch 15

1. **The archive's corridor approach:** The corridors grow quieter in layers as you approach. Water channels narrow. Bioluminescence changes from pulsing ("municipal") to still, pale, unmoving patches. The stone underfoot is smoother than anywhere else in the settlement -- worn by individual archivists walking the same path over generations.

2. **Archivist succession:** The name "Pem" passes with the role. Archivists walk the same corridor, tend the same records, take the same name, and are replaced when they die. (Consistent with characters.md line 337: "named for the position, not the individual.")

3. **Junior archivist:** Young, small, bright coloring (recently assigned). Feeds nutrient solution from a clay vessel to tissue cultures. Leaves when Pem signals with a faint color shift.

4. **Archive physical details:** Root structures form living columns (still growing, still carrying sap). Tissue cultures range in color from old-paper to translucent-luminescent to dark slow-pulsing. The air carries a faint static charge from the stored memories' chemical current.

5. **Pem's dismissal signal:** A faint, slow wash of color from throat to forelimb means "we will be alone now."

6. **Archive philosophy established in Pem's words:** "We remember what we choose. Every generation curates its inheritance. What you call 'history' we call 'the story the archive tells this year.'" And: "The archivist decides what is available. What matters is decided by whoever is asking."

7. **Memory deposit method:** The dying choose to deposit. The process is imperfect. Emotional coloring transfers more reliably than factual detail. (Consistent with world.md; now dramatized.)

8. **Pem's singing retrieval demonstrated:** A low, sustained note with overtones and undertones. The tissue responds with surface ripple, color shift, and sensory/emotional impressions that the listener can feel (temperature change, smell, a visual impression "like a photograph seen through water").

9. **Retrieved memory (archivist, 300 years ago):** A female archivist's last season. Impression of sitting high, looking at canopy, contentment mixed with awareness of running out. Pem: "The feelings transfer more reliably than the facts."

10. **Pem on previous human imprints:** "The imprints are available. They are also private. Memory belongs to the remembered." The deflection is institutional.

11. **The Tupi trader's psychological arc:** After 22 years, "he had stopped missing his people. He was not sure if this was peace or damage. The archivist recorded both interpretations." (Consistent with timeline.md; now dramatized with emotional weight.)

12. **Quill's father:** Died six years ago in a hospital in London. In his last week, morphine made "the ceiling interesting" -- a joke or observation or both. This is Quill's personal parallel to imperfect organic memory. (First establishment of this backstory detail.)

13. **Reed's computation discovery:** The eastern arrays have diverged from Krrik's model by nearly 12% in 4 days. Pattern clustering in the thinking moss. Krrik has reassigned two computation banks to pattern analysis without announcing it. Reed's surface root density measurements may be relevant. Reed wants to visit Krrik's lab with a witness. (Advances the science/Pruning thread.)

14. **Hart's debrief was "two days ago":** Places the Ch 13 debrief approximately 2 days before Ch 15's quarters scene (consistent with Day 19-20 for Ch 13 and Day 22-23 for Ch 15).

15. **Quill's spot in the quarters:** The root ledge near the doorway, described as "the same spot he had occupied during Hart's debrief two days ago." Now established as his habitual position -- present but peripheral.
