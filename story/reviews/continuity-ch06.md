## Issues Found

### CRITICAL: Fourth-Wall Break (Line 151)

> "which bothered him in the same diffuse way it had bothered him in Chapter 4, when he had stopped noticing the strangeness of the blue-green light."

The text explicitly references "Chapter 4" as a narrative unit. Vale is a character inside the story; he does not know he is in Chapter 4. This is a fourth-wall break that must be revised. Replace with a diegetic temporal reference, e.g., "which bothered him in the same diffuse way it had bothered him days earlier, when he had stopped noticing the strangeness of the blue-green light" or "during the tour" or similar in-world anchor.

**Action: Revise line 151. Remove "in Chapter 4" and replace with an in-world time reference.**

### MODERATE: Noll's Amber Forelimb Tell -- Visibility Question (Line 137)

Canon states (characters.md, Noll's chromatophore tells): "When genuinely uncertain, a faint amber flicker crosses his damaged forelimb. **Only Pem has known him long enough to notice.**"

In Chapter 6, Vale observes: "Noll's damaged forelimb twitched. A faint amber crossed it, quick and suppressed."

This is the first time Vale has observed a full Speaking Chambers session. The canon explicitly restricts awareness of this tell to Pem. However, the chapter brief itself calls for this beat ("A faint amber flicker crosses his damaged forelimb when genuinely uncertain"), and the broader character arc requires Vale to become a sharp reader of Noll. Two possible approaches:

1. **Soften Vale's certainty.** Have Vale notice the twitch and the color shift but not yet understand its significance. He sees something; he does not yet know what it means. This preserves the canon restriction while planting the observation for later payoff.
2. **Update the canon.** Revise the characters.md note to say "Few have known him long enough to notice" or "Pem was the first to notice," allowing Vale to independently discover the tell through his political acuity.

**Action: Choose one approach and apply. Option 1 is recommended as it rewards close reading and preserves Pem's unique status.**

### MINOR: Oona's "Eighty Million Years" Claim (Line 135)

Oona says: "This has been going on for about eighty million years."

World.md states the Amazon-Siberian cultural tension has "persisted for roughly 80 million years." This is consistent. However, the figure is presented as common knowledge among frogs, which it plausibly would be. No issue -- flagged only for the record.

### MINOR: Noll Referenced as "Dr. Krrik" by Siberian Leader (Line 131-133)

Noll says: "Dr. Krrik's latest projections suggest--" and the Siberian leader responds with "Krrik's projections." The Siberian drops the honorific "Dr." This is a nice, consistent detail: the Siberians strip diplomatic niceties, including titles. However, note that Krrik's canon title is "Dr. Krrik" -- the Siberian's omission of "Dr." should be read as characterization, not error. No fix needed.

### MINOR: Word Count

The brief targets approximately 3,200 words. The chapter prose (excluding continuity notes and chapter intent) runs approximately 3,800-4,000 words. Slightly over target but within acceptable range for a chapter of this structural complexity (two settings, multiple characters, the Siberian arrival sequence). No fix required unless strict word-count compliance is desired.
