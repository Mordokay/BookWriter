## Issues Found

### Critical

1. **Meta-reference breaks fourth wall (Line 49).**
   The prose reads: "Ninety percent. In Ch 9, the number had been sixty-seven, and that had made the room go quiet."
   "In Ch 9" is a chapter reference visible to the reader. Reed would not think in chapter numbers. This should reference the scene itself -- e.g., "The first time he had seen the growth model in Krrik's lab, the number had been sixty-seven" or "Weeks ago, when Krrik had first shown him the growth model, the number had been sixty-seven."
   **Must be fixed before publication.**

### Minor

1. **Siberian engineer physical description simplified.**
   In Ch 16 (line 53), the engineer is described as "smaller than the other Siberians, mottled grey with flecks of dark blue at the joints." In Ch 19 (line 5), she is described only as "compact and grey." The continuity notes correctly identify her as "mottled grey with dark blue flecks at joints," but the prose itself drops the distinguishing detail (the dark blue flecks). Not a contradiction -- "compact and grey" does not contradict the earlier description -- but a missed opportunity to confirm identity for attentive readers. Consider adding the flecks or the mottling in at least one of her descriptions.

2. **"Four sessions per day" -- potential ambiguity.**
   Line 153: "Noll expanded to four sessions per day." In context, this refers to operator synchronization drill sessions, not breathing circles. Ch 18 established breathing circles at "morning and evening sessions... at four locations." These are different things (operator drills vs. community breathing circles), and the escalation makes logical sense. However, a reader could momentarily confuse the two. The text is technically unambiguous because the surrounding context is clearly about operator drills, but a clarifying word ("Noll expanded to four drill sessions per day" or "four synchronization sessions per day") would eliminate any possible confusion.

3. **Timeline.md mismatch with chapter content.**
   Timeline.md's Day 29-31 entry describes Ch 19 as "The Pruning's active phase winds down... Vesh tends to the damaged operators. One young operator does not respond. Quill witnesses the cost." The chapter as written does not include Vesh or Quill (consistent with the chapter brief, which specifies "Quill not present"). Additionally, the timeline's Day 27-28 entry for Ch 18 describes "Pruning operations begin... Hundreds of synchronized operators coordinate quantum displacement" -- events that have not been dramatized in Ch 18 as written. This is a known pattern from previous continuity reviews: the timeline was written as an outline before chapters were drafted and has diverged. The timeline should be updated to reflect what actually occurs in Ch 18-19.

4. **Oona's "second week / third week" reference -- internal consistency assumed but not anchored.**
   Line 125: "This never used to happen until the third week. We are in the second week." Oona is clearly referring to weeks of the active Pruning crisis/voss growth cycle, not weeks of the biologists' stay (which is Week 5). The Pruning staging preparations began approximately three months before the story opened (per timeline.md), with active growth escalation over recent weeks. This is plausible -- the voss growth has been accelerating over the past two to three weeks of story time -- but the specific "week" numbering is internal to frog Pruning scheduling and has not been explicitly established elsewhere. This is not a contradiction, but if a later chapter references Pruning weeks, the numbering must be consistent with this statement.
