## Facts Established

New facts introduced in Chapter 7 that should be tracked in running state:

### Physical/Environmental
1. The fermentation cellars are underground, accessed by a root-walled corridor that slopes into warmth. Low, broad chamber with blue-green glow. Stone basins hold dark, wet culture beds in layered arrangements.
2. Oona's culture stock was relocated to the lower level due to Pruning preparations. The humidity and temperature are wrong there.
3. Morning wetting pools exist in corridor floors — shallow, barely deep enough to cover frogs' backs.
4. The eastern tadpole nursery consists of interconnected shallow pools at different temperatures, with clear water. Warm stones at pool edges. Moss beds pressed into warm stone.
5. The eastern nursery has twelve rotating caretakers. A western nursery has fifteen.
6. The nursery's water supply comes from the eastern channel, fed by the main filtration system.
7. Nursery moss is changed on a seven-day cycle.
8. The residential warrens have no walls or doors — chambers flow into each other.
9. The eastern section past the fourth junction is being cleared for Pruning staging preparations.

### Biological/Cultural
10. Tadpole temperature preferences affect development: too warm = fast development, poor coordination; too cool = slow development, broader color range.
11. Tadpoles are dark, almost black, with faint color along their tails.
12. Morning wetting involves sitting in water and breathing through skin. Some frogs do it with eyes closed; others eat and gossip simultaneously. Oona: "You remind your body that it is not separate from the water."
13. Consciousness circles encourage slowing down — "settling into the current."
14. Oona's sister joined a consciousness circle and now does everything slower, including dreaming about water.
15. Frog mating calls: basic call is instinctive; refinement (holding color at exact intensity, harmonic shaping) is taught by mothers, aunts, and caretakers. "A frog with no teaching can call, but the call is rough."
16. Regional aesthetic differences in mating calls: eastern warrens prefer lower calls and slower displays; western frogs prefer speed and quick color changes ("sparkle").
17. Mating display quality: depth, resonance (best calls make the pool vibrate), color range and control (four to five shades in sequence without bleeding). Sloppy transitions suggest a sloppy mind.
18. Communal sleeping is the norm. Sleeping alone is considered eccentric. "Sleeping alone makes you dry. Closed." A neighboring engineer built a root partition (not a wall, "a suggestion of a wall") and is considered both excellent and strange.
19. Frogs feel each other's breathing and chemistry even while sleeping.
20. Oona's partner died four years ago. Traditional call style: low, steady, three colors held long. Oona: "We live fast. We grieve fast. It does not mean we grieve less."
21. Pruning preparations displace families from residential sections. Equipment, coordination personnel, and frogs from outer districts are staged in cleared warrens.

### Characters
22. Gren: a frog whose clutch develops slowly. Blames water temperature. Other caretakers attribute it to genetics but cannot say so without Gren accusing them of judgment.
23. Dess: a frog who attends Vesh's breathing circles three times a week and dispenses unsolicited philosophical pronouncements. Told Oona "suffering is a temporary shape."
24. Mott: another frog who offers unsolicited philosophical commentary ("Contentment is the puddle accepting its depth"). Dismissed by Oona.
25. Oona keeps a small stone vessel of fermented grubs in a root nook near her sleeping chamber. Defends it with territorial instinct.
26. Hart previously tested Renn's bathing pool minerals and told Renn the mineral balance was off. Renn has not spoken to Hart since. Renn has maintained those pools for nine years.

### Marsh's Development
27. Marsh laughed genuinely — "not the polite exhale of a researcher maintaining rapport, but the real sound." Two frogs in a nearby chamber looked up.
28. Marsh stopped taking notes after twenty minutes in the nursery and just helped.
29. Marsh's notebook entry: "sleeping alone makes you dry" — described as "the kind of fact that could only arrive through sitting on a root ledge with someone who trusted you enough to say the ordinary thing instead of the impressive one."
