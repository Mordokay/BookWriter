# Continuity Review: Chapter 7

## Critical Issues (must fix)

1. **Owl facial disc described as "heart-shaped" -- incorrect for species (line 39).**
   The chapter reads: "The facial disc was pale, heart-shaped, the feathers arranged in concentric rings that framed the face like the aperture of a lens opening." Northern spotted owls (Strix occidentalis caurina) have a round facial disc with dark concentric rings. Heart-shaped facial discs are the defining feature of barn owls (Tytonidae family). The spotted owl's disc is round and brown with darker concentric barring. This contradicts world.md's commitment to starting from real biology ("Use the forest's real biology when possible. Real fungi, real animal behavior, real plant life. The psychedelic layer warps these details but starts from accuracy" -- style-guide.md, line 162). Fix: change "heart-shaped" to "round" or "circular" and adjust the surrounding description. The concentric rings detail is actually correct for spotted owls, so the "aperture of a lens" simile can stay.

2. **Internal contradiction: eyes will not close, then suddenly closed (lines 85-89).**
   Line 85: "her eyelids would not close against the pull of the question any more than her lungs would stop breathing against the pull of air. She tried again. Her lids descended a fraction and then rose, drawn back up by the dark mirrors..."
   Line 87: "Behind her closed lids: no relief."
   Line 89: "She opened her eyes."
   The transition from "eyelids would not close" to "behind her closed lids" is missing. The reader is told the gaze holds Ada's eyes open, that her lids only descend a fraction before being pulled back up. Then the very next paragraph starts with "Behind her closed lids," implying the eyes are now fully closed. No beat marks the moment the eyes finally close. This reads as a logical contradiction. Fix: either add a brief transitional beat showing the lids finally closing (perhaps on the third attempt, or the gaze releasing for a moment), or revise line 87 to describe what Ada sees in the fraction-of-a-second partial closure rather than behind fully closed lids.

## Moderate Issues (should fix)

The continuity notes declare "Temperature: low 30s F. Cold but overridden by psilocybin intensity" (line 218) without acknowledging the Zone 4 warming rule. This warming is an established signature of Zone 4 in world.md and timeline.md.

4. **Swarm listed among encounters "absorbed" by the forest (line 167).**
   Line 167: "The stag, the snake, the wolf, the swarm, all absorbed, all inhaled, each one a mirror the forest had held up and then put away." Running-state and Ch. 2 establish that the swarm "thinned and dispersed" -- it did not vanish into the forest the way the wolf, stag, and snake did. The "forest inhaling what it had exhaled" motif (running-state, line 68) explicitly tracks wolf (Ch. 3, absorbed by darkness), stag (Ch. 5, walked into forest), and snake (Ch. 6, absorbed by undergrowth). The swarm has a different departure mechanic. Lumping it with the others contradicts the established pattern. Fix: either remove "the swarm" from the list, or rephrase to acknowledge the different mode (e.g., "the stag, the snake, the wolf, all absorbed, all inhaled, the swarm dissolved, each one a mirror the forest had held up and then put away").

5. **Banned cliche variant: "She did not know how long it lasted" (line 121).**
   Constraints.md bans "she didn't know how long she had been walking." Line 121 uses: "She did not know how long it lasted." While not the exact banned phrase, it is the same construction ("she didn't know how long...") applied to duration rather than walking. The constraints.md appears to be banning the structure, not just the specific walking variant. Fix: rephrase to avoid the construction. The sentence continues beautifully with "The psilocybin stretched the weeping into its own era" -- this is strong enough to convey temporal dislocation without the generic opener.

6. **Filter words in prose (lines 11, 21, 23, 57, 65, 93, 153, 171).**
   Style-guide.md (line 115): "Minimize 'she felt,' 'she saw,' 'she heard,' 'she noticed,' 'she realized.'" Several instances remain in the prose:
   - Line 11: "She saw it without wanting to" -- this one arguably works because the "without wanting to" is the point, but "saw" is still a filter.
   - Line 21: "She registered it through the cascade" -- "registered" is a filter variant.
   - Line 23: "she felt in her kneecaps, her femurs" -- classic filter construction.
   - Line 93: "Ada felt it in her chest" -- classic filter.
   - Line 153: "a vibration she felt in her teeth" -- filter.
   - Line 171: "pulsing in the rhythm she felt in her sternum" -- filter.
   The chapter has approximately 7 filter word instances in the prose section. Given the chapter's focus on perception, some are harder to avoid, but the density is higher than previous revised chapters. Fix: rephrase the most straightforward cases. For example, line 93 could become "The third question arrived like the third strike of a chisel on the same fault line. It landed in her chest."

## Minor Issues (nice to fix)

7. **"The way" construction density -- 13 instances across the prose.**
   While this was flagged and reduced in Ch. 6 revision (from 21 to 11), Ch. 7 has 13 instances in the prose section alone. Many serve essential comparisons (e.g., "the way veins branched," "the way ice releases a stone in spring"), but the density is noticeable. 8. **Missing explicit cedar resin/smell detail during Zone 4 transition.**
   The chapter brief's sensory palette (line 101) specifies: "The resinous scent of larger trees (approaching cedars)." Line 147 notes the cedar bark is "fragrant even through the spore-thickness" which partially addresses this, but the transition from Douglas fir to western red cedar (lines 145-149) could benefit from a more explicit olfactory beat matching the brief's specification. Currently the cedar is noted as "fragrant" but the specific "cedar resin" smell from the sensory palette is not named.

9. **Owl wingspan described as "Three feet, four" (line 133).**
   A northern spotted owl's wingspan is typically 42-45 inches (approximately 3.5-3.75 feet). "Three feet, four" (i.e., 3-4 feet) is within biological range but reads as slightly casual for the level of precision elsewhere. Under psilocybin amplification, the wingspan appearing larger is expected. This is not an error but worth noting: the "four" at the upper end is consistent with amplification.

10. **No explicit mention of breath visibility in the cold air during the owl encounter.**
    Ch. 5 (stag encounter) established visible breath in cold air as a recurring detail. Ch. 7's owl clearing is similarly cold and Ada is breathing audibly (line 119: "each uneven breath"). The absence of visible breath here is not an error -- the bioluminescence-only lighting makes breath less visible than in moonlight -- but it is a slight departure from the established sensory pattern during cold encounters. The brief does not require it, so this is truly optional.

11. **The "closed the door" phrase on line 85 could trigger the door-imagery-reduction concern.**
    Ch. 5's revision log specifically notes reducing door imagery ("Changed 'like a door closing in an empty house' to 'hollow and final' to avoid triple door imagery in one chapter"). Line 85 includes "the person at the kitchen table who had smiled and said *of course* and closed the door." This is a callback to the Ch. 5 memory, so the door image is justified here. But note that the overall door/locked-door motif frequency should be tracked across the manuscript.
