# Continuity Review: Chapter 8 "Roots"

## CRITICAL Issues (must fix before proceeding)

### 1. Water bottle described as "empty" before Ada drinks the last inch
On line 45, when Ada sets the backpack down and the narration inventories its contents, the water bottle is called "the empty water bottle." However, Ada does not drink the last inch until line 147, roughly 100 lines later. Per the running state at Ch. 7 close, the bottle has "approximately one inch remaining." The first reference must say "nearly empty water bottle" or similar. The later scene (line 147) where she drinks the last inch and caps the empty bottle is correct -- it is only the premature "empty" label on line 45 that breaks continuity.

### 2. Frog toe count: "four on each foot" is biologically incorrect
Line 107: "Its toes gripped the bark and the mycelium simultaneously, four on each foot." Western toads (Anaxyrus boreas) have four toes on the front feet but five toes on the hind feet. Per world.md and style-guide.md, the story should start from accurate real biology before applying psychedelic amplification. Fix by either removing the specific count ("its toes gripped the bark and the mycelium simultaneously") or correcting to reflect the asymmetry if detail is desired.

## MODERATE Issues (should fix)

### 3. Devil's club scratches not referenced in prose
The running state lists devil's club scratches on Ada's left forearm (wrist to elbow, thin red lines with embedded spines) and right hand (swollen ridge between thumb and index finger). The continuity notes section mentions them, but the prose body of the chapter never references these injuries. Given that Ada presses her palms flat against the mycelium mat multiple times, the swollen ridge on her right hand and the scratches on her forearm would likely register as sensory detail -- warmth against irritated skin, the embedded spines reacting to pressure or warmth. A brief tactile reference during the palm-pressing scenes would maintain physical continuity.

### 4. Rain jacket state during the warmth sequence
The chapter correctly references the rain jacket multiple times (lines 13, 41, 49, 173). However, Zone 4 is described as notably warmer, and Ada's muscles are releasing tension, shivering is easing. The brief calls this out as a consideration: "In Zone 4 (warm, humid), she might push it off or open it." The chapter keeps the jacket on, which is reasonable, but given that Ada spends an extended time sitting in warmth and the jacket is described as "a wet rag" (line 41), some acknowledgment of whether she adjusts it (unzips, pushes sleeves up) would strengthen the physical tracking. This is moderate because keeping it on is plausible, but the warmth-release sequence is detailed enough that the jacket's wet-cold layer against her warming body is a missed sensory opportunity.

## MINOR Issues (nice to fix)

Check against Ch. 6 prose for the snake's voice description and adjust if needed.

The brief specifically calls for dehydration effects: "dry mouth, possibly lightheaded." Ada has been in the forest 7+ hours with minimal water, and the water bottle has been nearly empty since Ch. 6. While the chapter captures exhaustion thoroughly, explicit dehydration symptoms (dry mouth, cracked lips, lightheadedness) are absent from the prose. The water-drinking scene (line 147) would be a natural moment to register how dry her mouth is, how the warm stale water feels against parched tissue. This would also heighten the relief when she reaches the dawn stream in Ch. 12.

### 7. Jeans torn at right knee -- not referenced in prose
The running state tracks that Ada's jeans are torn at the right knee (from Ch. 2). When she sinks to the ground and her legs fold beneath her (line 45), the torn knee would likely contact the mycelium mat directly -- bare skin against the warm, spongy threadwork. This is a missed sensory detail that would cost only a clause and would reinforce the physical accumulation.

### 8. Boots "waterlogged" detail could be reinforced
The running state tracks waterlogged boots with saturated socks. Line 41 mentions "waterlogged boots" in passing. When Ada walks on the mycelium mat and her footsteps send waves of light, the squelching of waterlogged boots on the spongy surface is a missed sound detail that would layer nicely with the bioluminescent response.
