# Chapter Outline

## Structure Overview

- **Act I -- Descent (Chapters 1-3):** Entry, onset, disorientation, first encounters. Ada's control mechanisms fail.
- **Act II -- Confrontation (Chapters 4-10):** Escalating encounters that deepen in intimacy. Each chapter strips a layer of defense.
- **Act III -- Integration (Chapters 11-13):** Surrender, revelation, emergence. Dawn approaches. Ada emerges whole, not triumphant.

**Total chapters:** 13
**Estimated word count:** 75,000-90,000 words
**POV:** Close third person, locked to Ada. Past tense (with limited present-tense passages during peak psychedelic intensity).

---

## Chapter 1 -- "Threshold"

- **Purpose:** Establish Ada, the forest, the psychedelic onset, and the first crack in her control (the trail vanishes). Plant the frog.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Clinical interior voice still intact.
- **Zone:** Zone 1 (The Threshold)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada arrives at twilight, sits on nurse log, eats the Psilocybe cyanescens
  - First perceptual shifts begin (surfaces breathing, colors saturating, canopy warping)
  - The trail vanishes; Ada cannot retrace her steps
- **Emotional arc:** Controlled determination --> hairline crack of unease
- **Beginning state:** Tense but composed. She planned this. She is managing the experience before it starts.
- **Ending state:** Unsettled. The trail is gone. The onset is faster and stranger than expected. Confidence fractured.
- **Connection to next chapter:** The trail's disappearance triggers the anxious spiral that summons the swarm.

---

## Chapter 2 -- "Swarm"

- **Purpose:** First animal encounter. Externalize Ada's anxiety. Exhaust her first defense (strategic thinking/problem-solving). Transition into Zone 2.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Interior voice accelerates, fragments.
- **Zone:** Zone 1 transitioning to Zone 2 (The Tangle)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada tries to retrace her steps; the forest has shifted
  - The insect swarm materializes as her anxiety spirals
  - Swarm disperses only when Ada stops running and drops to her knees
- **Emotional arc:** Forced calm --> spiraling panic --> exhausted stillness
- **Beginning state:** Trying to problem-solve. Mind accelerating. Surface composure over mounting dread.
- **Ending state:** Shaken, scratched by devil's club, forced calm broken. In the Tangle now. Knows she cannot think her way out.
- **Connection to next chapter:** Ada is in survival mode -- running on fear, not thought. This primal state summons the wolf.

---

## Chapter 3 -- "Teeth"

- **Purpose:** Wolf encounter -- confront Ada's chronic fear/hypervigilance. Introduce the frog as active guide. Move Ada to the ravine threshold.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Short, clipped rhythm during wolf encounter.
- **Zone:** Zone 2 (The Tangle)
- **Key events:**
  - The wolf materializes, circles Ada. She tries survival responses; none work.
  - Ada collapses to her knees from exhaustion. The wolf approaches, then departs.
  - The frog appears (2nd appearance) and leads Ada through the tangle to the ravine
- **Emotional arc:** Primal fear / adrenaline --> exhaustion --> faint recognition that fear itself held the wolf there
- **Beginning state:** Fight-or-flight. Hypervigilant. Running on survival instinct after the swarm.
- **Ending state:** Drained. Adrenaline crash. Following the frog on instinct. At the ravine's edge.
- **Connection to next chapter:** The frog has led Ada to the ravine -- the threshold between Zone 2 and Zone 3. She must cross alone.

---

## Chapter 4 -- "Crossing"

- **Purpose:** Valley chapter. Physical respite. Transition from Zone 2 to Zone 3. Shift Ada from "lost" to "tested." Process the wolf encounter. Reassemble composure (which summons the stag).
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Rhythm opens slightly -- longer sentences, more body awareness.
- **Zone:** Transition from Zone 2 to Zone 3 (The Hollow)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada descends the ravine and crosses the cold stream (falls, goes under briefly)
  - On the far bank, the forest changes: undergrowth thins, clearings open, bioluminescence intensifies
  - Ada processes: drinks water, examines scratches, wrings out clothes. A wary alertness replaces panic.
- **Emotional arc:** Depleted numbness --> the shock of cold water --> wary calm --> composure quietly reassembling
- **Beginning state:** Drained from the wolf, guided by instinct. Running on fumes.
- **Ending state:** Cold, wet, but alert. The crossing has rinsed something off her. She stands straighter. The old armor begins to snap back into place.
- **Connection to next chapter:** Ada's reassembled composure -- the performance of "I can handle this" -- summons the stag.

---

## Chapter 5 -- "Antlers"

- **Purpose:** Stag encounter -- confront Ada's pride and false control. Crack the mask of composure. Escalate from survival-level encounters to identity-level.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Prose tightens during the staring standoff.
- **Zone:** Zone 3 (The Hollow)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada enters a moonlit clearing. The Roosevelt elk stands motionless, antlers branching impossibly wide.
  - The stag mirrors Ada's composure. She tries to out-composure it. She cannot sustain the performance.
  - The stag turns and departs; its antlers shed fragments as it goes. The crown was always weight.
- **Emotional arc:** Reassembled composure --> recognition --> exposure --> the mask shown to be a mask
- **Beginning state:** Standing straighter. Telling herself she is fine. Armor back in place.
- **Ending state:** Exposed. The armor is still there but she has seen it from the outside. Ambiguity about whether her composure is strength or defense.
- **Connection to next chapter:** The mask is cracked. What it was guarding -- shame -- can now surface. The snake waits at the Black Pond.

---

## Chapter 6 -- "Skin"

- **Purpose:** Snake encounter -- surface Ada's shame wound. Introduce the possibility of shedding. Frog as comforter (3rd appearance).
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Intimate, close, slow rhythm. Physical sensation dominant.
- **Zone:** Zone 3 (The Hollow) -- at the Black Pond
- **Key events:**
  - Ada finds the Black Pond; her reflection does things she is not doing
  - The snake appears. Shame surfaces as sensory fragments (heat in face, coiling gut, memory shards)
  - The snake sheds its skin. The frog sits with Ada at the pond's edge -- presence without action
- **Emotional arc:** Exposed vulnerability --> nausea of shame --> forced stillness --> the image of shedding as possibility
- **Beginning state:** Mask cracked from the stag. Transparent and undefended.
- **Ending state:** Nauseated with surfaced shame. But carrying the image of the shed skin: transformation through release.
- **Connection to next chapter:** Ada is raw and open. The psilocybin is intensifying toward peak. Her heightened perception activates the owl.

---

## Chapter 7 -- "Eyes"

- **Purpose:** Owl encounter -- confront Ada's painful knowledge and weaponized perception. Collapse her analytical distance. Bridge from Zone 3 to Zone 4. First involuntary tears.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Brief present-tense passage possible during the owl's gaze. Interior monologue begins to fray.
- **Zone:** Zone 3 approaching Zone 4
- **Key events:**
  - Pattern recognition amplifying painfully. Ada sees connections everywhere.
  - The spotted owl pins Ada with mirror-eyes. Memory fragments of seeing too much, too clearly.
  - Ada weeps involuntarily. The owl lifts away and leads her deeper toward Zone 4.
- **Emotional arc:** Hyper-awareness --> paralysis of seeing --> exhaustion of perception --> involuntary tears as release
- **Beginning state:** Raw from the snake. Defenses thinning. Psilocybin nearing peak.
- **Ending state:** Weeping, following the owl deeper. Analytical distance collapsed. She is inside her experience now, not above it.
- **Connection to next chapter:** The owl leads Ada toward Zone 4. She follows without choosing. The deepest territory -- and the midpoint reversal -- await.

---

## Chapter 8 -- "Roots"

- **Purpose:** Midpoint reversal. Ada stops trying to escape. Entry to Zone 4. Mycelium revelation. Frog as teacher of between-states (4th appearance). Lead Ada toward the boar.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense with possible brief present-tense fractures. Interior voice quiets. Sensory description dominates.
- **Zone:** Zone 4 (The Root)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada enters Zone 4: ancient cedars, visible mycelium mat, the hum. Peak psilocybin.
  - She sits against a cedar and stops resisting. Not a decision -- a collapse of alternatives.
  - The frog appears on a root, half on root, half on mycelium. Teaches between-states. Then leads Ada deeper.
- **Emotional arc:** Near-collapse --> surrender of resistance --> porosity --> quiet willingness to go deeper
- **Beginning state:** Following the owl, weeping, nearing total exhaustion. Thinnest defenses.
- **Ending state:** No longer trying to escape. Part of the forest. Breathing with it. Following the frog willingly toward what comes next.
- **Connection to next chapter:** The frog leads Ada toward the boar. She follows willingly because she has stopped resisting. The deepest confrontation -- grief -- requires this openness.

---

## Chapter 9 -- "Charge"

- **Purpose:** Crisis. The boar encounter -- Ada's unprocessed grief breaks through. Somatic release. The emotional climax of the animal sequence.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Rhythm shifts from quiet (opening) to raw, fragmented intensity during the charge, then to slow, heavy aftermath.
- **Zone:** Zone 4 (The Root)
- **Key events:**
  - The boar appears -- massive, tusked, eyes wet with pain. It charges the environment, then faces Ada.
  - Ada does not run (accumulated learning from all prior encounters). Grief erupts. Body-wracking sobs.
  - The boar lies down beside Ada. They rest together on the torn-up forest floor.
- **Emotional arc:** Open vulnerability --> the grief arriving full-force --> involuntary somatic release --> hollowed but spacious
- **Beginning state:** Open, surrendered, following the frog deeper. Vulnerable but not yet broken.
- **Ending state:** Broken open. The grief has moved through her body. Hollowed, lying in dirt, but the hollow feels like space where something can grow.
- **Connection to next chapter:** All emotion has been stripped. What remains is the architecture of identity itself -- the stories Ada tells about who she is. The spider targets this final structure.

---

## Chapter 10 -- "Web"

- **Purpose:** Spider encounter -- confront Ada's self-entrapment, the identity-narrative she has woven. Final defense falls. Strip Ada to the bare self.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Slow, measured, almost meditative rhythm.
- **Zone:** Zone 4 (The Root)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada walks into an orb web strung between cedars. Under psilocybin, each strand is a self-narrative.
  - The spider waits at the center -- patient, precise. Ada recognizes she is both spider and fly.
  - Ada stops struggling. The web loosens strand by strand. Not destroyed -- released.
- **Emotional arc:** Dissociated walking --> entrapment --> existential recognition --> stillness within the web --> loosening
- **Beginning state:** Hollowed from the boar. Grief has passed through. Walking without defenses.
- **Ending state:** Stripped of identity-narrative. No longer the competent one, the tough one, the fine one. What remains is breath, heartbeat, and the forest.
- **Connection to next chapter:** All defenses are gone. All animal-demons have been faced. What remains is the act of true surrender.

---

## Chapter 11 -- "Dissolve"

- **Purpose:** Climax. True surrender and ego dissolution. The mycelium network revelation. Integration of all fragments. Frog as witness (5th appearance). First hint of dawn.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense dominant, with controlled present-tense passages during dissolution. Prose fractures briefly: merged perception, synesthesia, blurred syntax.
- **Zone:** Zone 4 (deepest point), beginning transition toward Zone 5
- **Key events:**
  - Ada lies on the mycelium mat. Boundaries dissolve. Heartbeat becomes mycelium pulse.
  - She perceives the Armillaria network -- the ancient organism connecting everything. She is a node.
  - Ada weeps with relief. The frog witnesses. First pre-dawn gray appears. Ada rises.
- **Emotional arc:** Emptied stillness --> dissolution of self/forest boundary --> oceanic recognition --> relief --> rising
- **Beginning state:** Stripped, dismantled, lying on the forest floor with nothing left.
- **Ending state:** Reassembled differently. Boundaries returning but permeable. Ada-the-membrane, not Ada-the-fortress. Beginning to move upward.
- **Connection to next chapter:** Ada is rising out of the deep forest. The trip is declining. Dawn approaches. Integration settles into the body.

---

## Chapter 12 -- "Stream"

- **Purpose:** Integration embodied. Quiet aftermath. The walk from Zone 4 to Zone 5. Dawn transition. Frog as companion (6th appearance). Physical reckoning.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Clean, warm prose. Simple sentences with room to breathe.
- **Zone:** Transition from Zone 4 to Zone 5 (The Clearing)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada walks upward through transitioning forest. Bioluminescence fades as dawn light arrives; both coexist briefly.
  - She finds the dawn stream. Drinks. Washes her face. Examines her body -- scratched, torn, exhausted, alive.
  - The dawn chorus begins. The frog hops along the stream bank beside her. Companionship, not guidance.
- **Emotional arc:** Quiet wakefulness --> tenderness toward the world and herself --> the grief held as river, not stone --> beauty that is almost unbearable
- **Beginning state:** Reassembled, permeable, moving upward. Psilocybin declining. The world becoming specific again.
- **Ending state:** Clean(er). Awake. Grief still present but flowing. The clearing is ahead.
- **Connection to next chapter:** The forest is opening. The clearing -- and emergence -- await.

---

## Chapter 13 -- "Clearing"

- **Purpose:** Resolution. Emergence into ordinary world. Final frog appearance (7th). The forest object. Ada walks into her life, changed.
- **POV:** Close third, Ada. Past tense. Clean, direct, grounded. The prose has arrived where Ada has arrived: simple and true.
- **Zone:** Zone 5 (The Clearing)
- **Key events:**
  - Ada steps into the meadow. Gold light. Wood sorrel and wild violet. She stands and breathes.
  - The frog sits on a stone. Just a toad. It trills once. Sacred and ordinary collapsed into one.
  - Ada finds the forest object in her pocket. She walks toward the trailhead. She does not look back.
- **Emotional arc:** Quiet wholeness --> tenderness --> the grief held gently --> release without conclusion
- **Beginning state:** Quiet, awake, permeable. Psilocybin at afterglow. Tired in body, open in chest.
- **Ending state:** Whole. Wounded. Awake. Walking into the ordinary world carrying everything the night gave her.
- **Connection:** The clearing mirrors the threshold (Ch. 1) -- both are liminal spaces. But the clearing is open where the threshold was closed. The loop is a spiral: Ada returns to the surface changed.
