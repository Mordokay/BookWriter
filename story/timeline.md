# Timeline

## Ada's Backstory (Fragmentary)

Ada's past is not told in sequence. It surfaces in shards during the animal encounters -- triggered by sensory details, emotional resonance, or the psychedelic layer pulling buried material to the surface. The following fragments are the raw material. Each is linked to the animal that will activate it.

### The Wound Map

- **Fear / hypervigilance (Wolf):** Ada grew up in an unstable household. Not violent in the cinematic sense, but unpredictable. A parent whose moods shifted without warning. Ada learned to scan for danger constantly, to read rooms before entering them, to keep her body ready to react. This survival instinct served her as a child but has calcified into chronic anxiety and an inability to feel safe.

- **Pride / emotional armor (Stag):** Ada is intelligent and capable. She built an identity around competence -- the person who manages, who does not need help, who holds it together. This mask formed in response to chaos: if she could control herself perfectly, she could survive anything. But the mask has become a prison. She cannot ask for help. She cannot be seen struggling. She performs composure even when she is drowning.

- **Shame (Snake):** Something Ada did or failed to do that she carries as a core wound. This is the most guarded secret. It may involve betraying someone's trust, or failing to act when action was needed, or a choice she made that she cannot forgive. The specific content should remain fragmentary even to the reader -- shame resists full articulation. What matters is the physical sensation: the coiling in the gut, the heat in the face, the impulse to hide.

- **Painful knowledge (Owl):** Ada sees too much. She has always been perceptive -- reading people, understanding dynamics, sensing what is unspoken. This is a gift that wounds. She saw things in her family that a child should not have had to understand. She sees patterns in her relationships that she cannot unsee. The owl encounter activates the burden of awareness without the power to change what you see.

- **Hardened grief (Boar):** A loss Ada has not processed. Someone she loved -- a grandparent, a friend, possibly the parent whose instability shaped her -- is gone, and Ada converted the grief into toughness. She did not mourn. She got through it. She kept moving. The grief is still there, calcified into something blunt and heavy that she carries without acknowledging.

- **Anxiety / intrusive thoughts (Insect Swarm):** Ada's mind turns on itself. Racing thoughts, worst-case scenarios, the constant internal monologue of self-criticism. This is not a single event but a pattern -- the accumulated weight of hypervigilance, perfectionism, and unprocessed emotion manifesting as mental overwhelm. The swarm is what her mind sounds like when she cannot quiet it.

- **Self-entrapment (Spider):** The stories Ada tells herself about who she is. The identity she has constructed -- capable, independent, fine -- is also a web she cannot escape. She has defined herself so rigidly that she cannot change without feeling like she is dying. The spider encounter forces her to see the patterns she has woven and to recognize that she is both the spider and the fly.

### What We Do Not Know

- Ada's exact age (late twenties to early thirties).
- Her specific profession (something that rewards competence and control -- possibly academic, medical, or organizational).
- The full details of the shame wound. The story gives fragments, not a case file.
- Whether the unstable parent is alive or dead. Both are possible. If dead, this connects to the boar's grief wound. If alive, the grief may be for the parent Ada needed but never had.
- Whether Ada has been in therapy. She may have, unsuccessfully -- she is too good at performing insight without actually surrendering control. Or she may have avoided it entirely, preferring to manage alone.

## Immediate Pre-Story

### The Days Before

Ada drove to the forest alone. She has been coming apart slowly -- not in a dramatic, visible way, but in the way a foundation cracks before a building shows damage. Sleep has been difficult. Concentration has been difficult. The mask of competence is requiring more and more energy to maintain. She has been functioning, but the functioning has become the entire effort, leaving nothing for actual living.

She heard about the mushrooms from someone she trusts (a friend, an acquaintance with experience -- this person is mentioned only in passing, never named or described in detail). She obtained them in advance. This was not impulsive. She planned it. She chose this forest because she had been here before, years ago, and it stayed with her -- the size of the trees, the quality of the silence, the feeling of being inside something ancient.

### The Evening Of

- Ada parks her car at a trailhead as the sun is setting. Late autumn -- the days are short. Sunset is around 5:00 PM.
- She leaves her phone in the car. Deliberately. She locks the car and puts the keys in her backpack.
- She walks into the forest on a maintained trail. The trail is familiar for the first quarter mile. Then it branches and she takes the less-traveled fork.
- She finds a spot -- a nurse log in a small clearing, moss-covered, with a view of the canopy and the darkening sky.
- She sits down. She takes the mushrooms out of the paper bag. Psilocybe cyanescens, dried. She eats them slowly, chewing through the woody, slightly bitter texture. She drinks water.
- She waits. The onset takes 20 to 40 minutes. During this time, the forest darkens. Stars appear in canopy gaps. The first sounds of the night forest begin: insects, a distant owl call, the drip of moisture from leaves.
- The trip begins.

## The Journey's Time Structure

### Real Time vs. Experienced Time

The entire journey unfolds over one night: approximately sundown (5:00 PM) to dawn (7:00 AM) -- roughly 14 hours. Within that span, Ada experiences what feels like much longer. The psilocybin distorts duration so profoundly that she cannot track real time at all. Some encounters that take minutes feel like hours. Some transitions that take hours feel like moments.

The moon is the only external time marker, and even it is unreliable -- Ada may perceive it jumping position or hovering in place.

### The Wave Pattern Within the Night

The trip does not build in a straight line. It oscillates. Between major encounters, there are valleys -- moments where the intensity recedes and Ada can catch her breath, process what happened, and move through transitional space. These valleys are essential for pacing. They prevent the book from being 13 chapters of unrelenting intensity. They also mirror real psilocybin experience, which comes in waves.

**Wave structure mapped to chapters:**

| Wave | Rising | Crest | Valley |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wave 1 | Ch. 1 (onset, disorientation) | Ch. 2-3 (swarm + wolf) | Ch. 4 (crossing, respite) |
| Wave 2 | Ch. 5 (stag, exposure) | Ch. 6-7 (snake + owl) | Ch. 8 (roots, surrender) |
| Wave 3 | Ch. 9 (boar, grief) | Ch. 10 (spider, entrapment) | -- |
| Final wave | Ch. 11 (dissolution, climax) | -- | Ch. 12-13 (integration, dawn) |

Note: Wave 3 has no valley because the boar-spider sequence is the darkest stretch. The absence of a valley between these encounters creates the sustained pressure necessary for the crisis. The relief comes only in Chapter 11, and it comes as surrender, not as respite.

## Causality Notes

### How Ada's Emotional State Triggers Each Encounter

The encounters are not random. Each animal appears when Ada's emotional state activates the corresponding wound. The state that triggers each encounter is produced by the previous encounter's aftermath:

- **Insect swarm (Ch. 2):** **Trigger state -- racing mind.** Ada realizes she is lost. Her problem-solving mind accelerates into anxiety spiral. The swarm manifests from/mirrors the texture of her spiraling thoughts. **Causal link from Ch. 1:** The trail vanishing removes Ada's illusion of control, activating her anxious pattern-matching.

- **Wolf (Ch. 3):** **Trigger state -- primal fear and flight mode.** After the swarm, Ada is no longer thinking; she is running. Pure survival mode. The wolf is drawn to (or manifests from) this fight-or-flight energy. **Causal link from Ch. 2:** The swarm broke her cognitive defense. She dropped from mind to body, from planning to fleeing. Fear without strategy summons the wolf.

- **Stag (Ch. 5):** **Trigger state -- reassembled composure.** After the ravine crossing (Ch. 4), Ada recovers enough to stand straighter, wring out her clothes, tell herself she can handle this. The old armor snaps back into place. This performance of control summons the stag. **Causal link from Ch. 4:** The physical respite of the crossing allows Ada's habitual defense (composure, competence) to reassert itself. The stag mirrors what she has put back on.

- **Snake (Ch. 6):** **Trigger state -- exposed, undefended stillness.** The stag encounter showed Ada her armor is a mask. She is exposed. She is forced into stillness by terrain (the Black Pond blocks passage). In stillness without armor, shame surfaces. The snake emerges when defenses are down and movement stops. **Causal link from Ch. 5:** The stag stripped the armor. Without it, Ada is vulnerable to the wound the armor was protecting.

- **Owl (Ch. 7):** **Trigger state -- raw, perceptually heightened, psilocybin nearing peak.** After shame (the snake), Ada's pattern recognition is amplifying uncontrollably. She is seeing everything too clearly. The owl manifests in response to this hyper-awareness, reflecting it back. **Causal link from Ch. 6:** Shame opened Ada further. With her hidden wound surfaced, her habitual defense of perception/awareness is all she has left. The owl targets this final cognitive refuge.

- **Boar (Ch. 9):** **Trigger state -- open, surrendered, the frog has led her deeper.** After the midpoint reversal (Ch. 8), Ada has stopped fighting. She is porous, raw, undefended. The boar appears because grief can only surface when every defense has been laid down. Running, composure, hiding, and awareness all had to fail first. **Causal link from Ch. 8:** Surrender creates the conditions for the deepest wound. Ada is no longer blocking anything. Grief, the most deeply buried emotion, finally has room to surface.

- **Spider (Ch. 10):** **Trigger state -- hollowed, disassembled, identity loosened.** After the boar broke her open with grief, Ada is at her most formless. This is exactly when identity-narrative tries to reassemble. The spider represents the structural pattern that keeps rebuilding itself even after being shattered. **Causal link from Ch. 9:** Grief dismantled Ada's content (what she feels). The spider targets her container (how she organizes feeling). The pattern of self-reconstruction is the final defense.

### Encounter Escalation Logic

The encounters escalate in INTIMACY, not just scariness:

| Encounter | What is Targeted | Intimacy Level | How Ada Engages |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Insect swarm (Ch. 2) | Surface anxiety, racing mind | Surface | Panics, runs, eventually freezes |
| 2. Wolf (Ch. 3) | Primal fear, survival mode | Body | Tries survival tactics, exhaustion forces yielding |
| 3. Stag (Ch. 5) | Identity performance, pride | Persona | Tries to hold composure, is seen through |
| 4. Snake (Ch. 6) | Shame, hidden wound | Private self | Forced into stillness, cannot hide |
| 5. Owl (Ch. 7) | Painful knowledge, hyper-awareness | Existential | Cannot look away, weeps |
| 6. Boar (Ch. 9) | Unprocessed grief, body-level pain | Somatic | Does not run, receives the charge |
| 7. Spider (Ch. 10) | Self-entrapment, identity structure | Structural | Stops struggling, sits within the web |

Each encounter requires something the previous one taught. The wolf teaches that running escalates; Ada uses this against the boar. The stag teaches that armor is visible; Ada is visible to the owl. The snake teaches that hidden things surface in stillness; Ada sits still in the spider's web. Learning is cumulative and embodied, not intellectual.

### The Frog's Causality (Refined)

The frog appears at transitional moments. Its timing is linked to Ada's readiness for the next stage:

| Appearance | Chapter | Ada's State | What the Frog Does | What This Enables |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| 2nd | Ch. 3 | Post-wolf, drained, on her knees | Croaks, leads Ada through the tangle to the ravine | Guides Ada from the tangle (being lost) to the hollow (being tested). Physically moves her to the next zone. |

**Additional frog signals** (not full appearances):

- Ch. 9: The frog led Ada to the boar (at end of Ch. 8) and then withdrew. Absence during the boar encounter is intentional -- this is Ada's work.

## Continuity Risks

### Light and Darkness

- The story begins at twilight and ends at dawn. The vast majority takes place in darkness, with moonlight and bioluminescence as the only light sources.
- **Moon phase: waxing gibbous (approximately three-quarter moon).** This provides enough light for navigation in canopy gaps without eliminating the power of darkness. The moon rises in the east around mid-afternoon (already up at story start) and sets in the west in the early morning hours (approximately 3:00-4:00 AM). This means moonlight is available for the first two-thirds of the journey, then the darkest period coincides with Zone 4 (Chapters 8-11), where bioluminescence is the primary light source. Moon-set deepens the darkness exactly when the story is at its deepest.
- Dawn arrives gradually. The first gray light should appear in the east around 5:30-6:00 AM (late autumn). The transition from bioluminescence to dawn light is a key visual moment in Chapter 12.

### Physical Injuries and Conditions

These must accumulate and persist:

- **Devil's club scratches** (Ch. 2, Zone 2): On her hands and forearms. The fine spines break off in skin and itch. These should still be present and irritating in later chapters. Referenced when Ada examines herself (Ch. 4, Ch. 12).
- **Mud:** Accumulates progressively. From kneeling after the wolf (Ch. 3), from falling in the stream (Ch. 4), from the forest floor throughout. By Zone 4, Ada is caked in it -- hands, knees, face, clothing. She does not magically clean up. Partial rinsing at the dawn stream (Ch. 12).
- **Wet clothing:** From rain and the ravine stream crossing (Ch. 4). Wet clothing stays wet in a damp forest. Ada is cold when she is still, less so when moving. Her rain jacket (if she retrieves it from her backpack) helps but does not eliminate this. Zone 4 is warmer (mycelium activity, enclosed space), providing some relief.
- **Exhaustion:** Builds throughout. By Zone 4 (Ch. 8), Ada has been walking, climbing, crawling, and emotionally processing for 7+ hours. Her body is depleted. This should affect her movement, her posture, her breathing. The exhaustion is part of why the midpoint reversal happens -- she literally cannot keep fighting.
- **Dehydration:** She has a half-full water bottle. She drinks from it in Ch. 4 and Ch. 6. By Ch. 8, it is nearly empty. She drinks from the dawn stream in Ch. 12. Between those, she has limited water.
- **Bare skin exposure:** If clothing tears, exposed skin stays exposed. Scratches from devil's club and undergrowth on arms. Torn knees on jeans possible.
- **Tears:** Ada weeps in Ch. 7 (involuntary, from the owl), Ch. 9 (somatic grief), and Ch. 11 (relief). Tear tracks through mud/grime on her face are a visual detail.

### Objects Ada Carries

Track these throughout:

- **Backpack:** Contains water bottle, lighter, rain jacket, car keys. She may set the backpack down during intense encounters but should keep it through the journey. If she removes it in one chapter, note when she retrieves it. The backpack's weight and presence should be referenced when relevant.
- **Water bottle:** Half full at start. Referenced drinks: Ch. 4 (after crossing), Ch. 6 (after snake, at Black Pond). Nearly empty by Ch. 8. Empty or discarded by Ch. 11. Ada drinks from the dawn stream in Ch. 12.
- **Paper bag (mushrooms):** Discarded after ingestion in Ch. 1. May reappear as a spatial-disorientation signal (finding it again far from where she dropped it -- a brief, unsettling detail in Ch. 2 or 3), or may simply be gone.
- **Car keys:** In the backpack. Not referenced during the journey, but present at dawn (Ch. 13) -- confirming she can leave.
- **Lighter:** In the backpack. Never used. Referenced in Ch. 13 as inventory of what she carries out.
- **Rain jacket:** In the backpack. Ada may put it on during rain (Ch. 3-4 territory, or whenever intermittent rain occurs). If she puts it on, it stays on (wet, muddied, streaked).
- **Forest object:** Ada discovers a small object in her jacket pocket in Ch. 13 -- a stone, bark fragment, or piece of shed snakeskin she does not remember picking up. This should be planted earlier: a brief moment in Ch. 6 or Ch. 9 where Ada's hand closes around something without her conscious awareness.

### Temporal Consistency

- Even though Ada's experienced time is distorted, the chapter sequencing maintains a coherent order of events. The zone progression is: 1 -> 2 -> (ravine) -> 3 -> 4 -> 5. No backtracking to earlier zones.
- The psychedelic intensity follows the wave structure. It cannot be at peak intensity in Chapter 2 and at onset intensity in Chapter 8. The general arc is: rising (Ch. 1-3), high with oscillations (Ch. 4-7), peak (Ch. 8-10), extended plateau and decline (Ch. 11-12), afterglow (Ch. 13).
- Animal encounters escalate in emotional difficulty. The order is fixed: swarm, wolf, stag, snake, owl, boar, spider. Each encounter requires the emotional groundwork of the previous one.
- Time-of-night details should track loosely with the pharmacological arc. The moon is overhead in early chapters, lower in the sky by Ch. 7, and has set by Ch. 8-9. The darkest period (no moonlight, bioluminescence only) is Ch. 8-11. First gray light appears in Ch. 11 (end). Dawn fills Ch. 12-13.

### Weather

- Light intermittent rain is present during portions of the journey. It begins during Ch. 3-4 territory (the tangle and ravine crossing) and stops. May return briefly in Ch. 6-7. It should not be constant throughout.
- Fog may appear in low-lying areas, especially near the Black Pond (Ch. 6) and in Zone 4 (Ch. 8-10).
- Temperature drops through the night. The coldest point is pre-dawn (3:00-5:00 AM, aligning with Ch. 10-11). Zone 4 is an exception -- the mycelium activity and enclosed space make it warmer.
- No wind in the deep forest. The canopy blocks it. Wind may be present at the forest edge (Zone 1, Ch. 1) and the clearing (Zone 5, Ch. 13).

### Sound Continuity

- The insect chorus changes through the night. Different species are active at different times. The chorus should thin in the coldest hours (Ch. 10-11) and rebuild toward dawn (Ch. 12-13).
- The varied thrush calls at dusk (Ch. 1) and dawn (Ch. 12-13). It should not call at midnight.
- The dawn chorus (multiple bird species) begins in Ch. 12 and continues into Ch. 13. It should not occur before Ch. 12.
- The frog's trill can occur at any time -- Western toads call throughout the night, especially near water. Background frog and toad calls are distinct from the specific guide-toad's appearances.
- The mycelium hum appears gradually. It is:
  - Absent in Zone 1 (Ch. 1-2)
  - Barely perceptible in Zone 2 (Ch. 3)
  - Faintly present in Zone 3, growing (Ch. 5-7)
  - Dominant in Zone 4 (Ch. 8-11)
  - Fading in Zone 5 (Ch. 12-13), more memory than vibration by the clearing
- Silence -- true silence in the forest is rare and alarming. When it occurs, something is about to happen. The wolf encounter (Ch. 3) is preceded by sudden silence. The boar encounter (Ch. 9) may also be preceded by a deepening of the quiet.

### Emotional Continuity

- Ada's emotional state must progress, not reset. A breakthrough in one encounter should affect how she enters the next. Specific carry-forward effects:
  - After the swarm (Ch. 2): Ada knows her mind is unreliable. She shifts from cognitive strategies to physical survival.
  - After the wolf (Ch. 3): Ada's body has learned that yielding can release. She does not consciously know this yet.
  - After the stag (Ch. 5): Ada's composure is cracked. She can no longer automatically reassemble her mask.
  - After the snake (Ch. 6): Shame has surfaced. Ada is more raw, more open, less able to deflect.
  - After the owl (Ch. 7): Ada has wept. The tears broke something. She is following rather than leading for the first time.
  - After the midpoint (Ch. 8): Ada is no longer trying to escape. This is irreversible.
  - After the boar (Ch. 9): Grief has moved through her body. She is hollowed but not empty.
  - After the spider (Ch. 10): Identity-narrative has loosened. Ada is undefined.
  - After dissolution (Ch. 11): Integration has occurred. The emotional direction is now toward peace, not toward further breaking.

- The frog's teachings accumulate. Each appearance builds on the last. The frog's final appearance (Ch. 13) should resonate with everything it has shown Ada before.

- Ada's relationship to the forest changes irreversibly. By the midpoint (Ch. 8), she should no longer be trying to escape. By Zone 4 (Ch. 8-10), she should be moving inward by choice (or by surrender, which amounts to the same thing). She cannot revert to "get me out of here" in the final chapters without undermining the arc.

### Identified Continuity Risks from Plot Design

1. **The forest object in Ada's pocket (Ch. 13):** This needs a planting moment earlier in the story. Without it, the discovery feels like a deus ex machina. Plant in Ch. 6 (near the shed snakeskin) or Ch. 9 (during the boar encounter, when Ada is on the ground) -- a moment where her hand closes around something small without her conscious awareness.

2. **Ada's backpack through Zone 4:** During the intense encounters of Ch. 8-11, Ada is lying on the ground, sitting against trees, dissolving. Is she still wearing the backpack? She likely sets it down when she sits against the cedar in Ch. 8. It should be noted. She either picks it up again when she rises in Ch. 11, or it is nearby and she retrieves it. The backpack must be present in Ch. 13 because it contains the car keys.

3. **The rain jacket:** If Ada puts it on during rain (Ch. 3-4), does she take it off? In Zone 4 (warm, humid), she might push it off or open it. Track this garment. It should be on her in some state (open, muddied, pushed to her waist) through the remainder.

4. **Water consumption:** Half a water bottle is not much for 14 hours. Note the physical effects of dehydration: dry mouth, headache, lightheadedness. These compound the exhaustion. The dawn stream drink in Ch. 12 should feel like significant relief.

5. **Moon position vs. canopy cover:** The moon is only visible through canopy gaps. In Zone 2 (nearly closed canopy), moonlight is rare. In Zone 3 (clearings), moonlight floods in. In Zone 4 (ancient cedars, canopy invisible above), moonlight is absent -- and the moon may have set by this point. The stag encounter (Ch. 5) specifically requires moonlight in a clearing. Verify the moon is still up at ~9:00 PM (it should be -- a waxing gibbous in late autumn would still be high at that hour).

6. **The owl bridging Ch. 7-8:** The owl leads Ada from Zone 3 into Zone 4. This transition must be physically rendered -- the trees growing larger, the undergrowth thinning as root systems dominate, the bioluminescence intensifying. Ada follows the owl but the owl must eventually vanish, leaving Ada alone as she enters Zone 4.

7. **Encounter aftermath physical states:** After the boar encounter (Ch. 9), Ada is on the ground, having sobbed violently. She should be physically wrecked: hoarse, trembling, eyes swollen, nose running. These details should persist into Ch. 10, where she walks into the spider's web with all of this still on her body.

8. **Dawn timing vs. psilocybin descent:** The first hint of dawn light appears at the end of Ch. 11 (~5:00 AM, late autumn). By Ch. 12, pre-dawn gray is visible. By Ch. 13, gold light. Meanwhile, the psilocybin is declining from Ch. 11 onward. These two arcs -- rising natural light and declining psychedelic intensity -- should mirror each other. As the trip fades, the real world brightens. The forest becomes less numinous and more beautiful in an ordinary way. This parallel is the environmental signature of integration.
