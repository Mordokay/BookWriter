# Timeline

## Deep History

### ~200 Million Years Ago: The Divergence

An amphibian lineage in what is now the Amazon basin undergoes an unexplained evolutionary acceleration. Neural density increases exponentially over a geologically brief period. The cause is not recorded in the earliest archives -- or if it was recorded, those records have not survived. Theories within the Threnn range from symbiotic interaction with voss to cosmic radiation events to simple evolutionary luck. Krrik suspects a feedback loop between voss proximity and neural development. Vesh considers the question uninteresting. "You do not ask the river why it runs downhill," he says. Krrik finds this response infuriating.

The result: a small amphibian species develops language, tool use, and social organization. They call themselves the Threnn.

### ~180-150 Million Years Ago: Discovery of Voss

Early Threnn settlements discover the living substrate in deep root systems. Initially used as a light source and heat source. Its energy-generating properties are understood incrementally over millions of years. There is no single "discovery" moment -- voss integration into Threnn life is gradual, experimental, and punctuated by accidents, including several settlements lost to uncontrolled voss growth before the growth cycles are understood.

### ~150 Million Years Ago: The First Pruning

The first documented cosmic radiation peak triggers accelerated voss growth. The early Threnn lack quantum displacement technology. They manage the growth through physical cutting, controlled burning, and chemical suppression -- brute methods with high casualties. The First Pruning kills an estimated 30% of the existing Threnn population. It is remembered in the archive as **The Wet Burning.** It is the event that transforms the Threnn from a loose collection of settlements into a coordinated civilization. The need to survive the cycle creates the political, technological, and spiritual infrastructure that persists to the present.

### ~100-80 Million Years Ago: Development of Organic Computation

Threnn biophysicists develop the first cultivated neural networks. Early arrays are crude -- living tissue cultures that can store and process simple information. Over tens of millions of years, the technology matures into the organic computation systems that now underpin Threnn civilization. The dinosaurs go extinct during this period (roughly 66 million years ago). The Threnn observe the asteroid impact from the Amazon basin. They survive through subterranean shelters and voss energy reserves. The archive describes the event with the clinical detachment of a species that has already survived worse: "A large object fell. Many surface animals died. The root systems held."

### ~60-40 Million Years Ago: Quantum Displacement

The development of quantum displacement technology transforms Threnn civilization. Travel between settlements becomes instantaneous. The global network connects. The Marsh Federations emerge as a coordination structure for inter-settlement governance. Cultural exchange accelerates. The Siberians and Amazon frogs begin their ongoing argument about cultural superiority, now approximately 50 million years old with no resolution in sight.

### ~30-20 Million Years Ago: Interstellar Exploration

The Siberians pioneer interstellar quantum displacement. Early missions are dangerous and energy-intensive. Several are lost. The survivors report finding rocks, gas, ice, and microbial life. No civilizations. No intelligence. A great deal of emptiness.

The Amazon frogs consider the program a distraction. The Siberians consider the Amazon frogs' focus on consciousness-merging equally pointless. Both continue their respective pursuits with mutual condescension.

Over the following millions of years, several dozen star systems are visited. The results remain consistent: the universe is vast, mostly empty, and contains nothing as interesting as the Threnn hoped. The exploration program continues at a reduced pace, driven more by institutional momentum than genuine enthusiasm.

### ~20-10 Million Years Ago: The Consciousness-Merging Mission Formalizes

The Wet Way has existed in some form since early Threnn history, but the consciousness-merging rituals crystallize into their current form during this period. The connection between synchronized consciousness and Pruning operations drives the development -- frogs discover that merged-state neural coordination dramatically improves displacement precision. The spiritual and practical functions intertwine.

Debate about whether the rituals are primarily spiritual or primarily technical begins during this period. It has not been resolved. The archive contains approximately 400,000 years of arguments on the subject. Pem can cite both sides from memory. He declines to state a preference.

### ~5-3 Million Years Ago: Early Hominids

The Threnn observe the emergence of early human ancestors in Africa. Initial assessment: "Promising bipedal omnivores. Unusual vocal range. Destructive foraging habits. Likely transient." The African Rift settlement monitors human development most closely. Early hominids occasionally wander near Threnn settlements. The frogs relocate. The hominids notice nothing.

## Recent History (Thousands of Years)

### ~300,000 Years Ago: Homo Sapiens

The Threnn upgrade their assessment. Humans are not transient. They are spreading, organizing, and developing technology at a rate the frogs find startling and slightly alarming. For the first time, the Threnn implement formal secrecy protocols -- systematic monitoring, active concealment, settlement positioning calculated to avoid human detection.

The frogs debate whether to make contact. The debate lasts roughly 50,000 years. Consensus: no. Contact would produce obligation, entanglement, and eventually conflict. The frogs have watched enough species interact to know how it goes.

### ~10,000 Years Ago: Agriculture and Human Civilization

Humans settle, build cities, develop writing. The Threnn monitor from a distance. They find human civilization fascinating and troubling -- impressive in its ambition, alarming in its ecological impact, oddly fixated on permanence for a species with such short institutional memory.

The Amazon settlement expands its monitoring capacity. Linguists begin studying human languages systematically. The frogs develop fluency in Sumerian, Egyptian, Sanskrit, and later Greek, Latin, Mandarin, Arabic, and dozens of others. They find each language charming in its limitations.

### ~2,000-500 Years Ago: Human Expansion

European exploration and colonization of the Americas directly threatens Threnn concealment for the first time. Several close calls. The Amazon settlement contracts, relocates deeper into inaccessible forest. Quantum displacement allows rapid evacuation of exposed areas. No Threnn are discovered during this period, though two monitoring stations are inadvertently destroyed by logging operations.

The frogs observe human colonialism with the detached horror of a species that has watched many similar patterns across geological time. "They do what expanding populations do," Noll would later summarize. "They do it with unusual enthusiasm."

### Incidents of Human Discovery

The archive records approximately twelve incidents of human discovery across all settlements over the past 10,000 years. Details are sparse and handled with discretion.

**Notable incidents:**

- **Approximately 4,000 years ago (Central Africa):** A human herbalist stumbles into the Rift Pool settlement while collecting plants. Lives among the Threnn for approximately 11 human years. Her biological imprint survives in the African archive. She is remembered as "the tall one who sang poorly but with conviction."

- **Approximately 800 years ago (Amazon):** A Tupi trader, separated from his party during a river journey, discovers the outer edge of Vreth-in-the-Root. Absorbed into the community. Lives among the Threnn for approximately 22 human years. Learns Threnn-song to a functional degree. His observations are preserved in the archive and considered valuable for their outsider perspective on Threnn daily life. He reportedly found the food acceptable and the sleeping arrangements unbearable.

- **Approximately 200 years ago (Northern Europe):** A naturalist investigating peat bogs in Scandinavia discovers a dormant Bog Network entrance. The frogs relocate the entire network within hours via quantum displacement. The naturalist publishes a paper about unusual geological formations that is ignored by his contemporaries.

In each case of extended contact, the humans were treated with courtesy, given roles and companionship, and never permitted to leave. The frogs consider this necessary and regrettable. The distinction between "honored guest" and "permanent captive" is one the Threnn find less troubling than humans would. Boundaries, after all, are permeable.

### Evolution of the Consciousness-Merging Mission

Over recent millennia, the mission has shifted from a primarily functional practice (Pruning coordination) to a central spiritual aspiration. The rituals have become more elaborate, more frequent, and more culturally significant. Participation, while technically voluntary, carries increasing social expectation.

Dissent has grown in parallel. Each generation produces critics who question the mission's spiritual claims, its political uses, or both. The current generation's most prominent critic is Talla-of-the-Third-Pool. She is not the first. She is unlikely to be the last. The archive preserves the arguments of her predecessors. They sound remarkably similar.

## Present Day: The Story's Timeframe

### Six Months Before the Story Opens

**The biologists' expedition:** Dr. Martin Quill's research team -- five evolutionary biologists studying primate behavior -- receives funding for a six-month field study in a remote section of the Amazon basin. The location is chosen for its high primate density and minimal human disturbance. It is, by coincidence, within fifteen kilometers of Vreth-in-the-Root's outer monitoring perimeter.

**Signs of the coming Pruning:** Threnn surveyors detect accelerated voss growth patterns consistent with a radiation peak. Krrik begins preliminary analysis. The growth rate is higher than standard models predict. He reports this to Noll's office. The report is noted and filed.

### Three Months Before the Story Opens

**The biologists establish camp.** They are deep enough in the forest to be productive and remote enough to be miserable. Equipment malfunctions, insect problems, and supply difficulties are routine. Hart complains. Reed is fascinated by everything. Marsh documents. Vale argues with the logistics coordinator by satellite phone. Quill stares into the canopy and thinks about consciousness.

**Pruning preparations begin.** Noll calls for increased breathing circle attendance and initiates Pruning staging protocols. Displacement operators begin training exercises. Talla publicly questions the acceleration of consciousness-merging requirements, arguing that the severity of the Pruning is being exaggerated to justify increased social control. She is heard, valued, and gently set aside.

**Krrik's concern deepens.** Updated voss growth data shows the substrate expanding into areas previously considered stable. Growth patterns are anomalous -- denser, faster, and more structurally complex than historical records suggest. Krrik requests additional organic computation resources for modeling. His request is approved after the standard bureaucratic delay, which in Threnn society means three committee discussions and two pool-soakings.

### Two Weeks Before the Story Opens

**Monitoring stations detect the biologists.** Outer perimeter sensors register human activity closer than expected. The biologists' primate study has led them into areas the Threnn normally keep clear. Assessment: five humans, scientific equipment, no military capability, no satellite communication capable of real-time data transfer. Standard protocol: observation and avoidance.

**Talla's dissidence becomes more organized.** She begins holding informal meetings in the Third Pool district, attracting frogs who share her skepticism about the consciousness-merging mandate. Noll is aware. He does not intervene. He trusts the system's absorptive capacity.

**Voss growth exceeds projections.** Krrik's models are increasingly inaccurate. The growth is not following established patterns. He privately tells Noll this is the most severe Pruning cycle in the archive's accessible records. Noll privately agrees. Publicly, both maintain measured calm.

### Days Before the Story Opens

**One of the biologists (Reed) documents something unusual.** While cataloguing root systems near a primate study site, Reed notes anomalous root density patterns and soil chemistry readings. He files the observation in his field notes as a curiosity. He does not know what he is looking at. He is looking at voss growth visible from the surface for the first time in local Threnn memory.

**The monitoring team reports a containment risk.** The biologists are now operating within areas where Pruning staging activity will be visible or detectable within days. Standard avoidance may not be sufficient. A decision must be made: redirect the humans (risky, potentially alerting them further) or allow potential contact.

**Noll convenes a quiet discussion in the Pools.** Not an official assembly. Just a few coordinators, soaking in warm water, discussing what to do about five primatologists who are about to wander into a civilization they are not supposed to know exists.

### Chapter 0: Current State at Story Opening

**The biologists** are camped in deep Amazon forest, conducting primate research that has gone well enough to keep them extending their survey range. They are experienced field scientists, physically comfortable with discomfort, intellectually curious, and completely unaware that they are being watched by a civilization older than flowering plants.

**Vreth-in-the-Root** is entering Pruning mobilization. The settlement is tense but functional. Breathing circles are increasing in frequency. Displacement operators are running coordination exercises. Krrik is in his laboratory most hours. Noll is calm. Talla is angry. Oona is worried about whether Pruning mobilization will disrupt the fermentation cellars' temperature regulation. Vesh is leading a breathing circle and speaking about the impermanence of crisis. Pem is reviewing archive records of severe historical Prunings, looking for precedent and finding the available records thinner than expected.

**The voss** is growing. Faster than it should. In places it should not be. Something about this cycle is different, and neither the archive nor the models can explain why.

**The frogs know the humans are there.** The humans do not know the frogs exist.

The door is about to open.

## Story Events Timeline

The following maps the key plot events in chronological order during the story's timeframe (approximately 5 weeks of story time).

### Week 1: Discovery

**Day 1-2 (Ch. 1)**
Reed documents anomalous root density and soil chemistry near a primate study site. Marsh finds a warm, faintly luminous stone embedded in a root system. The biologists note a faint harmonic vibration from underground but cannot explain it.

**Day 3 (Ch. 2)**
The biologists push deeper into the forest following Reed's anomalous readings. The Threnn monitoring team reports to Noll that the humans are within the outer perimeter. Noll convenes a quiet discussion in the Pools about how to handle the situation. The biologists hear a sustained harmonic vibration clearly for the first time.

**Day 4 (Ch. 3)**
The biologists find the entrance to Vreth-in-the-Root by following root anomalies to a natural fissure. They encounter bioluminescent pathways. Noll and a delegation make first contact. The biologists enter the settlement.

**Day 5-6 (Ch. 4)**
Noll gives the biologists a curated tour of Vreth-in-the-Root: the Speaking Chambers, the Pools, the residential warrens, the organic computation arrays, the moss trenches. Noll reveals the Threnn have been aware of humanity for approximately 300,000 years.

**Day 7 (Ch. 5)**
The biologists are introduced to individual frogs and the natural pairings form: Vale-Noll, Marsh-Oona, Reed-Krrik, Quill-Vesh, Hart-Talla. Oona casually mentions the Pruning for the first time.

---

### Week 2: Immersion

**Day 8-9 (Ch. 6)**
Vale interrogates Noll about frog governance during a community planning session in the Speaking Chambers. A Siberian delegation begins arriving for Pruning coordination. Vale identifies the gap between the frogs' egalitarian claims and their actual power structure.

**Day 10 (Ch. 7)**
Marsh spends a full day with Oona, visiting the fermentation cellars, tadpole nurseries, and residential warrens. She observes the quiet evacuation of families from areas near the Pruning staging grounds.

**Day 11-12 (Ch. 8)**
Reed enters Krrik's laboratory. Krrik shows him the organic computation arrays, quantum displacement apparatus, and voss substrate. Krrik reveals the voss growth models are failing -- growth is anomalous. Reed offers his team's surface data on root density changes. Krrik cautiously accepts.

**Day 13 (Ch. 9)**
Quill's first deep conversation with Vesh about the Wet Way, consciousness, death, and the mission. Vesh mentions, almost in passing, that the Pruning's merging rituals kill some participants.

**Day 14 (Ch. 10)**
Talla takes Hart through the parts of frog civilization the curated tour omitted: the outer settlement where voss growth encroaches, the breathing circle attendance records, the frogs displaced by Pruning staging.

---

### Week 3: Deepening

**Day 15-16 (Ch. 11)**
Quill visits Archivist Pem. Pem reveals fragments about previous humans absorbed into frog society -- the African herbalist, the Tupi trader. Pem deflects questions about their detailed fates. The curated nature of the archive becomes clear.

**Day 17-18 (Ch. 12)**
The full Siberian delegation arrives. Cultural clash between Amazon and Siberian frogs over the role of consciousness-merging in Pruning operations. Siberian data combined with Reed's surface observations confirms the Pruning is the most severe in centuries. Krrik and the Siberian chief engineer begin collaborative (argumentative) work.

**Day 19-20 (Ch. 13)**
Noll announces increased breathing circle requirements. Vale attends a circle at Noll's invitation and experiences a brief consciousness-synchronization. Talla begins holding larger informal meetings in the Third Pool district.

**Day 21 (Ch. 14)**
Krrik's revised models project growth exceeding anything in the accessible archive. Voss grows through a residential section wall. Displacement operators run increasingly complex coordination exercises. Krrik privately tells Reed the biological modulators may not be sufficient. Reed's surface observation data becomes a critical input to Krrik's modulation research.

---

### Week 4: Crisis

**Day 22-23 (Ch. 15)**
Quill observes a full consciousness-merging ritual performed by experienced operators preparing for the Pruning. One operator is helped away unresponsive during the session. Quill is profoundly affected by the proximity of the ritual.

**Day 24 (Ch. 16)**
Marsh notices increased mourning rituals for elderly frogs dying under Pruning preparation stress. She visits Pem to ask about historical Pruning mortality and discovers the archive manages information politically -- it records what the civilization needs to forget, but makes it difficult to access.

**Day 25-26 (Ch. 17)**
Talla publicly refuses to attend breathing circles, the most overtly political act in the settlement's recent memory. Her informal meetings call for transparency about Pruning mortality data and examination of alternative methods. Noll visits the biologists' quarters and requests they speak publicly to help unify the community. All refuse or deflect.

**Day 27-28 (Ch. 18)**
The Pruning operations begin. Hundreds of synchronized operators coordinate quantum displacement of voss across hemispheres. The first wave is initially sufficient, then the voss pushes back in the eastern sectors. Three operators are pulled from the pools unconscious; one does not recover. Krrik adjusts the biological modulators using patterns informed by Reed's surface data. The second displacement wave holds. Barely. Operations continue over multiple days.

---

### Week 5: The Narrowing

**Day 29-31 (Ch. 19)**
The Pruning's active phase winds down. The voss growth is managed but not eliminated. Operators recover slowly. Some who lost coherence during synchronization have not returned to full individual consciousness. Vesh tends to the damaged operators. One young operator does not respond. Quill witnesses the cost.

**Day 32 (Ch. 20)**
As the settlement begins to recover, Noll invites the biologists to the Pools. He delivers the ultimatum: remain as honored witnesses or undergo dignified cessation. Pem cites the precedent of previous humans. The biologists absorb the news.

**Day 33-34 (Ch. 21)**
Each biologist processes the ultimatum through their frog counterpart. Vale confronts Noll. Marsh grieves with Oona. Reed grapples with his own conflicted fascination in conversation with Krrik. Hart sits with Talla at the settlement's edge. Quill asks Vesh what full consciousness-merging would actually feel like.

**Day 35 and beyond (Ch. 22)**
Weeks pass (extending the story to approximately six weeks total). The biologists are absorbed into frog life: Vale observes governance, Marsh studies domestic life alongside Oona, Reed works in Krrik's laboratory, Hart catalogues ecological data with Talla, and Quill attends breathing circles. The final scene: Quill in a warm pool, eyes closed, the chord almost resolving. The civilization continues. It is enough.
